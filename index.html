🔬 What is forward testing?
A regular backtest lets the model study the whole period and then answer quiz questions. Walk-forward testing is the honest version: the model only studies past days, then trades tomorrow cold, like real life.

🧭 How I would use this
  1. Use radar marks as a watchlist, not automatic trades.
  2. Use LONG/SHORT trigger prints as the actionable entry.
  3. Use elite badge/score confirmation as size-up information.
  4. Start small: $1k cap → 1 MNQ tight; $2k cap → 1–2 MNQ; $3k cap → 2–3 MNQ.
  5. Log every session. Shadow-live CSV is the final arbiter before adding size.
📖 How the model works

Stage 1 — Radar zone: every 1-minute bar is scored using price extension, order flow pressure, ES futures, and VIX context. A dim mark appears when something looks like a reversal zone.

Stage 2 — Trigger: inside a zone, it waits for price to prove the turn with FVG or 5-minute IFVG structure. First one to fire prints LONG or SHORT.

Stage 3 — Elite badge: strongest score-confirmed signals get an elite tag.

📐 How the backtest was calculated

Entries use next-bar market open. Zone thresholds use expanding intraday data only. Exit banks half at +100, moves runner to break-even, caps runner at +600, and flattens at 4pm. Commissions/slippage are not included.

⚠️ Limitations and confidence

Sample warning: 15 days and 44 labels is promising but small. Exact dollar numbers are estimates. Confidence improves after 25+ forward-tested sessions and 60+ labeled trades.

📚 What the metrics mean

Profit factor: gross wins divided by gross losses. Drawdown: worst peak-to-valley loss. Typical heat: how far price moves against entry before resolving. Max move available: the biggest favorable move after signal.
