## Core Model

The trader's strongest reversal idea is not "fade every extreme." It is:

1. Price reaches a meaningful edge: high/low of day, swing high/low, prior value/POC/VWAP/support/resistance, or a fresh breakout/breakdown area.
2. Aggressive traders push into that edge.
3. The footprint shows they are losing effectiveness: exhaustion, absorption, delta weakening/divergence, trapped traders, failed imbalances, or no continuation.
4. Entry is near the failed edge, with a tight structural stop beyond the edge.
5. The move should work quickly. If it stalls, scratches, small losses, or small profits are preferred.

The A+ version is a confluence trade: edge + aggressive participation + failure/absorption + clean stop + immediate response.

## Setup 1: Exhaustion After Absorption Reversal

**Plain-English description:** The flagship setup. Price pushes into an important level after prior absorption or aggressive activity, then the footprint prints very light volume at the extreme. Buyers or sellers have run out of ammunition.

**Order-flow story:** Aggressive traders drove the move. At the edge, passive liquidity absorbed them or their pressure faded. The final extreme trades only tiny volume, showing no fresh participation left to continue.

**Trader psychology:** Late longs buy the high or late shorts sell the low, but there is nobody left behind them. Stronger hands are passive on the other side. The late traders are exposed.

**Bullish version:** Market sells into a swing low, low of day, prior support, value area, or retest zone. Selling delta was strong earlier but weakens into the low. The low prints tiny volume/exhaustion. Long is favored once the signal bar closes and next bar trades upward.

**Bearish version:** Market rallies into a swing high, high of day, prior resistance, value area, or retest zone. Buying delta was strong earlier but weakens or flips negative into the high. The high prints tiny volume/exhaustion. Short is favored once the signal bar closes and next bar trades downward.

**Required conditions:**
- Directly supported: exhaustion print at swing high/swing low, high of day, or low of day.
- Directly supported: low volume at the edge of the bar.
- Directly supported: swing filter concept, with 25 bars and exhaustion volume limit 10 discussed for the 2026 ES model.
- Directly supported: context matters; do not trade every exhaustion print.
- Interpretation: stronger if preceded by absorption, trapped traders, delta weakening, failed continuation, or stacked imbalance failure.

**Confirming evidence:**
- Signal bar closes with exhaustion still present.
- Next bar opens/trades in the reversal direction.
- Delta pressure weakens into the extreme: selling gets weaker into lows or buying gets weaker into highs.
- The reversal starts quickly; best examples move within a few bars.

**Invalidation evidence:**
- Price trades beyond the exhaustion edge and accepts there.
- Next bars do not respond; market goes sideways too long.
- Aggressive continuation appears through the level.
- The exhaustion occurs in bad timing, such as directly ahead of cash open, or inside a noisy non-edge area.

**Entry logic:** Enter after the signal bar closes and the next bar starts trading in the reversal direction. The 2026 source check explicitly supports not waiting for extra confirmation beyond bar close plus next-bar directional trade for pure exhaustion.

**Stop logic:** Stop just beyond the signal high for shorts or just beyond the signal low for longs. The trader repeatedly frames the appeal as tight stop near the edge.

**What should happen quickly after entry:** Price should reject the edge quickly. If it drifts sideways for 10 minutes, scratch, take a small profit, or accept a small loss. The best moves happen right away.

**A+ versus mediocre/bad:**
- A+: at a major level, after absorption or delta weakening, tiny edge volume, clean signal bar, tight stop, immediate response.
- Mediocre: exhaustion in the middle of nowhere, large signal bar, no clear level, weak/no directional response.
- Bad: taking every exhaustion print mechanically.

## Setup 2: Absorption Reversal

**Plain-English description:** Aggressive buying or selling hits the market, but price does not continue. Passive buyers or sellers absorb the attack, and price reverses.

**Order-flow story:** Aggressive sellers sell the bid into passive buyers, or aggressive buyers lift offers into passive sellers. The aggressive side shows activity but fails to move price enough.

**Trader psychology:** Aggressive traders believe they are breaking the market. Passive liquidity is large enough or persistent enough to stop them. When the aggressive flow dries up, trapped participants exit.

**Bullish version:** Market pushes down with aggressive selling, often negative delta, but price holds or closes green. Passive buyers absorb selling at bid/support. Long when price starts lifting away from absorbed low.

**Bearish version:** Market pushes up with aggressive buying, often positive delta, but price stalls or reverses. Passive sellers absorb buying at offer/resistance. Short when price starts rejecting the absorbed high.

**Required conditions:**
- Directly supported: passive buyers/sellers are the important side.
- Directly supported: negative delta on a green candle can be absorption by passive buyers.
- Directly supported: strong positive delta at resistance can be bearish if absorbed and no continuation follows.
- Interpretation: use large volume at price, POC/value area, repeated tests, or imbalance behavior to locate absorption.

**Confirming evidence:**
- Aggressive flow fails to create continuation.
- Candle body rejects the direction of aggression.
- Price revisits the area and still cannot break through.
- Delta contradicts price: green candle with negative delta, red candle with positive delta, or aggressive delta with no progress.

**Invalidation evidence:**
- Price accepts beyond absorbed zone.
- Aggressive flow continues and expands after the supposed absorption.
- The passive area gets consumed and follow-through develops.

**Entry logic:** Enter after absorption is visible and price begins moving away from the absorbed level. More conservative entry waits for a retest/failure of the same zone.

**Stop logic:** Stop behind the absorbed level or signal extreme. If passive buyers absorbed selling at a low, stop below that low. If passive sellers absorbed buying at a high, stop above that high.

**What should happen quickly after entry:** The side that was absorbed should give up. Price should move away from the absorption zone, not keep churning there.

**A+ versus mediocre/bad:**
- A+: clear passive defense, aggressive delta trapped, meaningful level, failure to continue, clean rotation away.
- Mediocre: absorption inferred from one ambiguous candle.
- Bad: calling every large-volume candle absorption.

## Setup 3: Failed Breakout / Failed Breakdown Reversal

**Plain-English description:** Price breaks a visible high/low or value boundary, but order flow does not confirm continuation. The breakout traders are forced to unwind.

**Order-flow story:** Breakout participation appears, often through stops or market orders, but it cannot hold outside the level. The breakout area becomes a rejection zone.

**Trader psychology:** Breakout traders enter late. When price fails to continue, they become trapped and fuel the reversal.

**Bullish version:** Price breaks below a low/support/value area, cannot continue lower, shows exhaustion/absorption/positive delta response, then reclaims the level.

**Bearish version:** Price breaks above a high/resistance/value area, cannot continue higher, shows exhaustion/absorption/negative delta response, then loses the level.

**Required conditions:**
- Directly supported: trader emphasizes context around highs/lows, value areas, and breakout/non-breakout day types.
- Directly supported: no continuation after strong positive delta at highs is bearish.
- Interpretation: reclaim/failure of breakout level is the operational confirmation.

**Confirming evidence:**
- Breakout bar does not hold.
- Strong delta does not produce more price progress.
- Next bar reverses back inside the prior range or value.
- Trapped trader or exhaustion evidence appears at the extension.

**Invalidation evidence:**
- Price accepts outside the breakout area.
- Delta and imbalances continue in breakout direction.
- Retest holds and continues.

**Entry logic:** Enter on failure/reclaim rather than at the first breakout tick. Short failed breakouts after return below the breakout level; long failed breakdowns after return above breakdown level.

**Stop logic:** Stop beyond the failed extension high/low.

**What should happen quickly after entry:** Failed breakouts should snap back. Slow grinding action after re-entry into range is lower quality.

**A+ versus mediocre/bad:**
- A+: obvious level, emotional break, no continuation, trapped flow, quick reclaim.
- Mediocre: minor local level only.
- Bad: fading a real trend-day breakout.

## Setup 4: Stop-Run / Liquidity-Sweep Reversal

**Plain-English description:** Price sweeps known liquidity beyond a high/low, triggers stops, then fails to continue.

**Order-flow story:** Market orders sweep through resting liquidity. Volume spikes at or beyond the level, but after the sweep the market cannot extend.

**Trader psychology:** Stops fire. Some participants mistake stop volume for true initiative flow. If no follow-through appears, the sweep exhausts itself.

**Bullish version:** Sweep below low triggers sell stops; sellers fail to continue; price reclaims low.

**Bearish version:** Sweep above high triggers buy stops; buyers fail to continue; price loses high.

**Required conditions:**
- Directly supported: suspicious/large volume near new lows can be stops.
- Directly supported: trader differentiates stop orders from true trapped traders.
- Interpretation: require reclaim and order-flow failure to separate sweep reversal from continuation.

**Confirming evidence:**
- One-bar or brief extension beyond obvious level.
- Heavy volume or aggressive delta at the sweep.
- No continuation after the sweep.
- Reclaim of the swept level.

**Invalidation evidence:**
- Level holds after sweep and becomes support/resistance in the direction of the break.
- Fresh imbalances stack in breakout direction after the sweep.

**Entry logic:** Enter after the sweep fails and price returns through the level.

**Stop logic:** Stop just beyond sweep extreme.

**What should happen quickly after entry:** Price should move away from the sweep zone as trapped stop participants are absorbed.

**A+ versus mediocre/bad:**
- A+: sweep of a clean level, large emotional volume, immediate reclaim, absorption/exhaustion.
- Mediocre: vague level or delayed reclaim.
- Bad: trying to fade a real liquidation trend.

## Setup 5: Trapped Trader Reversal

**Plain-English description:** Traders enter aggressively at a poor location, usually because they fear missing the move or chase a breakout. Their orders are absorbed; then they puke.

**Order-flow story:** The footprint shows aggressive buying/selling but not enough price progress. The trapped side is identified by failure to continue and by absorption against them.

**Trader psychology:** FOMO traders enter late. If their entry cannot move price, their exit becomes reversal fuel.

**Bullish version:** Aggressive sellers hit lows, fail to push lower, get absorbed by passive buyers, then cover.

**Bearish version:** Aggressive buyers lift highs, fail to push higher, get absorbed by passive sellers, then liquidate.

**Required conditions:**
- Directly supported: trapped traders can be FOMO participants; not every stop order is a trap.
- Directly supported: trapped traders do not always create a huge reversal; the move may be shorter than expected.
- Directly supported: follow-through matters.

**Confirming evidence:**
- Big aggressive flow at bad location.
- Absorption or lack of continuation.
- Subsequent reversal through the trapped entry area.

**Invalidation evidence:**
- The aggressive side continues and wins.
- Price holds above/below the trap area and builds acceptance.

**Entry logic:** Enter after evidence that the trapped side is failing, not simply because large volume printed.

**Stop logic:** Stop beyond the trapped zone/extreme.

**What should happen quickly after entry:** A forced unwind should show up quickly. If not, the trap is weak or not real.

**A+ versus mediocre/bad:**
- A+: clear FOMO/late entry into passive liquidity, no continuation, clean unwind path.
- Mediocre: uncertain whether flow is a stop-out, hedge, or true new trapped position.
- Bad: assuming all large delta is trapped.

## Setup 6: Delta-Divergence Reversal

**Plain-English description:** Price and delta stop confirming each other. Price makes a new extreme while delta weakens, flips, or fails to support the move.

**Order-flow story:** Aggressive pressure is no longer producing proportional price progress. Delta can be strong but ineffective, or delta can weaken while price still tests extremes.

**Trader psychology:** Traders focused only on candle direction miss that the aggressive side is deteriorating. The smart read is effort versus result.

**Bullish version:** Price makes lower low or retests low, but selling delta is less negative, flips positive, or closes green with negative delta absorption.

**Bearish version:** Price makes higher high or retests high, but buying delta is less positive, flips negative, or closes red despite positive delta.

**Required conditions:**
- Directly supported: cumulative delta divergence, delta reversal, delta weakness, min/max delta readings.
- Directly supported: strong positive delta can be bearish when absorbed at resistance.
- Interpretation: use price extreme versus delta extreme as a measurable divergence.

**Confirming evidence:**
- Delta weakening into a level.
- Price fails to continue despite strong aggressive delta.
- Reversal candle follows with opposite delta or opposite close.

**Invalidation evidence:**
- Delta re-accelerates in trend direction and price extends.
- Divergence appears away from any meaningful level.

**Entry logic:** Enter on reversal confirmation after divergence at an edge, not on divergence alone.

**Stop logic:** Stop beyond divergent price extreme.

**What should happen quickly after entry:** Delta failure should translate into price rotation. If price keeps accepting at the extreme, no trade.

**A+ versus mediocre/bad:**
- A+: divergence at high/low/value level with absorption/exhaustion.
- Mediocre: divergence on noisy internal bars.
- Bad: countertrend entry on trend day without level failure.

## Setup 7: Stacked-Imbalance Failure Reversal

**Plain-English description:** Stacked imbalances imply initiative buying or selling, but the market fails to follow through. The failed imbalance becomes reversal evidence.

**Order-flow story:** Multiple price levels show one-sided aggression. In a healthy continuation, price should move. If it cannot, the aggression was absorbed or mistimed.

**Trader psychology:** Imbalance traders expect continuation. When follow-through fails, they are wrong fast.

**Bullish version:** Stacked sell imbalances appear into lows/support but fail to push lower; price reclaims above them.

**Bearish version:** Stacked buy imbalances appear into highs/resistance but fail to push higher; price falls back through them.

**Required conditions:**
- Directly supported: stacked imbalances must be taken in market context.
- Directly supported: buying a stacked buying imbalance can stop out without follow-through.
- Directly supported: inverse imbalances/absorption can pair with stacked imbalance concepts.

**Confirming evidence:**
- Stacked imbalance at edge.
- No immediate continuation.
- Opposite response through imbalance area.
- Absorption/exhaustion at same zone.

**Invalidation evidence:**
- Imbalance creates clean continuation.
- Retest holds in imbalance direction.

**Entry logic:** Do not fade the imbalance immediately. Wait for failure/no follow-through and reversal through the imbalance zone.

**Stop logic:** Stop beyond the imbalance extreme or signal bar.

**What should happen quickly after entry:** Failed imbalance should reverse promptly; otherwise it may be consolidation before continuation.

**A+ versus mediocre/bad:**
- A+: stacked imbalance into a meaningful level, absorbed, no follow-through, sharp reversal.
- Mediocre: imbalance in chop.
- Bad: fading imbalances on strong trend days without failure.

## Setup 8: Climactic Volume Reversal

**Plain-English description:** Large volume hits at an extreme, but the market does not continue. The volume is interpreted as climax, absorption, stop activity, or exhaustion depending on context.

**Order-flow story:** Heavy participation appears late in the move. If price cannot extend after that effort, the side creating the volume may be finished.

**Trader psychology:** Crowd activity spikes after the move is already extended. Late participants give stronger hands liquidity.

**Bullish version:** Heavy selling volume at lows, but price holds/reclaims.

**Bearish version:** Heavy buying volume at highs, but price stalls/rejects.

**Required conditions:**
- Directly supported: large volume must be interpreted in context, not blindly.
- Directly supported: big volume can be absorption, stops, or suspicious activity, and may not be a reversal by itself.
- Interpretation: require failure/absorption/exhaustion after climactic volume.

**Confirming evidence:**
- Volume spike at level.
- Delta aggression fails.
- Next bars reject the extreme.
- Passive liquidity or POC/value behavior supports the rejection.

**Invalidation evidence:**
- Volume spike initiates a real trend leg.
- Price accepts beyond the spike.

**Entry logic:** Wait for post-volume failure. Do not enter only because volume is high.

**Stop logic:** Stop beyond climactic extreme.

**What should happen quickly after entry:** The market should rotate away once the climax has finished.

**A+ versus mediocre/bad:**
- A+: volume climax plus no continuation plus level plus tight stop.
- Mediocre: just a big bar.
- Bad: fading news-driven trend volume.

## Setup 9: Retest-Confirmation Reversal

**Plain-English description:** Market tests a prior extreme or order-flow level, fails to break it, and confirms with a second bar or directional response.

**Order-flow story:** The first touch creates the level. The retest shows whether aggressive traders can take it. If they cannot, the level becomes a trade location.

**Trader psychology:** Traders who expect the retest to break are wrong when the level holds. The second failure is more convincing than the first signal.

**Bullish version:** Prior low/swing low/value area holds on retest; sellers weaken; long after confirmation.

**Bearish version:** Prior high/swing high/value area holds on retest; buyers weaken; short after confirmation.

**Required conditions:**
- Directly supported: the trader repeatedly anchors to swing highs/lows, high/low of day, value areas, and retests.
- Directly supported: bar-close confirmation is used for signals.
- Interpretation: second-chance entries after immediate stop-out can be valid if order flow is unchanged.

**Confirming evidence:**
- Retest fails at same zone.
- Confirmation bar closes and next bar trades away.
- Absorption/exhaustion/delta weakness appears on retest.

**Invalidation evidence:**
- Retest accepts beyond level.
- Second test breaks cleanly with continuation.

**Entry logic:** Enter after retest rejection, not on blind touch.

**Stop logic:** Stop beyond retest extreme.

**What should happen quickly after entry:** Retest rejection should create quick rotation away from the level.

**A+ versus mediocre/bad:**
- A+: clean level, second test, weaker delta, exhaustion/absorption, immediate rejection.
- Mediocre: too many tests grinding the level.
- Bad: retest against a strong trend with no order-flow failure.

## Setup 10: Failed-Continuation Reversal

**Plain-English description:** A move starts in one direction, but the next bars do not continue. The absence of expected follow-through is itself reversal evidence.

**Order-flow story:** Healthy initiative flow should keep producing price progress and consistent delta. When it stalls, the initiating side is weak.

**Trader psychology:** Traders expect continuation after a strong candle, breakout, imbalance, or delta surge. When it fails, their confidence collapses.

**Bullish version:** Strong sell bar or sell imbalances fail to continue lower; sellers weaken; price reverses up.

**Bearish version:** Strong buy bar or buy imbalances fail to continue higher; buyers weaken; price reverses down.

**Required conditions:**
- Directly supported: if expecting continuation, positive deltas should remain consistently strong in an up move.
- Directly supported: lack of continuation after strong positive delta at resistance is bearish.
- Directly supported: if exhaustion reversal is going to work, it should happen sooner rather than later.

**Confirming evidence:**
- Strong initial flow followed by weak/no progress.
- Delta decays or flips.
- Price returns through the initiating bar or level.

**Invalidation evidence:**
- Follow-through appears.
- Pullback is shallow and continuation resumes.

**Entry logic:** Enter only after failed continuation is visible, preferably at a level.

**Stop logic:** Stop beyond the failed continuation extreme.

**What should happen quickly after entry:** The market should unwind the failed continuation bar or zone quickly.

**A+ versus mediocre/bad:**
- A+: obvious expected continuation that fails immediately at a level.
- Mediocre: slow chop with no clear expectation.
- Bad: fighting a true trend leg.
