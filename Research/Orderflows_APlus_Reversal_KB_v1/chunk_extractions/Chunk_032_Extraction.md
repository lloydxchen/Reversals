## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Delta at a high**: market rallies with positive delta; at the turn, aggressive buying slows (delta decreases or turns negative), then selling takes over. "Coming up to your high and you got no more buying... selling is taking over." (v67, [04:32]) [REPEATED]
- **Negative delta on rising market = supportive/passive buying**: limit-order buyers absorb aggressive sellers; sellers "give the market a break" to let it breathe before selling again. Mike notes he did this himself at an institutional desk. (v62, [05:12]) [ONCE]
- **Liquidity being pulled on rally**: as price rises, offers are pulled by sellers who want higher prices, reducing liquidity and allowing price to float up with smaller delta. (v62, [06:56]) [ONCE]
- **Supportive buying dries up → reversal**: at a high, limit buyers absorb sellers; when supportive buying is exhausted, sellers overrun the bids and price reverses. "Supportive buying dries up... boom, more aggressive sellers coming in." (v62, [10:44]) [REPEATED]
- **Trapped buyers in a red bar**: buyers bought the breakout, bar closes red; they are now underwater and bailing. The selling imbalances represent their exit/capitulation. (v67, [07:02]) [ONCE]
- **Sellers covering into a bounce**: large negative-delta move is followed by covering; "all these people that are selling it got to start covering and rally back up again." (v62, [31:24]) [REPEATED]
- **Stacked imbalance at high = institutional supply**: "it takes real solid money to sell the out of the market like that... when I see negative delta of 10,000 I don't think it's a bunch of day traders." (v67, [23:56]) [ONCE]
- **Congestion/absorption before a big move**: small-delta bars clustering around a level = both sides testing; a sharp delta spike out of that zone = one side won. "Moves come out of areas of absorption, areas of congestion." (v66, [25:51]) [ONCE]
- **Rally into prior supply level → re-test**: price sells off from level A, bounces, rallies back to same level → Delta Scalper fires again at same level → confirms supply is still there. (v63, [09:19]) [ONCE]

## G. Delta / delta-divergence ideas

(v62, [17:29]) [REPEATED]
- **Cumulative delta on range charts** = even less useful; the bar count and time basis compound the lag. (v62, [22:41]) [ONCE]

- **Divergence alone is not enough**: "just cuz you got divergences doesn't necessarily mean it's going to work out." Ratio needed to confirm. (v67, [06:01]) [REPEATED]

- **Red bar with positive delta at a new high = partial bearish signal**: price action (red bar) confirms selling pressure even if delta alone is ambiguous. (v63, [08:46]) [ONCE]

- **Delta of 10,000 on a 10-range S&P bar**: "when I see negative delta of 10,000... no questions asked, sell it." — extremely large bar delta = institutional participation = high-confidence signal. (v67, [23:56]) [ONCE] NEEDS-OPERATIONALIZATION (no absolute threshold stated for other markets)

- **Positive delta at a swing high does not rule out a reversal**: "I want to see price action in the bar... if that bar can't even close lower than it opens, it's probably not going to be a very effective signal." — candle color as filter on delta. (v67, [02:12]) [ONCE]

## K. Confirmation — what SHOULD happen quickly after entry/trigger

- **Market should not immediately re-test signal extreme**: a rally back to within a tick of the signal high within 1–2 bars is stressful but not invalidating if stop is not touched; a re-close beyond the extreme = exit. (v63, [06:16]) [ONCE]
