## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Ratio with Divergence** (bearish/bullish): His declared single-best setup — "if there's only one setup you could take, take a divergence with a ratio period." Cited repeatedly across all five videos. (v51, [16:46]; v53, [02:32]; v54, [04:36]; v55, [04:13]) [REPEATED]
- **Delta Scalper signal**: Reads delta in-bar to alert when aggression is shifting; generates buy/sell arrows. Used as primary attention-directing tool across all sessions. (v51, [04:26]; v52, [02:13]) [REPEATED]
- **Three-in-a-Row (3IR)**: Three consecutive ratio bars in same direction, followed by a pullback between the two POCs before entry. Bullish: ratio/ratio/ratio → green bar + pullback → long. Bearish: ratio/ratio/ratio → red bar + pullback → short. (v51, [18:41]; v52, [02:47]) [REPEATED]
- **Stacked Imbalance** (bearish/bullish): Consecutive same-direction imbalances on top of each other at a high or low. "Very high percentage trade" when stacked selling imbalances off the high or stacked buying imbalances off the low. (v51, [19:35]; v52, [24:38]; v54, [07:52]) [REPEATED]
- **Multiple Imbalance bar** (not stacked, same bar): Three or more imbalances within a single bar across many price levels, even if not visually stacked — "still quite effective." (v51, [13:35]; v53, [03:23]; v54, [11:09]) [REPEATED]
- **Delta Surge**: Sudden spike in delta magnitude; used as a buy/sell attention signal, generally secondary to Delta Scalper. (v51, [04:26]; v52, [19:25]) [REPEATED]
- **Delta Scalper + Transition (dual signal)**: When both indicators fire simultaneously, treated as a higher-probability entry. (v52, [17:58]) [ONCE]
- **Trap Traders** (buy / sell): Counter-move trapped participants forced to cover. (v51, [09:18]; v52, [22:15]) [REPEATED]
- **Single Print**: Gap/unfilled area in the footprint indicating strong directional move; used as a target/reference level and as a trigger/confirmation. (v52, [06:39]; v53, [02:57]) [REPEATED]
- **Ratio Bounds Low** (stopping volume signal): Bullish ratio at an extreme low indicating stopping volume absorbed by buyers. (v51, [24:09]; v52, [05:47]; v54, [12:13]) [REPEATED]
- **Stopping volume**: High volume at an extreme with little net price progress; "stopping volume" at the high is bearish. (v51, [24:09]; v52, [07:37]) [REPEATED]
- **POC at extreme** (bearish/bullish): Point of control located at or near the top (bearish) or bottom (bullish) of a bar — "by itself I'd find this bearish." (v51, [20:04]; v52, [05:47]; v53, [03:23]) [REPEATED]
- **Failed Divergence (trend-day signal)**: Multiple divergences that fail in quick succession (every 5–10 minutes) = sign market is moving higher/lower, not reversing. (v51, [17:43]; v54, [14:00]) [REPEATED]
- **Price Rejector**: Proprietary indicator generating sell/buy at extremes; combined with other signals. (v51, [04:26]; v52, [08:06]) [REPEATED]
- **Volos U-Turn**: Proprietary indicator (black arrow on chart), fires on momentum exhaustion; used alongside Delta Scalper. (v52, [16:50]) [ONCE]
- **Naked Divergence** (no ratio): Delta divergence without a confirming ratio — noted as "worked" once but treated as lower quality. (v51, [09:18]) [ONCE]
- **O2X trade** (within-2-ticks-of-low): "You got a low with a ratio try and buy it within two ticks off the low." (v54, [23:04]) [ONCE]
- **Double top with decreasing volume**: Second test of a swing high trading less volume than first — "I like the back that trades less the second time around." Used as a bearish confirmation. (v52, [09:45]) [REPEATED]
- **Hammer candle with imbalance in wick**: Long-wick small-body bar with a buying imbalance embedded in the wick — "usually a nice little trade." (v53, [07:18]) [ONCE]
- **Stacked imbalance vs. counter stacked imbalance** (bearish pattern): Stack buying imbalance followed within a few bars by a stack selling imbalance = "very bearish because you get a stacked imbalance going the other direction." (v54, [15:28]) [ONCE]

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Stacked selling imbalance off high / buying off low**: Aggressive sellers/buyers dominate at an extreme. "Over half the bar is aggressive selling and the market moves lower — that's not brain surgery." (v54, [26:05]) [ONCE]
- **Double-top with less volume**: "The back [second test] trades less... you're hitting this important high where there was stopping volume earlier — what do you do? You react." Supply still present from first test; less urgency from buyers on second attempt. (v52, [07:37]) [REPEATED]

## R. Contradictions / nuances

- **Ratio threshold evolution**: He states "I used to use 25, widened out a few years ago to 30" in a June 2017 video. This directly confirms the digest note that early ratio was ~7 (product-launch era) and later became ≥30; it also pinpoints the ~2015 shift to 30. (v54, [12:13]) [REFINES known nuance]
- **"Ratios are not lines in the sand — they are guidelines"**: A ratio of 7.68 described as "very close to 7 — if you took it great, if you didn't okay." This partially softens the ≥30 hard threshold. (v54, [09:46]) [NUANCE]
- **Delta Scalper buy on a red bar = never take it**: Explicit rule contradicts taking any buy signal on a red candle regardless of other confluence. (v51, [12:38]; v54, [20:08]) [CONSISTENT rule, but worth noting how hard this is]
- **Stacked imbalance = immediate-term only EXCEPT on retests**: He says stacked buying imbalance on a retest of a high is "important" and led to a new high. This is a continuation context for stacked imbalances, distinct from the reversal context at extremes. (v51, [19:35]) [NUANCE vs. usual reversal framing]
- **Delta Scalper buy into existing up trend = "probably not going to be very effective"**: This explicitly downgrades the signal quality in trend-continuation mode, whereas in reversals (buy off a low after a down move) it is preferred. (v52, [14:42]) [NUANCE]
- **Doji bars are neutral / skip**: He states this as a strong preference rule, but acknowledges "it is giving you a buy here... it's got a big strong positive Delta as well, so I'm not sure if I'd be interested or not." (v52, [06:14]) [MINOR INTERNAL CONTRADICTION]
- **Time stop is variable (not a fixed 10 minutes)**: The digest says "~10-min time stop." This chunk shows examples where he waits nearly an hour (5 years trade at 8:30 → 10:00) before calling it slow, and in another says "an hour later, throw in the towel." The rule appears to be: exit when order flow gives negative signs OR when it has been considerably longer with no progress — duration depends on the speed of bar formation. (v55, [07:39]; v54, [13:03]) [REFINES/NUANCES digest threshold]
- **He explicitly states "not a red-light/green-light system"**: (v53, [02:08]) [CONSISTENT with digest spine]
- **Signals near the opening price = avoid**: "Always hesitant trading around the opening price when we come back to it later in the day." This is a context filter not just for the open bar, but for any later retest of the opening price. (v52, [21:18]) [NUANCE — broadens the "avoid open" filter]
