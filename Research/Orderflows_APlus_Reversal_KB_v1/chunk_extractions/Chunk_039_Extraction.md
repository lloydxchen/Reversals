## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Ratio + Divergence** (his "go-to" and most frequently cited): negative delta on a new high (or positive delta on a new low) paired with a bearish (or bullish) ratio in the same bar. Bearish version in stocks/bonds/grains/FX. [REPEATED]

- **Delta Scalper signal** (tool-generated, confirms or initiates entry; referenced across all 6 videos). [REPEATED]
- **Delta Surge** — a single bar with an abnormally large delta (positive or negative), signaling aggressive directional order flow. He distinguishes it from the Delta Scalper as a separate indicator. (v108, [07:11]; v110, [02:20]; v112, [11:00]) [REPEATED]
- **Stopping volume** — heavy bid-side volume while price is still negative delta; price holds and then reverses. (v108, [03:14]; v113, [02:24]) [REPEATED]
- **Stacked / Multiple imbalances** (same-direction cluster = continuation; opposite at extreme = reversal). Noted in v108, v110, v111, v113. [REPEATED]
- **Supportive buying / bid-side volume absorption** — large volume on the bid-side of the footprint at a potential low; price holds despite negative delta. (v108, [03:14]; v109, [12:25]; v110, [06:31]; v113, [18:41]) [REPEATED]
- **Point of Control (POC) at extreme** — POC at top of bar = bearish; POC at bottom of bar = bullish. Used as confirmation. (v108, [15:44]; v109, [02:52]; v111, [03:28]; v113, [02:24]) [REPEATED]
- **Price Rejector signal** — proprietary indicator flagging price rejection at a level. (v108, [04:38]; v109, [03:45]; v111, [02:34]; v113, [02:52]) [REPEATED]
- **O2X trade** (named setup in v109): bearish ratio at swing high, pull back within 2 ticks of that level, enter short; "X marks the spot much lower." (v109, [13:26]) [ONCE]
- **Three-in-a-row** (3 same-direction bars): signals that traders looking for a pullback entry may not get one; implies strength of the move. (v110, [00:52]; v113, [02:52]) [REPEATED]

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Supportive buying absorption**: Large bid-side volume at a low while delta is still negative. The market is "offering" but being absorbed. "I mean what's gonna happen is the markets gonna move up." When the aggressive buying returns, those selling are trapped. (v109, [03:45]; v108, [03:14]) [REPEATED]
- **Support level as "known" bid zone flipping to resistance**: Former support where large bids were resting — if price rallies then comes back down to that same level, the trader who had the big bid should have canceled it. If they didn't and got filled again, they're now a weak holder and that level flips. "If it rallies all the way up here and starts coming off don't look for it to be a great buy again." (v108, [18:06]–[19:02]) [ONCE — new nuance on level flip psychology]
- **Exhaustion at HOD/LOD via delta structure**: Strong buying (large positive deltas) leads to the high, then on the final bars at the high the delta turns negative — "at this point what do you think this market's gonna do?" (v108, [13:04]) [REPEATED]
- **Stalled delta at prior swing = trapped buyers**: "You trade right up to it okay then you start selling off." If price returns to a prior high-volume bar and sellers are at the offer, buyers who bid there have now turned into potential sellers. (v111, [04:36]) [ONCE]
- **Failed divergence = trend signal**: "Multiple failed divergences" and continuing strong negative delta = trend day, not reversal day. (v111, [10:00]; v110, [08:36]) [REPEATED]
- **Delta surge failure**: A delta surge fires, but the following 1–3 bars show the opposing delta returning. "That buying coming in — it's over, it's over already." Forces an exit of any long position. (v111, [07:39]) [ONCE]
- **Persistent negative delta near HOD = primed to move lower**: "You're up here at your high and you got predominantly negative deltas — what does that tell you? The market is getting primed to move lower." (v111, [23:35]) [REPEATED]

## G. Delta / delta-divergence ideas

- **Delta "stringing together"**: need multiple bars of consistent direction to confirm the move is real. One bar of positive delta during a sell does not mean the move is over. "Just because you got one bar with positive Delta does not mean the move is over." (v111, [06:21]) [REPEATED]
