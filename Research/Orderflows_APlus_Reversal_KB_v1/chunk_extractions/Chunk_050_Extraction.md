## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Trapped Traders / Extreme POC setup (bullish & bearish):** Point of control on the extreme of a bar (top of a red bar = resistance/supply absorbed; bottom of a green bar = support/demand absorbed). Magenta/purple color = resistance POC; blue = support POC. Signals that longer-timeframe traders are absorbing shorter-timeframe traders and will turn price against them. (v169, [34:00]–[37:18])

- **Overwhelming Imbalances (Multiple Imbalances) — continuation/reversal bar (bullish & bearish):** When there are more than five imbalances in a single bar, software draws a colored box around the bar. Used for both continuations and as a trigger to enter on the next bar. (v169, [37:47]–[39:33])

- **Ratio and Divergence setup:** Named explicitly as a separate setup he teaches frequently. Referenced alongside the multiple-imbalance setup and the one-second chart example. Definition not fully elaborated in this chunk. (v169, [39:33]–[40:27])

- **Confirming Order Flow Entry (entry execution gate):** Not a standalone setup but a meta-rule: wait for complimentary order flow after the signal bar before executing. If confirmation does not appear in the next 1–2 bars, skip or exit the trade. Applied to both long and short setups. (v170, [28:20]–[30:31])

- **"Bad Trade" recognition — trade management via order flow (bearish/bullish):** Using post-entry order flow (Delta, POC migration, imbalances) to detect early that a trade thesis is failing, and cutting it at a small loss rather than riding to the full stop. (v170, [00:29]–[26:03])

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Extreme POC / Trapped Trader psychology:** "Short term traders run into longer term traders with the opposite view. Those long term traders absorb the short term traders and then turn the market on them and move it in the opposite direction." The extreme POC on a bar is visual evidence of longer-TF supply or demand absorbing short-TF aggression. (v169, [34:00]–[34:52]) [REPEATED]

- **Overwhelming imbalance story:** Aggressive traders "lifting all the offers with impunity" or hammering bids — volume surges well above background (e.g., 200+ contracts vs. 20–30 in prior bars). Strong Delta accompanies. The market is about to take off because committed, well-funded participants are stepping in aggressively. (v169, [27:11]–[28:19]) [REPEATED]

- **Failed-entry / sideways decay story:** After a signal, if buying or selling absorption is present but mild, the market drifts sideways. "Imagine what's happening: there's some support of buying, you're going sideways — potential absorption going on. Once you remove that absorption the bottom falls out." The sideways action itself reveals the absorption is insufficient to fuel a real move. (v170, [20:46]–[21:40]) [ONCE]

- **"Market gives you an opportunity to get out":** Before going to the full stop, there is nearly always a bounce/recovery bar that provides an exit at a smaller loss. Recognizing this bounce as an exit opportunity (rather than a resumption signal) is the core trade-management insight. (v170, [24:40]–[25:39]) [REPEATED]

## D. Exhaustion clues

- Volume surge followed by stall: In the overwhelming-imbalance context, after several bars of expanding Delta and volume (e.g., 388 → 883 → 1847 on a 30-sec chart), the final bar probes higher but then starts dropping — Delta remains positive but price reverses. This is the exhaustion signal — "the move is finally over." (v169, [31:51]–[33:02]) [ONCE]

- Inability to extend: "Can't even take out the low of the signal bar" on the very next bar after a bearish signal = early exhaustion of the selling impulse. (v170, [33:49]–[34:20]) [REPEATED]

- Sideways drift post-signal: 7–8 bars without follow-through = "reason for the trade has deteriorated." The signal's energy has been absorbed/neutralized. (v170, [19:21]–[20:20]) [ONCE] NEEDS-OPERATIONALIZATION (bar count is illustrative, not a hard rule)

## E. Absorption clues

- **Sideways drift with mild positive Delta while short:** "You do have some support of buying... going sideways... potential absorption going on. ## G. Delta / delta-divergence ideas

- **Delta as direction filter (basic):** Positive Delta at a low = buyers are in control, market likely to reverse up. Negative Delta at a high = sellers in control, likely to reverse down. Absence of the expected Delta sign after a signal is a skip/exit cue. (v169, [22:59]–[23:53]; v170, [29:17]–[29:39]) [REPEATED]

- **Strong Delta definition (magnitude):** "Strong positive Delta 134... do you see any big positive Deltas? Biggest one's 27... 194 is almost double [119]." The comparison is always relative to the prior bars in the same instrument/session. No absolute threshold given beyond the illustrative numbers. (v169, [29:46]–[28:19]) [ONCE] NEEDS-OPERATIONALIZATION

- **Delta expanding with price = healthy move, not exhaustion yet:** Volume 388 → 883 → 1847, Delta 134 → 229 → 463 across three 30-sec bars = momentum still valid, keep holding. Entry is earliest bar, not the late bars. (v169, [31:18]–[32:32]) [ONCE]

- **Neutral/near-zero Delta is not automatically negative:** "Positive one... could be zero... it's kind of neutral in that sense." Requires other confirming/denying clues. (v170, [16:03]–[16:33]) [ONCE]

- **Delta sign flip check within order flow confirmation gate:** If short but next bar shows positive Delta = skip/exit. If long but next bar shows negative Delta = warning sign but not automatically fatal (depends on magnitude and other clues). (v170, [31:32]–[33:25]) [REPEATED]
