## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Initiative selling / initiative buying** (bearish/bullish): price breaks below a well-known bid level and sellers pile in below value; stacked selling imbalances appear immediately after the break. Distinguished from responsive selling. (v260, [05:12])
- **Absorption reversal at delta extreme**: market makes new lows (or new highs) on declining negative delta → delta turns positive (or vice versa) → passive buyers absorbing aggressive sellers → bullish reversal signal. (v267, [02:07]–[04:43])
- **Delta surge / delta divergence at HOD/LOD**: at the HOD, deltas sputter/dry up or go negative while price is still testing the high → distribution in progress → bearish signal. Mirror image at LOD for bullish. (v264, [10:31]–[11:33]; v267, [06:15]–[08:52])
- **Delta footprint absorption cluster**: multiple consecutive bars with negative delta at price (horizontal delta showing more aggressive sellers), yet price does not fall — passive buyers absorbing at that zone; when the zone holds on re-test it becomes a support ledge. (v265, [07:16]–[09:33])
- **POC-migration trend identification**: in a trending market, point of controls migrate directionally with price; when POCs stop migrating (cluster at same level) that signals a potential pause/reversal zone. (v268, [02:44]–[12:55])
- **Passive-seller resistance / supply-trailing-lower**: on a strong down day, sellers distribute by offering out at progressively lower levels rather than hitting bids; visible as heavy offer-side volume (bid < offer at multiple price levels) in the delta footprint even as price makes new lows; market goes down ON positive delta. (v270, [04:26]–[09:22])
- **POC-cluster resistance**: when 3+ consecutive bars print POCs at the same price zone, and market later rallies back to that zone and fails, it becomes overhead resistance; short on rejection with stop above the POC zone. (v270, [10:23]–[12:55])
- **Failed divergence / flow-driven market**: 3+ bullish divergences (positive delta, new/equal low) that get taken out with impunity → confirms trend day / flow-driven market; great shorting opportunities on bounces. Already in digest but materially reinforced here with a count. (v260, [10:39]–[11:14])
- **Pre-cash-open heavy delta foreshadowing**: heavy selling volume in the 5 minutes *before* the cash open (institutional pre-positioning) foreshadows the direction of the session; cumulative delta crosses zero early as a confirming lagging signal. (v263, [02:14]–[05:10])

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Initiative selling after big-bid break (v260)**: large visible bids (3,500–4,500 contracts at a price level) act as a magnet; "everyone sees this level." When it breaks, trapped longs must exit, stops fire, and initiative sellers pile in below value. Stacked selling imbalances appear immediately. Once below the big bid, that level flips to resistance: "if you have a big size that's looking to be support, you shouldn't be trading below it." (v260, [04:15]–[08:36])
- **Distribution at highs on small delta (v264)**: institutions want to sell near HOD where buyers are most eager; they offer out (passive), which results in small/declining deltas even as price retests the high. "If you have a lot of supply, distribute it up here near the highs where everyone is looking to buy." Eventually aggressive buyers exhaust themselves, passive supply wins, and price falls. (v264, [11:33]–[13:01])
- **Absorption at lows → reversal (v267)**: passive buyers absorb aggressive sellers at price; visible as declining negative delta (minus 173 → minus 79 → minus 46 → positive) on a move down. The absorption cluster becomes a support zone that the market re-tests before reversing. (v267, [02:07]–[04:43])
- **Positive delta at new lows = passive sellers distributing lower (v270)**: counterintuitive — market making new lows on positive delta (1,035+ positive delta on a red bar) means large sellers are offering it out at each new low rather than hitting bids; bottom fishers provide the aggressive buying. The passive sellers are "happy to trail it down" rather than clear the order book. (v270, [04:59]–[09:22])
- **Pre-open institutional pre-positioning (v263)**: large orders appearing 5 min before cash open at institutional scale (10,000–15,000 contracts in 5 minutes = "cash session volume already") signals the institutional book is slanted to that direction all day. "They're trying to feed it into the market, get some done before the cash open because they know they've got more to do." (v263, [04:05]–[04:38])

## R. Contradictions / nuances

1. **Positive delta at new lows is NOT always bullish**: three distinct scenarios, only one bullish — (a) passive buyers absorbing → reversal (bullish); (b) passive sellers distributing on a declining market → continuation (bearish); (c) delta divergence as exhaustion signal at HOD. The distinguishing test is the *offer-side footprint*: is the heavy volume on the offer side (bearish) or on the bid side (bullish)? Delta alone is insufficient. (v270, [04:59]–[08:52]; v267, [07:53]–[10:26]) [EXPLICITLY flagged as a potential trap for delta traders]

2. **Cumulative delta zero-cross is a lagging entry signal, not a trigger**: he explicitly warns against waiting for it as an entry; it only confirms a move already underway. Contrasts with common retail use of cum-delta cross as a signal. (v263, [05:42]–[06:40])

3. **DOM / heat map bids and offers are NOT confirming until they are trading**: "It's one thing to look at a limit order book and see the size there, but you want to see: is it trading? Because if it's trading there, it's absorbing." Visible size without transactions is not actionable. (v270, [13:49]–[14:17])

4. **Prior-day POC levels: he pulls them but does not endorse them**: "I don't like to pull those levels from the day before" — yet he identifies a 2-tick overshoot of a prior-day POC cluster on a 115-point sell day as "interesting." Conditional use, not a standard rule. (v260, [14:22])

5. **Small-delta bars near an extreme are ambiguous**: a bar with final delta ±13 cannot be called bullish or bearish — it "could easily have gone the other direction." The Max/Min Delta = 0 flags are more informative than the headline number in these cases. (v264, [05:46]–[06:18])

6. **NFP/CPI: don't be in the market AT ALL** — stronger statement than just "avoid the open." The correct action is to be entirely out, not just to widen stops or reduce size. (v270, [00:52]–[01:20])

7. **POC2/POC3 (2nd and 3rd highest-volume price levels)**: exist in the software and can be displayed, but he says for most practical purposes just the POC1 is sufficient; on a 4-range chart with only 5 price levels "you don't really need the second." Not used in his standard workflow. (v268, [01:25]–[01:54])
