## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Multiple Imbalances (off swing low / swing high)** — bars with multiple buying imbalances (not necessarily stacked) at a swing low trigger a bullish reversal entry; multiple selling imbalances at a swing high trigger a bearish entry. Key distinction: imbalances do NOT need to be stacked neatly; they just need to be present in the bar. (v212, [32:46])
  - Bullish variant: multiple buying imbalances at the low of the day or swing low. (v212, [33:13])
  - Bearish variant: multiple selling imbalances at swing high or high of the day. (v212, [32:46])

- **Value Area Gap** — occurs when a bar opens outside the previous bar's value area and does not trade back into it; instead trades away, forming a gap between value areas. Trade in the direction of the gap. (v212, [37:20])

- **Bearish / Bullish Point of Control (Slingshot POC)** — a prominently bearish or bullish per-bar POC signals resistance or support. "Extremely bullish / extremely bearish" grading. Software highlights them in magenta (bearish) or green/blue (bullish). (v212, [27:50])

- **Market Weakness (Bullish / Bearish)** — two variants:
  - Bearish Market Weakness: market rising but buying weakens (delta tapers, turns negative) → sell signal. (v211, [52:58])
  - Bullish Market Weakness: market falling but selling weakens (delta tapers, flips positive) → buy signal. (v211, [53:32])

- **Market Sweep** — a trader takes out several price levels in one trade. Sweep detector identifies them earlier than stacked imbalances on a standard footprint. (v211, [37:44])

- **Order Flow Sequencing** — bullish or bearish sequencing (resting liquidity stacked and taken out in a direction). Appears at swing highs/lows before the actual reversal bar. (v211, [43:55])

- **Stacked Imbalances (standard)** — 3+ imbalances stacked neatly on top of each other; used as near-term S/R. [REPEATED across both videos]

- **Delta Divergence** — price makes new low but delta turns positive (or vice versa); signals reversal. (v212, [30:50])

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Multiple Imbalances at Low**: "Price is an advertising mechanism — if it gets low enough you're going to attract people to buy at these levels." Aggressive buyers appear because the price is perceived as a bargain; selling dries up, then aggressive buying imbalances appear suddenly where there were none before. (v212, [31:44])

- **Value Area Gap**: Market opens outside previous bar's value area and cannot close back into it — signals the prior value is rejected; trapped longs (if gap down) or trapped shorts (if gap up) must exit, accelerating the move away from the gap. (v212, [37:20])

- **Bearish Market Weakness**: Buyers have been in control but "if everyone has bought, who's going to move the market higher? Nobody." Delta tapers: strong positive → less positive → neutral → negative. Market exhausts itself through weakening buying, not necessarily a sharp sell-off. (v211, [53:58])

- **Bullish Market Weakness**: Sellers are "getting less and less until buyers take control." Watch delta: strong negative → less negative → flip positive. (v211, [53:32])

- **Market Sweep at Turn**: Institutional buyer sweeps multiple offer levels in one order. Footprint shows little counter-trade (no contra-side imbalances) during the sweep. Once the sweep is identified, you can enter *before* the subsequent stacked-imbalance bar forms. (v211, [37:44])

- **Flow-Driven vs. Rotating Market**: In a rotating market, "the big players are absent… it's just the usual suspects." In a flow-driven market, institutional size creates sustained imbalances and large deltas in one direction. Know which regime you're in before entering reversals. (v211, [32:32])

## D. Exhaustion clues

- **Bullish Market Weakness signal** = selling exhaustion: delta on down bars weakens from strong negative to near-zero or positive; the software highlights with an arrow/indicator. (v211, [53:32])
- **Bearish Market Weakness signal** = buying exhaustion: delta on up bars weakens from strong positive to near-zero or negative. Example: 355, 137, 400, 414, 313, 27, then negative 300. (v211, [54:30])
- **Max delta = 0**: bars where max delta never turned positive means "aggressive buying never in control" — exhaustion of buyers in a down move. (v211, [37:14])
- **Market Exhaustion tool**: software draws lines at price levels in bars where no further trading below/above is occurring — "nobody interested in trading below this price level." (v211, [50:16])

## E. Absorption clues

- **Selling imbalances during a rally** = a large passive buyer absorbing supply: "There's a big passive buyer who doesn't want to move the market higher, happy to absorb that selling." Selling imbalances in an up-trending bar signal absorption, not breakdown. (v212, [14:32]) [ONCE]
- **Value area developing sideways** with gaps between bars = absorption zone; the bar-level value area analysis shows where volume is being accepted vs. rejected. (v211, [57:56])

## F. Stacked imbalance ideas

- Stacked imbalances (3+ neatly on top of each other) are used by "most order flow traders" as near-term S/R. But Mike explicitly says this misses the multiple-imbalance signal: "If you're looking for stacked imbalances you're not even noticing what's happening in this bar" (which has spread-out imbalances). (v212, [34:34])
- Stacked selling imbalances used as resistance: "You got stack selling imbalance, market traded back up… if the selling imbalance holds you look for the market to sell off." (v211, [30:58])
- When a stacked selling imbalance fails to hold (market immediately rallies through it), that is confirmation the prior multiple-imbalance bullish setup is working. (v212, [37:20])

## G. Delta / delta-divergence ideas

- **Neutral delta band ±50 in e-minis**: explicitly stated. Bars with delta of 2, 7, 0 = "basically neutral." Bar with 321 = "strong." (v212, [25:12]) [REPEATED from digest — confirms ±50]
- **Delta divergence at a low**: "Price made a new low but you got buying coming in — positive delta. That's a divergence." Next bar shows buying imbalances. (v212, [30:50])
- **Max delta / min delta**: max delta = peak of aggressive buying in a bar; min delta = peak of aggressive selling. "Bars where max delta is zero — aggressive buying never in control." Used as exhaustion confirmation. (v211, [37:14], v212, [18:34])
- **Cumulative delta**: running total for the session. Strong positive cumulative at 10:00 = strong day; near zero = directionless; strong negative = weak day. (v212, [19:32])
- **Delta weakens then flips**: the sequence in bullish market weakness: big negative → less negative → near zero → positive; triggers entry. (v211, [53:32])

## H. Failed breakout / breakdown / stop-run / liquidity-sweep ideas

- **Value Area Gap failure**: market rallies but cannot close the value area gap from the previous down bar. "It rallied two ticks from where it opened, couldn't even close that gap" — signals the prior bearish signal is intact, continuation lower. (v212, [27:18])
- **Stacked imbalance failure**: stacked selling imbalance appears during a rally off a low; if it "immediately failed" and market continues higher, that confirms the bullish multiple-imbalance reversal. (v212, [37:20])
- **Market Sweep early warning**: sweep detected at a swing high before the big down bar forms. "Where would you rather be short — up here at 95 or down here at 80? That's an extra 15 ticks." (v211, [43:26])

## I. Trapped-trader ideas

- **Value Area Gap traps longs/shorts**: when value gap occurs on the downside and market cannot fill it back, longs who bought expecting a recovery are trapped; their exits accelerate the sell-off. (v212, [38:22])
- **Multiple imbalances at LOD trap short sellers**: sellers who shorted near the low see aggressive buying imbalances appear; their stops (above entry) get triggered, adding fuel to the reversal. (v212, [33:13]) [SPECULATIVE — implied by the narrative of price advertising at low levels]

## J. Entry triggers (the exact "go" event)

- **Multiple Imbalance reversal entry**: enter on (or at close of) the bar that shows multiple buying/selling imbalances at a swing low/high — not waiting for a stacked imbalance. The software highlights the bar with a magenta/color box. (v212, [36:26])
- **Market Weakness entry**: enter when the weakness arrow/signal appears in the software (after delta has weakened and the signal fires). (v211, [50:16])
- **Value Area Gap entry**: enter in the direction of the gap when the current bar fails to close back into the prior bar's value area. (v212, [37:51])
- **Sweep entry**: enter on detection of the sweep at a key level (swing high/low), before the confirming imbalance bar. (v211, [43:26])

## K. Confirmation — what SHOULD happen quickly after entry/trigger

- **Immediate follow-through**: "For a market to move in the direction you expect it to there needs to be follow-through order flow in the direction you expect." (v212, [28:50])
- **Delta must shift**: after a bullish entry, next bars should show positive or strengthening delta; after bearish, negative/strengthening negative delta.
- **Value area should move in trade direction**: "We're starting to see value area high is higher, value area low is higher — starting to make that move back up." (v212, [32:09])
- **No opposing imbalances**: "As soon as you see this negative information you could manage your trade right" — absence of opposing imbalances confirms the reversal is holding. (v212, [29:52])

## L. Invalidation — what should NOT happen / what kills the thesis

- **Opposite order flow appearing immediately**: "You get long in here, the fact that you're seeing negative POC, negative value area moves, negative delta… you don't need to let it go stop you out. Even if you got long at 04 and have a stop at 4100 you don't need to come all the way down — as soon as you see this negative information you could manage your trade." (v212, [29:22])
- **Value area gap closes**: if the market fills back into the prior value area after a gap, the gap thesis is invalidated.
- **Delta re-strengthens in prior direction**: if you entered long and delta turns strongly negative again, the bullish market weakness thesis is invalidated.
- **Stacked imbalances hold against trade direction**: if a stacked selling imbalance holds and market cannot push through after a bullish entry, reassess. (v212, [36:53])

## R. Contradictions / nuances

- **Multiple imbalances fire BEFORE stacked imbalances**: explicitly and repeatedly stated. Standard order flow education teaches to look for stacked imbalances; Mike says this causes traders to miss earlier, better-entry signals from multiple (non-stacked) imbalances. This is a direct refinement/contradiction of the "wait for stacked" approach. (v212, [34:34], [36:26])

- **Imbalances in BOTH directions in a single bar** ≠ noise: "You see a bar with selling imbalances and a bar with buying imbalances in the same bar — it's just telling you it's a quiet market." Mixed imbalances within one bar = rotating/low-velocity regime, not a signal. (v211, [34:32])

- **Market can go UP on selling imbalances** (absorption): "Sometimes you do see selling imbalances in a market that's moving up — because somebody is there absorbing the selling." Selling imbalances ≠ always bearish; context-dependent. (v212, [14:32])

- **Delta "not always" directional with price**: "Generally as the market is selling off delta in each bar is negative — not always but generally." Explicitly hedged. (v211, [26:12])

- **Value area gap ≠ trade gap fill**: unlike traditional gap-fill theory, a value area gap in the footprint is a continuation signal (trade away from it), not a fade-the-gap signal. (v212, [37:51])

- **"It doesn't need to be the low of the day"**: multiple-imbalance setup works at any swing low, not exclusively at HOD/LOD. Nuance vs. digest language about "clean swing/HOD/LOD." (v212, [32:46])

- **Tight stop below the imbalance bar** (not at the swing extreme): the early entry via multiple imbalances allows a stop below the imbalance bar itself, not the prior swing low — potentially tighter than "1 tick beyond extreme" from digest. (v212, [23:38]) [SPECULATIVE — implied by the tighter-entry narrative]
