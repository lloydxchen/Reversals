## A. Setup types & names he uses
- **POC Wave** — 3-bar point-of-control pattern. Bearish: green bar (bar1 POC), then red bar with HIGHER POC (bar2), then red bar with LOWER POC than bar1 (bar3). Bullish = mirror: red candle bar1, green bar2 with LOWER POC, green bar3 with HIGHER POC than bar1. (v424, "POC Wave", [02:21]–[03:23]) [ONCE]
- **Delta Breakout** — Delta expanding strongly from small/neutral to large positive (bullish) or negative (bearish) over consecutive bars; box drawn around the breakout bars. (v429, "Delta Breakouts", [08:40]–[09:15]) [ONCE]
- **Orderflow Gap ("Gap and Go")** — gap between value areas of two consecutive footprint bars; bullish or bearish. Distinct from ICT fair-value gap. (v443, "Identifying Gaps", [00:26], [10:29]) [ONCE]
- **Exhaustion Print** — lack of trading volume at the edge of a bar (top of red / bottom of green). (v447, "Two Order Flow Nuances", [09:02], [10:39]) [REPEATED — also v443 [11:22]]
- **Imbalance Reversal** — "a crude sign of absorption or distribution taking place in a bar"; selling imbalance at bottom of green candle or buying imbalance at top of red candle. (v447, "Two Order Flow Nuances", [09:32], [10:39]) [ONCE]
- **Thin Print** — thin/zero volume in the two-way auction inside a bar; momentum sign, and a gap-like level the market returns to fill. (v456, "Top Footprint Technique", [04:32]) [ONCE]
- **Negative Delta at the high of the day** (his unofficial title for v442) — market makes new highs on persistent negative cumulative/bar Delta = reversal-down warning. (v442, "Order Flow Reveals", [00:51]) [ONCE — but core reversal idea]

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup
- **Negative Delta at highs (institutional distribution):** Institutions with big books to sell find it "**easiest to sell in a rising market, not in a falling market**" — in a rising market they get good fills; in a falling market they chase. So persistent aggressive selling absorbed by passive bids while price grinds up = a big seller distributing. Eventually either "**the aggressive sellers dry up**" OR the buyers supporting the move "**get loaded up with all the gold they want**" and stop buying → then selling pressure wins and market drops. (v442, [07:14], [16:36]–[18:17]) [ONCE — high value]
- **Tug-of-war framing:** aggressive selling "being absorbed by strong passive bids"; every burst of positive Delta is "met with negative flow," like a rubber band wrapped around a finger — "something's got to give." (v442, [07:43], [09:29], [10:01]) [REPEATED within video]
- **Trapped traders:** seen better on footprint than bar chart; when traders are trapped "you're not going to see volume on the other side" (e.g. selling sweeps a low to 0×0, then immediately bounces — a big seller swept then market reverted). (v426, "Aggressive vs Passive", [00:47], [01:14]) [ONCE]
- **Exhaustion-print psychology:** a move is "exhausted"; if truly exhausted it "should start going in the opposite direction" right away. If the next bar just goes inside, the buyer/seller is merely pausing "to let the market calm down... catch its breath" before continuing — NOT a reversal. (v447, [13:39]–[14:42]) [ONCE]
- **Thin print = "poor order flow":** market moved too fast through liquidity; market "will look to correct it" by coming back to fill, like a gap. (v456, [07:42]–[08:11]) [ONCE]
- **Gap holding logic:** if price comes back toward a gap and prints exhaustion (e.g. 9, then 1) and reverses, the gap "is probably holding... traders aren't interested in selling it down into that gap." (v443, [11:22]–[11:53]) [ONCE]

## D. Exhaustion clues

## E. Absorption clues

## G. Delta / delta-divergence ideas
- **Definition:** Delta = net difference between aggressive buying (lifts offer = +1) and aggressive selling (hits bid = −1) at a price/bar. (v429, [01:19]–[01:55]; v442 [00:51]; v448 [12:38]) [REPEATED]
- **Small Delta = neutral / ignore:** values like 1, 3, 4 are "neutral... could go either way"; a single split-second trade could flip the sign. Don't read into a zero Delta — "just a coincidence." (v429, [02:58], [03:26]) [REPEATED]
- **Delta matters most when GROWING / strong:** "Delta numbers matter the most when they are growing... strong either positive or negative" (e.g. −89, −167, −80, −112 = strong aggressive selling). (v429, [04:21]) [REPEATED]
- **His "strong" reference for cumulative-/bar-Delta context:** "anything over 100 you got... that's it; everything else is less than 50." He gauges "strong" relative to surrounding bars, not a fixed number. (v442, [06:08]) [ONCE] (relative — NEEDS-OPERATIONALIZATION)
- **DELTA DIVERGENCE (core reversal signal):** price making new highs while bar/cumulative Delta is NEGATIVE (or vice versa at lows). At a new high you "expect positive Delta"; getting negative Delta there = something's wrong. (v442, [00:51], [03:52], [08:57]) [ONCE — highest-value reversal idea]
- **Self-negating Delta:** a +267 followed by selling that drags it back to +69; +255 immediately followed by −236 → "negates itself." Bursts of positive flow consistently met by negative flow = distribution. (v442, [07:43], [05:32]) [REPEATED within video]
- **Cumulative vs bar Delta:** he uses cumulative Delta for the bigger-picture reversal read, bar Delta for the moment. Tracks cum Delta drifting more negative as new highs print (−86 → −278 → −1000 → −3000+). (v442, [02:47]–[11:02]) [ONCE]
- Confirm on higher timeframe: same negative-Delta-into-highs pattern visible on a 5-min chart (7 consecutive negative 5-min bars = 30 min of selling at highs) before the drop. (v442, [15:22]) [ONCE]
- **Color tooling for Delta strength:** cyan/light-blue = strong supportive (bullish) Delta; magenta = strong bearish Delta. Wants cyan at highs to trust continuation; seeing only magenta after the high = bearish. (v442, [12:20]–[14:52]) [ONCE]
- Opposite case at lows: lots of positive Delta at the lows = "somebody is trying to buy in a falling market" → bullish reversal info. (v442, [18:17]) [ONCE]

## R. Contradictions / nuances
- **Reversal vs momentum environment is decisive:** the SAME order-flow tool can be wrong if applied in the wrong regime — "maybe they're looking at something that's momentum based in a reversal environment." Tools must be matched to context. (v439, [01:54]; v447, [14:42]) [REPEATED]
