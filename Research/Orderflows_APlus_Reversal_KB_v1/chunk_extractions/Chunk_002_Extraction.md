- **"Ratio and Divergence" setup (buy off low / sell off high)** — his daily bread-and-butter reversal: at a new low, a bullish ratio + a delta divergence = buy; at a new high, bearish ratio + divergence = sell (v70, [08:05], [11:56], [13:46]; v80, [03:58], [04:26]; v47, [13:38]). [REPEATED]

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup
- Core thesis: a reversal needs **"the last buyer has bought and the last seller has sold"** — you catch ends of moves / beginnings of new moves, not ranges (v9, [03:25], [03:48]). [REPEATED]
- **Stopping volume story:** "the market is rallying rallying rallying, everyone's getting excited 'hey we're going higher,' boom there's a big seller there stopping this move higher and it just falls over" (v80, [06:43]). Mirror for buys: big buyer halts the decline (v80, [07:44], [09:30]). [REPEATED]
- **Trapped/excited longs at breakouts:** "people are getting excited, we're trying to break it out but boom it just runs into a big supply up here that halts the move" (v80, [09:30]). Breakout buyers are the ones who get hurt. [REPEATED]
- **Shift from demand-driven to supply-driven market:** strong positive deltas (1100/600/1200/1600) into a high, then Delta scalper flips = "demand has dried up, supply is coming into the market" → reversal down (v80, [11:40]–[12:37]). Mirror: selling dries up, buying takes over off a low (v47, [13:38]). [REPEATED]
- **Absorption/eat-back of delta at a low:** swing from −4,900 delta to only −900 = "you just ate back an additional 4,000 delta" → selling absorbed, expect bounce (v47, [13:38]). [ONCE, strong]
- **Stacked imbalance psychology:** "very aggressive buyers came in here to move the market higher… the buying dried up a little, came back down, more aggressive buying came back in… the buying that was here earlier is reappearing" → that level becomes support to defend (v47, [04:44]–[05:15]). [REPEATED]
- **Supportive buying under a big SELL imbalance (counterintuitive):** a 2381 selling imbalance you'd expect to drop, but "it's telling you the opposite… there's supportive buying down here, buyers coming in and bidding it" → short-term bounce (v70, [12:51]); same lesson v80 ([13:04] buyers absent = keep going). ## R. Contradictions / nuances
- **Big SELL imbalance can mean buy, not sell:** "you see a big imbalance like that you expect the market to sell off, but it's telling you the opposite — there's supportive buying down here" (v70, [12:51]). Contradicts naive "imbalance = continuation"; he explicitly teaches imbalance-as-support/resistance as the non-obvious use (v47, [09:31]; webinar tease). Nuance: depends on whether price holds above it and whether buyers actually appear (contrast v80 [13:04] where no buyers = continuation down).
- **"There's never more buyers than sellers"** (zero-sum) — he insists the right framing is more *aggressive* buying vs selling, not "more buyers" (v65, [00:37]; v9, [02:59]). Corrects common retail language.
- **He disclaims he is NOT picking tops/bottoms:** "people think you're always trying to pick highs and lows — no… I'm letting the market tell me there's a high/low here" (v70, [10:34]). Nuance vs the "price rejector picks swing highs/lows" framing.
- **Divergence is necessary-ish but not sufficient:** ratio+divergence is the setup, yet "divergence by itself is not a big enough reason," and divergences that FAIL repeatedly mean TREND (the opposite of reversal) (v70, [14:51], [11:00]). Conditional/"it depends."
- **Threshold settings are personal, not absolute:** "find your sweet spot"; explicitly refuses to give a one-size number and ties it to belief/confidence and market liquidity (v65, [07:11], [13:05]). Anti-mechanical caveat for any indicator port.
- **Signal quality vs user error:** he repeatedly reframes "losing" signals as the trader failing to take profits or holding too long, not bad signals (v9, [27:55]–[29:17]). Survivorship/attribution nuance.
- **Timeframe nuance:** he dislikes minute charts but concedes order flow works on 15-min for swing-style and in more volatile regimes (v80, [05:25], [15:52]).
- **Regime caveat:** during extreme news weeks (Brexit) the model breaks — "everything that normally works just doesn't work" (v9, [24:32]).
