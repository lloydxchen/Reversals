## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **"One contract at the high/low" exhaustion setup** (bearish at HOD / bullish at LOD): retest of daily high or low prints one contract (or near-zero volume) vs. thousands in prior bars — "the last buyer has bought / the last seller has sold." Called "one of my favorite setups," "automatic trade," "great white whale." (v297, "One Of My Favorite Order Flow Trade Setups", [00:28], [06:29])
- **Delta divergence + ratio combo setup at swing low/high**: bullish = new or equal low with positive delta on green candle + blue ratio reading; bearish = new or equal high with negative delta + ratio. Named "bread and butter" in v292 context. (v292, "Tale Of Two Lows", [02:23])
- **"Tale of Two Lows" quality-differentiation setup**: comparing two bullish signals at the same low — one with supply absorbing (bearish imbalance on the way up) vs. one with declining volume on retest and no unfinished auctions. The second is rated clearly superior. (v292, [05:06])
- **Anchored Volume Profile retest**: price returns to POC or VAL/VAH of a profile anchored from a key event (news, swing high/low, cash open) — used as a S/R trade and target. (v294, "Anchored Volume Profile", [03:09])
- **Value Scalper signal** (new indicator): detects disequilibrium/shifts in intrabar value area; outputs bullish/bearish triangles with optional follow-through gate ("trade entry signal" = 2 ticks over 2 bars). (v291, "How To Trade With Value Scalper", [06:57], [32:17])
- **Prominent POC acting as S/R**: POC that persists and is tested — shown on both time-based and range-based charts as a support/resistance reference. (v295, "Chart Types", [09:00])
- **Value area gap / expanding value area (EVA)**: gap between consecutive bar value areas signals potential breakout move. Called "bullish sign" when gap appears to the upside; also termed "Eva." (v292, [17:04]; v295, [18:36])

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **One-contract HOD retest**: first high prints heavy volume (813+ offer-side, 1000s contracts); second retest of same high prints only 1 contract. Interpretation: buyers no longer willing to pay up; someone may be "probing to attract buyers or trigger stops" but nobody follows. Next bar: strong negative delta confirms sellers taking control. (v297, [02:33], [03:02], [06:57])
- **Tom DeMark attribution**: "When the last buyer bought the move's over; when the last seller sells the move is over" — explicitly cited as lesson from DeMark seminar at Dean Witter in the 1990s. (v297, [03:02])
- **Tale of Two Lows — inferior trade**: strong supply comes into the rally bar (597 buying imbalance in a red declining bar = supply absorbing buying, not bullish). "Someone has supply to offer out to the market." Market closes the unfinished auction below. (v292, [07:57])
- **Tale of Two Lows — superior trade**: each time price pokes below the low, bids absorb selling and market bounces; volume shrinks dramatically on the final test (376 → 29 → 2). No unfinished auction. Resistance level flips to support. Bullish stacked imbalance appears. (v292, [09:19], [13:15], [15:58])
- **Delta footprint passive-buyer absorption**: at a swing low, negative delta bars reduce in magnitude as price revisits area ("negative, negative, negative — bids absorbing every time"). Then aggressive buying imbalances appear as price breaks above resistance. "Someone bidding up the market, not just lifting the offer." (v293, [05:00], [07:47])
- **Value Scalper psychology**: markets spend most of the time overlapping value (balancing); the opportunity is when price moves away from established value and seeks new value — "disequilibrium." Tool finds the moment one party's perceived value no longer matches another's. (v291, [06:02], [40:12])

## D. Exhaustion clues

- **Near-zero volume at HOD/LOD retest** is the primary exhaustion signal: "one contract at the high of the day… that just screams out to you that buyers are done." Contrasted explicitly with healthy bar volume (20,000 contracts per bar in prior bars). (v297, [02:33], [06:29])
- **Volume declining dramatically on second test of a low**: from 376 → 29 → 2 contracts at the same price level is a key exhaustion marker. "Where are my strong sellers, they're not there anymore." (v292, [12:42])
- **Buying drying up on rally after bullish signal**: "if the buying dries up and strong selling comes in, you'll get stopped out" — follow-through buying is required after entry, failure to sustain is an immediate exit signal. (v292, [06:01])
- **Small delta at extremes**: delta goes from large negative → smaller negative → positive (e.g., -607 → -114 → -70 → positive) indicates exhaustion of selling pressure. (v292, [07:30])

## E. Absorption clues

- **Big buying imbalance in a declining/red bar = bearish absorption (supply absorbing buying)**: "it's actually bearish because the market is starting to tick down and someone is coming in with supply to absorb whatever buying is taking place." IMPORTANT nuance: imbalance direction alone doesn't determine bullish/bearish; price direction + imbalance must agree. (v292, [07:57])
- **Repeated negative delta at a support level shrinks each test = bullish absorption**: "every time it would come in these bids would absorb that selling, market would trade back up." Passive bids resting absorb aggressive selling. (v293, [08:20])
- **Aggressive buying imbalances continue ABOVE former resistance = absorption confirmed, more move ahead**: "once you get past that resistance and you still see aggressive buying, it's telling you there's more to this move." (v293, [11:30])
- **Value area overlap across multiple bars = balancing / absorption of directional pressure**: multiple consecutive bars with overlapping value areas show market is absorbing and rotating, not trending. Gap in value areas = absorption phase ending. (v292, [16:54], [17:04])

## F. Stacked imbalance ideas

- **Stacked selling imbalances appear faster on range-based chart** at a high, before the equivalent appears on a time-based chart — "on the range base chart it's going to come in a little bit quicker." This is a key tactical advantage of range charts. (v295, [07:54])
- **Stacked buying imbalances after resistance break as confirmation**: after price clears former supply level, stacked buying imbalances reinforce the move. "Stack buying imbalance comes in." (v292, [15:26])
- **Stacked selling imbalances on time-based chart catch consolidation → breakdown**: 5 POCs aligned at the same price for 30 min, then stack selling imbalance → "a strong signal that we could punch lower." (v295, [13:12])
- **EVA (expanding/gap in value areas) = pre-stacked-imbalance signal**: value area gap precedes the price move; stacked imbalances then confirm direction. (v292, [17:24])

## G. Delta / delta-divergence ideas

- **Bullish delta divergence definition (explicit)**: "new or equal low with positive delta on a green candle." Bearish: "new or equal high with negative delta [on a red candle]." (v292, [02:23])
- **Ratio (blue number) as second filter**: ratio reads order flow in the bottom of a green candle or top of a red bar; blue coloring = within a parameter indicating "price defense or price rejection." Black = normal order flow, not actionable. (v292, [03:26])
- **Divergence + ratio = "bread and butter" but NOT sufficient alone**: "having a ratio and divergence is good, it's almost good enough [but] you still want to follow the order flow after you get into the trade." (v292, [06:37])
- **Delta footprint (horizontal delta at price) vs. diagonal delta**: he prefers horizontal delta footprint as secondary chart because it better shows where absorption is happening vs. the diagonal delta chart. (v293, [03:33])
- **Delta magnitude signal for exhaustion**: bar delta of minus 1400 out of 20,000 bar volume = ~40% negative = "strong selling" confirmation after the one-contract high. (v297, [11:25])
- **Progressive delta weakening at lows**: -607 → -114 → -70 → positive = sellers exhausted, bullish signal developing. (v292, [07:30])

## H. Failed breakout / breakdown / stop-run / liquidity-sweep ideas

- **One-contract probe at HOD**: speculates the single contract may be "someone trying to trade it to trigger other buyers or trigger stops" — but failure to attract follow-through = failed breakout confirmation. (v297, [06:57])
- **Unfinished auction "hanging over" the trade**: when an unfinished auction exists (bid/offer printed but not closed), market often comes back to close it — this overhangs a long trade and is a reason the first low in v292 is inferior. "Not written in stone but have it in the back of your mind." (v292, [07:05])

## J. Entry triggers (the exact "go" event)

- **One-contract HOD/LOD retest**: bar closes at/near the extreme with near-zero volume → enter short (for HOD) / long (for LOD) on bar close. "As soon as this bar closes, it's go time." (v297, [04:35], [10:33])
- **Value Scalper trade entry signal**: condition met in signal bar + price moves 2 ticks beyond signal bar's extreme within the next 2 bars → triangle appears; that bar (the one that moves 2 ticks) is the entry bar. Stop above/below the signal bar's high/low. (v291, [32:17], [33:27])
- **Divergence + ratio combo**: ratio + divergence at swing low/high, then watch for follow-through order flow to confirm. Entry on the bar that shows continued positive (or negative) order flow, not merely on the signal bar alone. (v292, [06:37])
- **Anchored profile POC/VAL/VAH retest**: drop a buy stop at the POC (or VAL for bullish play); market tests it and shows order flow support → enter. (v294, [03:09])
- **Breaking resistance flipped to support**: buy stop placed above the resistance level (e.g., O2 in v292 example); when price trades above it, enter long; stop below the newly-confirmed support. (v292, [14:53])

## R. Contradictions / nuances

- **Buying imbalance can be bearish**: a large buying imbalance in a red (declining) bar indicates supply absorbing buying — the opposite of what most traders assume. This directly nuances the standard "buying imbalance = bullish" rule. "The 597 buying imbalance here — some people will say isn't that bullish? In this case it's actually bearish." (v292, [07:57]) [REPEATED across multiple videos but stated with unusual clarity here]
- **Heavy volume at extreme does NOT equal exhaustion — context is bar direction AND position**: 20,000 contracts per bar is healthy; the one contract at the HOD retest is exhaustion because it's at the extreme AND almost nothing traded vs. prior bars. Must compare to the same price level, not just bar volume. (v297, [03:37])
- **"Doesn't happen every day" — highest-rated setup is the rarest**: the "automatic trade" one-contract setup is explicitly rare; caution against over-weighting it in a daily model. (v297, [11:54])
- **Value Scalper is NOT a profile or VWAP tool**: "it's not like VWAP or total value area — it's reading what's happening in a shorter time period." Specifically a short-term intrabar value tool, not a daily/multi-day structure tool. (v291, [51:12])
- **Range-based charts see some signals earlier but miss prolonged balance signals**: range charts show stacked imbalances earlier in fast moves; time-based charts reveal POC clusters and extended consolidations. Neither is universally better — each reveals different information. Contradiction of any claim that one chart type is strictly superior. (v295, [07:54], [13:12])
- **Tick replay required for Value Scalper historical data**: without tick replay enabled, value scalper calculates only from the moment the chart is opened — historical signals are not available. This is a research/backtesting constraint. (v291, [06:57])
- **Unfinished auction is a consideration, not an absolute veto**: "I don't want you not taking a trade just because there's an unfinished auction there — but have it in the back of your mind." Conditional, not binary. (v292, [07:05])
- **Anchored profile start point is "kind of irrelevant" in an absolute sense**: the anchor is whatever makes the most sense given what just moved the market — there is no universal anchor point rule. This nuances any fixed "anchor from open" approach. (v294, [10:24])
