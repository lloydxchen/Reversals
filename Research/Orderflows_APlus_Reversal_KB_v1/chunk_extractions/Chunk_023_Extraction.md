## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **O2X setup** (bearish variant described): new high (the "O" = circle the high), pullback within 2 ticks of that high, sell at retest, stop 1 tick above the high. Named explicitly. (v28, "Orderflows Market Analysis February 23 2017", [16:41])
- **Ratio + Divergence** (combo): ratio signal fires at a high with negative delta; serves as a bearish setup trigger. Mentioned in ES analysis pre-cash-open. (v28, [05:04])
- **Delta Surge** (buy/sell): proprietary indicator fires at a level; used as a directional signal. Not always acted on if at POC mid-range. (v28, [05:55])
- **Stacked Buying/Selling Imbalance** (support/resistance reversal): 3+ same-color imbalances; when price revisits and a counter-color imbalance appears, that is the exit/reversal trigger. (v29, [26:46]; v28 [03:19])
- **POC-as-support/resistance**: in a trending market POC of prior bar(s) acts as S/R for next bar. Explicitly tracked as a predictive tool. (v29, [19:58])
- **Delta Divergence at extreme**: negative delta at high of day or positive delta at low of day as a context flag (bearish/bullish backdrop). (v28, [05:04], [07:21])
- **Delta Max/Min Threshold (10,000+) on 10-range chart** (directional momentum entry): if intrabar delta reaches or exceeds ±10,000 on a 10-range ES chart, treat as a directional momentum entry — almost "close eyes and press buy/sell." (v28, [14:40])
- **Multiple-imbalances off a low** (bullish reversal confirmation): stacked buying imbalances at a swing low followed by aggressive buying volume (900–2,300 at price) mark the start of a rally. (v28, [10:07])
- **Point-of-control line-up (3+ POCs)**: 3+ consecutive bars with POC at the same level signals a sideways/congestion market; combined with strengthening negative delta it forecasts a further leg down. (v29, [36:04])

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **O2X (ZB bearish example)**: At a new high, price-action buyers ("price action guys") anticipate a pullback-to-long setup — they see the retest as a buy signal. Simultaneously, an aggressive seller comes in at the exact retest level (imbalance appears on the way back up), "someone came in and sold the heck out of it." The O2X trader fades the bullish price-action crowd using the sell imbalance + ratio + POC at top as confirmation. Trapped: bullish pullback buyers are immediately offside. (v28, [17:33])
- **Delta surge at POC mid-range**: Even when the delta surge fires a valid structural signal, if it's right at POC/mid-value-area, the "everyone's happy to trade" psychology means both sides transact freely — no one is trapped, so follow-through is unlikely. Explicitly: "value is the area where everyone's happy to trade … back and forth people that got to move sides." (v28, [06:24])
- **Stacked imbalance support failure**: Buyers lean on the stacked buying imbalance (their logical stop is far below). When a selling imbalance appears within/near that level, it means aggressive sellers have returned to the exact price where bulls were comfortable — those bulls are now trapped. The exit signal (selling imbalance appearing at stacked-buy-imbalance zone) is the signal that the support thesis has failed. (v29, [33:54])
- **Trending market delta deterioration**: After an initial leg from a low, if price goes sideways AND delta becomes increasingly negative (−221 → −446 → −1,300), sellers are "grabbing control by the neck" and a new leg lower is imminent. Buying in this context is fighting the tape. (v29, [36:56])
- **10,000+ delta bar**: Massive one-sided aggression in a single bar on a 10-range chart — represents institutional/large-order flow that is almost self-fulfilling; traders should simply follow direction. (v28, [14:40])
