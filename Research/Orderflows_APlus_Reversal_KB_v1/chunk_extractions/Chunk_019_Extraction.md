## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Stacked imbalances** — 3+ consecutive imbalances in same direction; used both as directional signal AND as a S/R zone that price pulls back into (v14, "3 Keys Webinar", [13:18]). Bullish or bearish depending on direction.
- **Huge imbalances** — imbalances where the raw volume exceeds a threshold (example given: >3,000 contracts on e-mini); these mark areas where strong directional participants transacted heavily; used as support/resistance zones on pullback (v14, "3 Keys Webinar", [14:12]–[16:10]). [ONCE]
- **Imbalance at extreme of bar (inverse/trapped-trader)** — selling imbalance at TOP of a bar that CLOSES DOWN (bearish arrow); buying imbalance at BOTTOM of a bar that CLOSES UP (bullish arrow). Described as unique to his software, signals a short-term reversal (v14, "3 Keys Webinar", [17:57]–[19:46]).
- **POC-at-extreme (stopping volume)** — Point of Control at the top of a red bar = resistance/stopping volume; POC at bottom of a green bar = support/stopping volume. Particularly potent at swing highs/lows (v16, "POC At Extremes", [00:25]–[00:54]).
- **Delta divergence at high/low** — price makes new high but delta turns negative (or weakens dramatically), or new low with delta turning positive. Signals exhaustion and potential reversal (v14, "3 Keys Webinar", [27:08]–[28:58]).
- **Stacked buying imbalance FOLLOWED BY stacked selling imbalance within 5-bar zone** — when a stack buy imbalance is immediately followed (within the same ~5 bars) by a stack sell imbalance, the original stack buy is negated and treated as bearish (v16, "POC At Extremes", [06:45]–[07:41]).
- **Valtos Flip** — indicator that reads "sudden abrupt changes in buying and selling which often precede explosive moves"; designed for scalping/short-term (v17, "Valtos Indicator Suite", [06:18]). [ONCE]
- **Valtos Transition** — indicator reading change from supply-driven to demand-driven market (or vice versa); medium-term signal (v17, "Valtos Indicator Suite", [07:53]–[10:34]). [ONCE]
- **Valtos U-turn** — indicator reading order flow to find "hidden support or hidden resistance" where buyers/sellers have given up; short-term to swing (v17, "Valtos Indicator Suite", [11:22]–[11:51]). [ONCE]
- **Absorption at low** — small consecutive delta numbers (e.g., 39, 71, 38, 73, 27, 25) at a key level indicating market facilitating trade without directional control; followed by increasing positive delta as signal (v14, "3 Keys Webinar", [29:32]–[31:02]).

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Inverse imbalance / trapped trader**: "You have the market trading up, you have aggressive buyers pushing it higher, but there's a strong seller up there. You get this imbalance [selling at top of up bar], then bar closes lower. You're left with this imbalance at the top of the bar — that's a very bearish signal." Psychology: buyers tried to push higher, were absorbed/blocked by a seller, bar closes against them — they are now trapped long at the top of a failing bar (v14, "3 Keys Webinar", [18:24]–[19:23]).
- **"Trapped quantity is small — it's psychology, not forced-exit"**: "74 lots isn't really that trapped and it's really a change in psychology more than anything else because there's enough volume in the markets. Even if it was 700 lots they have this imbalance, there's still plenty of volume to get out over the next bar or two. Maybe I have a different definition of trapped than other traders." (v14, "3 Keys Webinar", [19:46]). [REPEATED — same nuance as digest]
- **Absorption at a low (small delta)**: "How do you know the market has found a level that it can trade around that everyone's happy to trade? Markets exist to facilitate trade and delta is going to tell you generally that — hey, the market's facilitating trade by having small deltas; no one's really in control." Then aggressive buying picks up as a go-signal (v14, "3 Keys Webinar", [29:32]–[30:26]).
- **POC-at-extreme story**: POC at the top of a red bar indicates "volume in the market stopping that move." At a swing high, it signals the market is being defended; at a swing low, buyers are stepping in (v16, "POC At Extremes", [00:25]–[00:54]).
- **Stacked buy then stacked sell within 5 bars**: Original stacked buy represented demand; the stacked sell that follows within the zone means supply has overpowered that demand, the bull thesis collapses (v16, "POC At Extremes", [07:14]–[07:41]).
- **Huge imbalance psychology**: "I know at this level there's strong buyers — people happy to buy it at this level." The big absolute volume at a price marks institutional interest; when price returns, that level is defended (v14, "3 Keys Webinar", [15:14]–[16:36]).

## J. Entry triggers (the exact "go" event)

- **Imbalance at extreme of bar**: trigger is the close of the bar that shows the inverse imbalance (selling imbalance at top of down-close bar, or buying imbalance at bottom of up-close bar). Entry on next bar (v14, "3 Keys Webinar", [18:24]–[19:23]).
- **Delta divergence**: "If you want to take it a step further... if you're not convinced in this bar and you wait for this bar to confirm it — still giving you a decent chance to get in." Second-bar confirmation entry acceptable (v14, "3 Keys Webinar", [32:58]–[33:27]).
- **POC at extreme + ratio bounds low + divergence**: in the POC-at-extreme setup, entry is in the next bar after the signal bar. Example: "You should be looking to get in on the next bar — somewhere around 66.5." (v16, "POC At Extremes", [04:16]).
- **Delta building off a low**: after small-delta absorption period, see 127, 181, 276 building positive delta — go long as buyers accumulate; entry on increasing delta reading (v14, "3 Keys Webinar", [30:26]–[31:02]).
- **Stacked buying imbalance FOLLOWED by stacked selling imbalance within 5 bars**: DO NOT enter long on the stacked buy; the ensuing stacked sell confirms bearish (v16, "POC At Extremes", [07:14]–[07:41]).

## R. Contradictions / nuances

- **Stacked imbalance ≠ automatic entry**: A stacked buying imbalance at a swing high with POC at top + divergence + bearish ratio should NOT be bought — in fact it should be faded. This directly contradicts naive reading of stacked-imbalance rule. The context (location + other confluence) overrides the imbalance direction (v16, "POC At Extremes", [03:07]–[04:16]). [REPEATED in spirit but this is a live worked example reinforcing the anti-blind rule]
- **"Trapped" is psychological, not mechanical**: Mike explicitly distinguishes his interpretation from other educators — he does NOT believe trapped traders will be squeezed out because volume is sufficient to absorb exits; the signal is the change in psychology, not forced liquidation (v14, "3 Keys Webinar", [19:46]).
- **Imbalance at extreme of bar**: "Doesn't necessarily have to be the low of the day... sometimes it might be on the bar right after it, or even the second bar after it." — The trigger bar does not have to be a key swing/day-low; local context matters, not only HOD/LOD (v14, "3 Keys Webinar", [21:47]). Slight nuance vs. the A+ requirement for "clean swing/HOD/LOD."
- **Stacked buy + stacked sell within 5 bars is bearish**: even though the stacked buy would normally be bullish; the immediate follow-on stacked sell within the decay window negates it entirely — the zone indicator makes this explicit (v16, "POC At Extremes", [07:14]).
- **Holiday/thin conditions**: Mike watches markets but does not trade them (week between Christmas and New Year); implies signals may form but the context (thin volume) reduces reliability (v16, "POC At Extremes", [00:00]–[00:25]).
- **Ratio indicator value**: a ratio of 5917 is described as "poised to sell off" but the formula is explicitly not disclosed ("a ratio unique to my orderflow trader software — I'm not going to discuss it") (v14, "3 Keys Webinar", [32:00]). Digest notes ratio ≥30 (exhaustion) / ≤0.69 (defense); the 5917 value here is from a different ratio metric (appears to be the displayed imbalance ratio at one bar, not the normalized exhaustion ratio).
- **POC tolerance is a zone, not a price**: "It's support and resistance levels, not support and resistance price. It's fine to get through it by a tick or two — it's not the end of the world." (v14, "3 Keys Webinar", [40:12]). Operationally: accept 1–2 tick penetration without invalidation.
- **V17 indicators are bar-chart overlays, not footprint-dependent**: the Valtos Flip/Transition/U-turn run on normal bar charts (NinjaTrader), not requiring the footprint display — this implies the order-flow signals can be abstracted and do not require per-price-level footprint data visible to user (v17, "Valtos Indicator Suite", [03:39]–[04:03]).
