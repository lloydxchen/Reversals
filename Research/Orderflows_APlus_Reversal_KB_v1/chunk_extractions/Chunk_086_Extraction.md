## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Divergence low**: Sellers dominate (expected at lows), but buyers are stepping in aggressively — the "last sellers sold" moment. The positive Delta at the low tells you supply is being absorbed; aggressive buying is now dominant. (v288, [02:15])
- **Stacked imbalances at exhaustion**: Four buying imbalances in one bar + Delta closing at Max Delta = the bar is "dominated by aggressive buyers." Sellers who came in at the pullback are trapped because the bar never let sellers control. (v288, [05:04])
- **Zero Min Delta**: Sellers were present but never turned Delta negative — they are too weak to overwhelm buyers. This is a bullish structural sign because the direction of aggression is unbroken. (v288, [07:06])
- **Buying tail psychology**: Anxious/greedy traders sell prematurely at lows (or buy prematurely at highs) before the real move — they get trapped. The footprint shows very light volume at those levels. Price returns to repair the "bad structure" they left. (v290, [51:52])
- **Market sweep**: A large institution or desk needs to fill urgently; they sweep all available liquidity (bids or offers). This leaves a directional footprint that precedes continuation or signals that liquidity vacuum has been created. (v290, [44:21])
- **Market weakness**: Buying aggression diminishes as price rallies (delta sequence declining: 400 → 178 → 163 → 78 → -14). The market is still going up but the fuel is running out; sellers begin asserting. The reversal precedes the candlestick evidence. (v290, [57:50])
- **EVA follow-through failure**: Bullish EVA fires but next bar goes inside or only 1 tick higher — "other order flow coming into the market" has negated the signal; trapped longs are immediately offside. (v288, [14:25])
