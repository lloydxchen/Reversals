## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Delta divergence (ratio + divergence)** — bearish: high with negative delta + ratio on same or next bar; bullish: low with positive delta (or delta turn) + ratio on same or next bar. Primary entry trigger across all five videos. [REPEATED]
- **Stacked selling/buying imbalance** — three or more same-color imbalances indicating directional aggression. Used at highs (bearish) and lows (bullish). [REPEATED]
- **Multiple imbalances** — two or more imbalances in a single bar treated as a directional signal, especially when appearing in cluster across adjacent bars (e.g., 1-2-3 imbalances in one bar). [REPEATED]
- **Point of control on the extreme (POC extreme)** — POC prints near the top or bottom of a bar, indicating the bulk of volume traded at that extreme — bullish when near the bottom, bearish when near the top. [REPEATED]
- **Single prints / price rejection** — thin-volume zones on a footprint bar indicating rapid price movement/rejection; bullish single prints at lows, bearish at highs. (v89, [14:22]; v94, [17:17])
- **Trapped traders (at highs)** — large volume on the offer side at the high of a move followed by reversal; buyers become forced sellers. (v95, [27:05]) [REPEATED]
- **Delta surge / transition** — delta swings dramatically from negative max to positive close (or vice versa) within a single bar, indicating absorption/shift; also referenced via the "Transition" indicator. (v94, [19:58]; v95, [04:24]) [REPEATED]
- **Failed divergence / failed signal** — divergence signal that does not produce a move; multiple failed divergences in a short window (~8:00–10:00) seen as evidence of a trend day bias upward. (v89, [09:10]) [REPEATED]
- **Stacked imbalance failure / reversal** — stacked imbalances that fail to produce a move and instead see supportive buying at the level are treated as a bullish reversal signal (imbalance holding = no-follow-through = thesis abandoned). (v94, [03:35]–[05:47]) [REPEATED per digest nuance]
- **Delta Scalper signal** — proprietary indicator reading delta; gives buy/sell signals; used as confirmation or early trigger. [REPEATED]
- **Price Rejector signal** — proprietary indicator appearing at highs as a sell signal. (v93, [03:15]; v95, [06:36]) [REPEATED]
- **Zero prints (bullish)** — price skips a level (no trades), indicating rapid buying pressure. (v89, [13:52]; v93, [11:27]) [REPEATED]
