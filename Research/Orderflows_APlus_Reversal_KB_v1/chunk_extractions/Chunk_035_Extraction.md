## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Stop-induced imbalance psychology**: outside traders hold stops above the prior high; a sweep hits those stops creating a brief "all zeros" bar, fills them, then reverses immediately. "That's what a stop-induced stacked imbalance looks like — all zeros — usually in the next bar it comes right down." (v84 [11:39]) [ONCE]
- **Accumulation at low**: persistent positive delta while price hangs at a low signals someone systematically absorbing sell orders — "almost as if someone is accumulating a position." When price eventually breaks above the prior high, trapped shorts cover, fueling the rally. (v84 [02:23]) [ONCE]
- **Absorption exhaustion at high**: price at a prior stacked-imbalance level with still-positive delta = buyers are being absorbed by resting sellers / the bid side; when aggressive buying dries up (delta weakens) the setup is ripe for reversal. (v84 [03:44]) [REPEATED]
- **Demand → supply flip in crude**: buying imbalances all the way up, then suddenly selling imbalances appear — "aggressive sellers are taking control ... markets move based on supply and demand." (v84 [16:44]) [REPEATED]
- **Trapped traders at new extreme**: latecomers rush into a new HOD/LOD; a ratio + negative delta immediately after the print traps them; they must exit/cover, driving the reversal. (v86 [16:27], v88 [10:19]) [REPEATED]
- **Failed breakout psychology at a low (bearish stacked imbalance inside range)**: stacked sell imbalances appearing *at* the low (not *through* the low) attract breakout traders who pile in short; price stops right at the low and bounces, trapping the shorts. "I prefer stacked imbalances if they're coming in near a low on the other side of the low — going into new lows, not going into the low." (v85 [06:20]) [ONCE — explicit nuance]
- **Liquidity evaporation near news events**: "volume is just drying up already right before the minutes come out ... 37 in a bar — which is nothing." Pre-FOMC dryness = avoid signals; post-FOMC liquidity returns and signals become valid. (v86 [17:05]) [ONCE]

## R. Contradictions / nuances (anything that conflicts with common belief, with his other statements, or that is conditional / "it depends")

- **Stacked sell imbalance at a low ≠ always bear continuation**: stacked sell imbalances printed *within the range* near a low attract breakout shorts who are then trapped; Mike explicitly prefers stacked imbalances that form *through* the low (new territory). This nuances the simpler rule "stacked = continuation." (v85 [06:20]) [ONCE]
- **Positive delta at a high with large negative delta on next bar doesn't negate the trade**: "you've got the selling imbalance down here — you got a big volume number — you have negative delta — but read what's happening in the bar — you got bidding in here basically — in the area where the stacked imbalance was — so aggressive buyers are still there." Positive delta with resting bid = continuation, not reversal, until the bid is exhausted. (v84 [03:20]) [ONCE]
- **Accumulation (positive delta at a low) = bullish ONLY if price breaks higher**: if price hangs at the low with positive delta but never rallies, the positive delta could represent absorption of the rally attempt. Context-dependent. (v84 [02:23]) [SPECULATIVE — his framing was bullish but he noted the signal didn't immediately work]
- **Delta = 0 at a low is NOT a signal**: explicitly: "don't try to read too much into it — it's just coincidence." Contradicts any system that would treat zero-delta bars as special. (v88 [16:51]) [ONCE]
- **Three POCs in a row is a bullish/bearish signal, but ideal entry requires a pullback between them**: if the pullback doesn't arrive, he abandons the setup rather than chasing. "I want the bar to come right after the three in a row — it just sort of goes sideways at this point." (v86 [03:56]) [ONCE]
- **Delta Scalper on fast charts (233 tick / 4-range)**: he shows them but calls 233-tick "ridiculous" for serious trading and recommends only "quick hits" (3–5 ticks), not reversal trades. The same indicator logic applies but the timeframe/profit-target framework is completely different. (v84 [25:22], v85 [15:44]) [ONCE]
- **"Not a red-light/green-light system"** reaffirmed: even with all signals present, outcome is probabilistic; he shows multiple failed setups and explicitly states "it doesn't work that way — it reads the delta — if there's nothing in the delta up here to tell you to get short it's not going to give you a signal — period." (v86 [24:32]) [REPEATED]
- **Large negative delta at cash close ≠ clean reversal signal**: "massive orders to be executed on cash close — sometimes they will start them early — don't get too excited." Warns against using close-of-day delta spikes as independent reversal signals. (v88 [08:01]) [ONCE]
