## A. Setup types & names he uses
- **"Price rejection" / "price rejector" reversal** — buy or sell signal flagged when price is being rejected at a high/low/swing-high/swing-low after a move (v3, "Price Rejector Preview", [00:29]; v4, "Crude Analysis", [00:33]). The indicator's stated job: "determine when the **last buyer has bought and the last seller has sold**… identify low-risk trade entries at potential turning points in the market mainly **highs, lows, swing highs and swing lows**" (v4, [00:33]–[01:04]). [REPEATED]
- **Ratio / "blue number" rejection (footprint ratio)** — a separate, single-bar rejection sign Mike reads natively in his Orderflows Trader: "a blue number… at the end of a move… that's a sign of rejection" (v3, [01:01]). Used as confluence/"extra bonus" with the price rejector (v3, [07:17]). [REPEATED]
- **Divergence reversal (delta divergence at new/equal high or low)** — "negative delta at a new or equal high" → short (v5, "Trade Recap", [01:28]–[01:54]). [REPEATED]
- **Overlapping / opposing stacked-imbalance reversal** — a stacked buying imbalance that FAILS, then a stacked SELLING imbalance prints within the same 5-price zone → go with the SECOND (opposing) imbalance (v8, "Overlapping Imbalances", [02:29]–[03:59]). This is his clearest discrete reversal pattern with a named direction rule. [REPEATED in v8]
- **Stopping-volume reversal** — buy/sell where a move stalls on heavy volume that halts price (v3, [12:20]; v5, [11:17] "ratio bounce low indicating some stopping volume"). [REPEATED]
- Directional naming: green hash = **buy** signal, red hash = **sell** signal (v3, [03:52]).

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup
- **Core reversal thesis (fair vs unfair price):** "Markets move because it's looking for a fair price… Once it moves away from a fair price to an **unfair price**, that's where you're going to get… rejection" (v3, [02:56]). Rejection = the market refusing an unfair price. [REPEATED]
- **"Last buyer has bought / last seller has sold"** — the reversal is the exhaustion of one side; once the last aggressor is filled, no one is left to continue (v4, [00:33]). [REPEATED — central psychology]
- **Overlapping imbalance story (institutional order-working):** a stacked buying imbalance that fails means "these buyers… bought what they needed to do and they're not buying anymore… they're gone and the market sells off" (v8, [02:02]). When the opposing imbalance prints, "it went from a strong buying market to a strong selling market… a supply driven market… to a demand driven market… sellers have now become the buyers" (v8, [03:59], [07:30]). [REPEATED]
- **Iceberg / refreshing-size absorption story (bonds trade, v5):** "quite a bit of size here at 23… kept refreshing — those iceberg orders… I was really looking for that move to get through there and push lower… once we got through… you see a vacuum, the market gets sucked lower and it did" (v5, [07:55]–[08:25]). Breaking the refreshing iceberg → vacuum/acceleration. [ONCE — very concrete]
- **Trapped longs after a fake pop:** "you get long, you get that initial pop, and then it doesn't continue. This is usually not a good sign, especially once you see the red candles coming in" (v3, [21:24]). [REPEATED]

## F. Stacked imbalance ideas
- **Definition:** stacked imbalances = consecutive buying (blue) or selling (red) imbalances at successive prices; "blue numbers" = buying imbalance, "red numbers" = selling imbalance (v8, [01:01]). [REPEATED]
- **THE reversal rule (overlapping/opposing imbalances):** within a **5-price zone**, a stacked buying imbalance that FAILS followed by a stacked selling imbalance → **go with the second (opposing) imbalance**; "you go with the bear… in the direction of the second one… usually a quite powerful signal" (v8, [02:29]–[03:59]). [REPEATED in v8]
- **Same-direction stacks are weaker / he avoids them:** "I'm not a big fan of… buying imbalance, buying imbalance, because… you're going to have a stop that runs through, especially on a shorter-term chart" (v8, [11:37]). [REPEATED]
- **Chart-resolution sensitivity:** on a 4-range chart imbalances appear that would "fill in" and disappear on a 10-range chart — "I don't think you'd have this stacked imbalance if I was using a slightly longer chart" (v8, [12:01]). IMPORTANT implementation caveat. [ONCE]
- **Treat as immediate-term S/R only:** stacked imbalance acts as support only within those ~5 bars; "I don't look for it… to come back to that level… two hours later and react to it" (v8, [04:53], [14:06]). [REPEATED]
- **Configurable params he names (from his Imbalancer):** imbalance box size = 5 levels; minimum stacked = 2/3/4 (your choice); imbalance trigger % = 300% / 400% / 1000% (adjustable); minimum volume filter e.g. 200 lots (v8, [02:29]–[03:28]).
