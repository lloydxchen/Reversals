## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Ratio Divergence (bullish/bearish):** Positive delta divergence at a low + ratio bounds low = bullish signal; negative delta divergence at a high + ratio bounds high = bearish signal. Explicit "three in a row" rule: three consecutive blue ratio numbers constitute a setup; the fourth bar is the pullback/entry bar. (v24, [02:58]; v25, [01:01]; v26, [04:00]; v27, [03:45]) [REPEATED]
- **Three-in-a-Row Ratio Setup:** Three consecutive blue (bearish or bullish) ratio prints; look for pullback in the fourth bar, entry between the POC of bar 2 and bar 3 (or bar 3 and bar 2's POC). Specifically: "as long as it's three blue numbers I don't give a… I don't care if it's ratio bonds low or ratio bonds high, as long as it's three blue ratios." (v24, [20:19]; v26, [04:00]; v27, [03:45]) [REPEATED]
- **Delta Surge (bullish):** Indicator fires when buy-delta pattern picks up; used as an alert to catching a turn from rotation to trending. Free download indicator. (v24, [15:15]; v26, [05:16]) [REPEATED]
- **Ratio Bounds Low / Ratio Bounds High:** Sub-type of ratio signal indicating price rejection at the high or low of a bar, combined with a ratio number. (v24, [02:58]; v24, [08:03]; v25, [06:10]) [REPEATED]
- **Price Rejector signal:** Indicator giving a sell signal at a swing high with POC near top; referenced in soybeans (v25, [04:47]) [ONCE]
- **Point of Control at Extreme (stopping volume proxy):** POC right at the bottom of a bar = "decent sign of some stopping volume" but volume quantity matters for conviction. (v24, [11:22]) [REPEATED]
- **O2X Setup (bearish/bullish):** Pull back to within two ticks of the prior swing high/low, with POC at the high; stop is just above the high (three tick stop in example). Described as a named setup he "has written about many times on the website and recorded multiple videos on." (v27, [15:40]) [ONCE in this chunk — NAMED setup]
- **Minus Development:** Stacked buying imbalances on a bar that is turning down; the market fails to do what the imbalances imply = bearish signal. Defined explicitly: "buying imbalances on a bar that's turning down, that's minus development." (v27, [07:17]) [ONCE — explicitly named and defined]
- **Transition / Flip / U-turn indicators:** Proprietary indicators reading orderflow signals on bar charts. Settings referenced: Transition = 3, 100, 400; Flip = 5, 25; U-turn = 3, 500. All are reversal indicators in nature. (v24, [00:57]; v26, [18:22]; v27, [01:26]) [REPEATED]

- **Three-in-a-row ratio + pullback psychology:** The three consecutive ratios signal that price rejection is building. ## R. Contradictions / nuances (anything conditional or that conflicts)

- **"Let the market take you in" vs. acting on the signal bar close:** He explicitly critiques traders who enter "as soon as the bar closes" or "immediately" on a signal. His approach requires waiting for the market to trade through the signal bar's extreme by a few ticks. This is a refinement/nuance versus the digest's "follow-through via stop order (live)" description — he is using a stop order but set at a threshold beyond the bar's extreme, not just 1 tick over. (v26, [20:11]; v27, [21:16]) [ONCE]
- **Strong delta into a high is bearish — but not always:** "I wouldn't be getting short [at a high] when I'm staring at these very strong deltas on the way up — 2000, 2000, 4900 — and yeah it's -1300 which is quite strong but it's on the back of strong buying." Negative delta reading at a high is less reliable as a reversal signal when the prior bars showed very strong positive delta. The relative magnitude matters. (v26, [12:20]) [ONCE — explicit nuance]
- **Divergence without ratio = incomplete signal:** "Divergence, no ratio — and it just comes right back." Multiple examples where divergence alone failed. Ratio is required confirmation. But separately he says "if you get it in the next bar, go for it" — so the ratio can be one bar delayed. (v24, [09:00]; v24, [23:05]) [REPEATED]
- **"Three in a row" fourth-bar ideal is a red bar — but green can work:** "What I normally look for is this fourth bar to be a red bar — in this case it's green… what happens is you get the red bar further out." The idealized entry bar should visually be a pullback (red for bull setup), but he continues to look for the pullback even when the fourth bar is green; it just forces him to wait for a later bar. (v26, [06:21]) [ONCE]
- **Bonds and fives move together but not in sync:** "Sometimes it takes a while for them to move in the same direction — one may be ticking up while the other ticks down." Warning against mechanically assuming intermarket correlation is instantaneous. (v27, [15:07]) [ONCE]
- **"Perfect" signal can still fail:** "Perfectly legitimate place to get short — it didn't work out, okay, took a loss." He explicitly says a high-quality reading of the setup does not guarantee success; this is the core probabilistic framing. (v26, [15:13]) [REPEATED — consistent nuance]
- **Multiple indicators on same chart cause confusion/bad exits:** "If I've got three indicators… you're going to be getting out of trades too early because there's a signal here and a sell here." Explicitly anti-stacking. He runs the indicators on separate charts for different instruments. (v27, [32:22]) [ONCE]
- **Opening price stay-out conflicts with general "unchanged" filter:** The digest mentions "unchanged line stay-out" as a context filter. This chunk reinforces it with explicit rationale: POC keeps clustering at the opening price on trendless days, and buying imbalances that appear there mean nothing directionally.
