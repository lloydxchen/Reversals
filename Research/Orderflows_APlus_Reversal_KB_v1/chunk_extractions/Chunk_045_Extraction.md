## A. Setup types & names he uses
- **Order flows divergence + ratio + prominent point of control (PPOC) reversal** — his "bread-and-butter" reversal at highs/lows of day. Bearish: new/equal high + negative Delta + bearish PPOC (resistance) + confirming price action. Bullish: new/equal low + positive Delta + supportive PPOC + confirming price action (v144, "high percentage setup", [40:14]–[41:29]) [REPEATED].
- **Prominent point of control (PPOC) reversal/pullback** — buy first touch of a supportive (cyan/aqua) PPOC at a swing low; sell first touch of a resistance (magenta/pink) PPOC at a swing high. Can also play pullback (second-chance) to the same PPOC (v144 [15:44]–[17:50], [27:38]; v147 [02:32]; v150 whole video) [REPEATED].
- **Multiple-imbalance reversal** — 3+ imbalances in one bar (not necessarily stacked) signaling aggressive buyers at a low / sellers at a high (v144 [22:39], [23:33]) [REPEATED].
- **Stacked-imbalance reversal** — 3+ imbalances stacked directly on top of each other (the "zone"); he treats it as valid but says it makes you enter later/worse than the multiple-imbalance read (v144 [23:05], [23:33]) [REPEATED].
- **Trapped-trader reversal** — fade traders who got positioned late at a high/low and are stuck (v144 [38:36]–[39:25]; v149 [02:02]) [REPEATED].
- **Market sweep** — big trader sweeps through several price levels in one trade; used as confirmation of a turn (v144 [18:13]–[20:59]) [ONCE/REPEATED within v144].
- **Supportive-buying at first touch of swing low** (entry before a double bottom confirms) (v144 [36:27]–[37:45]) [REPEATED].

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup
- **Supply/demand turning points**: a turn = market shifting from demand-driven (buyers interested, price up) to supply-driven (excess inventory, price down) or vice versa; uses the new-Corvette-then-discounted-Corvette analogy (v144 [00:00], [26:25]–[27:15]) [REPEATED].
- **Trapped traders at highs**: late longs buy the breakout expecting higher; their buying is "met with supply," market sells off, they must get out (v144 [38:36]; v149 [02:51]–[04:07]) [REPEATED].
- **Trapped traders at lows**: traders chase a sharp move down, sell new lows late, market snaps back, they're trapped and must cover — needs enough volume there for the trapped size to exit (v144 [39:00]–[39:25]) [ONCE].
- **Big traders provide support/resistance**: they bid at a level, know price will bounce and come back so they can be patient and buy more — that's their edge; retail waiting for a double-bottom confirmation misses the better price (v144 [37:21]–[37:45]) [REPEATED].
- **Market sweeps** happen because someone "thinks something is about to happen" / has info / a big desk order must get done now (v144 [20:28]).
- **Head fakes**: false breakout above range highs — "everyone wants to pile in and start getting long" right before the sell-off (v149 [13:38]) [ONCE].

## D. Exhaustion clues
- At a high: predominantly **negative Delta bar after bar** ("negative negative negative one positive…") while price just goes sideways off the high → supply present, sellers willing (v149 [07:05]) [REPEATED].
- "**Stick a fork in it, it's done**": point of controls printing lower-lower-lower and Delta all negative and getting weaker (361 → 139 → −174 → −167 → −202) → up-move exhausted (v149 [21:15]–[21:46]) [ONCE; numbers are tape-read examples, not thresholds].
- A lot of supply "getting heavy up here" after a big rally → limited upside profit potential, fade it (v144 [30:36]) [ONCE].

## E. Absorption clues
- **Supportive buying / resistance selling** at a PPOC: market repeatedly returns to the level and reacts (cyan = buyers absorbing at support; magenta = sellers absorbing at resistance) (v144 [15:44]–[16:37]; v149 [03:43]) [REPEATED].
- At a swing high, re-test of the high is met by the **same resistance selling seen earlier** (look-left to where trapped traders/selling appeared) → absorption of the retest, high likely holds (v149 [04:07], [17:23]) [REPEATED].
- NEEDS-OPERATIONALIZATION: "absorption/supportive buying" is read from the tool's color, not a stated numeric rule.

## F. Stacked imbalance ideas
- Imbalance definition: aggressive buyers vs sellers (or sellers vs buyers) by a **4-to-1 ratio or more**; "four to one seems to be the industry standard … I stick to 400 [4:1]"; notes some use 1000 [10:1] (v144 [22:39]) [REPEATED — core threshold].
- **Stacked imbalance** = 3+ imbalances stacked one directly atop another (the shaded "zone") (v144 [23:05]) [REPEATED].
- **Multiple imbalance** = 3+ imbalances in a bar NOT necessarily stacked; he ADDED this because eyes/most software only catch stacked ones and you'd miss the trade (v144 [22:39], [30:36]–[31:07]; v149 [20:19]–[20:49]) [REPEATED].
- Key tiering point: relying only on stacked imbalances forces a worse entry — example, get long at 55 off the multiple-imbalance low vs waiting for the stacked imbalance at 75 = "20 ticks of extra profit you're giving up" (v144 [23:33]–[24:02]) [ONCE; numbers are illustrative].
- Example bar: ~45% of bar aggressive buying, reading imbalances "18 against 73, against 29, against 131, 0 against 31" on an 8-range chart (~9 price levels) (v144 [23:33]) [ONCE; tape example].

## G. Delta / delta-divergence ideas
- **Order flows divergence (basic)** = new/equal high with negative Delta, OR new/equal low with positive Delta (v144 [14:26], [15:19]) [REPEATED].
- **His differentiator**: divergence must also have **confirming price action** in the bar; without it "it's got a high chance of failing." Calls out competitors selling bare-divergence indicators for $500 (v144 [14:26]–[15:44], [41:07]) [REPEATED].
- Off a low he wants **positive Deltas stringing together and getting stronger**: "market's not going to move higher from the lows on negative Delta getting stronger; it's going to move off the lows on positive Delta getting stronger" (v149 [16:25]–[16:57]) [REPEATED — directional principle].
- **Declining/weakening negative Delta into a low** = aggressive sellers "lightening up / getting weaker" (e.g., −2200 → −1600 → −1010 then turns positive 78) precedes the turn; pair with multiple imbalances for the trade (v149 [08:23]–[09:25]) [ONCE; numbers illustrative].
- A divergence with **no ratio** is weaker ("just your regular number … 6.49, just order flow going through"); doesn't expect much of a move (v149 [06:11], [12:17]) [REPEATED].
- Confirming a swing low via **point of control behavior**: as market rallies, POCs print higher and price stops pulling all the way back to them (pulls back 1 tick, then 2–3 ticks through, then opens above) → "full head of steam," low confirmed (v149 [05:23]–[06:11]) [ONCE — useful confirmation heuristic].

## H. Failed breakout / breakdown / stop-run / liquidity-sweep ideas
- **Head fakes**: range trades 80–87/80–90 (Bund), finally "breaks out to the upside" above the range, sucks in longs, then sells off — "those are those head fakes before the market starts to sell off" (v149 [13:12]–[13:58]) [REPEATED concept].
- **Market sweep** through multiple levels just before a turn is itself a confirmation (sweep down then market sells off more; sweep up then rallies) (v144 [20:04]–[20:59]) [REPEATED within v144].
- **Failed divergence cascade** in a trending market: divergence after divergence all fail ("failed divergence, failed divergence … picking up a falling knife") — don't fade a trend on bare divergences (v149 [14:22], [15:39]) [REPEATED].

- Caveat: for trapped traders to exit there must be enough volume at the level (their size needs counterparties) (v144 [39:25]) [ONCE].

## J. Entry triggers (the exact "go" event)
- **First touch of a PPOC**: bid at a supportive (cyan) PPOC at a swing low; offer at a resistance (magenta) PPOC at a swing high (v144 [16:37]–[17:50]; v150 throughout) [REPEATED].
- **Divergence bar + PPOC + ratio**: short/long the bar that prints the divergence at the day high/low (v144 [41:29]; v149 [16:02]) [REPEATED].
- **Multiple/stacked imbalance bar at a swing extreme** = aggressive side entering; enter off the low/high (v144 [23:33]; v149 [14:47] "I love getting buy imbalances at lows / swing lows / lows of days") [REPEATED].
- **Second-chance / pullback entry**: if you miss the first touch, enter on the pullback to the same PPOC (or after the bar that confirms trapped traders) (v150 [07:29], [10:23]) [REPEATED].
- **Aggressive vs patient**: aggressive = enter immediately at the level; patient = wait for the pullback to the level with a tighter stop (v144 [28:34]; v150 [05:45], [10:23]) [REPEATED].

## L. Invalidation — what should NOT happen / what kills the thesis

- **Level breaks**: "if the support level breaks down you should be out"; "if you violate that high then the reason for being in the trade is not there" (v144 [17:03], [28:05]) [REPEATED].
- **Market goes against you immediately**: "if I'm getting short here and the market just goes against me immediately, I'm out — you don't stay in that trade" (v151 [04:23]) [REPEATED].

- **Bare divergence in a trending market** keeps failing — don't keep fading (v149 [14:22]) [REPEATED].

## R. Contradictions / nuances
- **Stacked vs multiple imbalance**: contradicts the common order-flow teaching to wait for the stacked-imbalance "zone" — he says that costs you the better entry; multiple imbalances (unstacked) are the edge most traders ignore (v144 [22:39], [30:36]) [REPEATED].
- **Divergence alone is not tradeable**: contradicts vendors selling bare divergence; in a trend bare divergences fail repeatedly ("falling knife") — only fade with ratio/PPOC/price-action confluence (v144 [14:55]; v149 [14:22]) [REPEATED].
- **Don't wait for confirmation (double bottom) BUT do require the move to confirm within 2–3 bars after entry** — nuance: he rejects waiting for chart-pattern confirmation pre-entry (you'd miss the low-risk price), yet demands fast post-entry confirmation or he's out (v144 [36:27]–[38:08] vs v151 [03:34]) — conditional, not contradictory.
- **Aggressive vs patient entry "it depends"**: sometimes price doesn't pull back so you must be aggressive; other times wait for the pullback with a tighter stop — depends on trader/plan (v144 [28:34]; v150 [10:23]) [REPEATED].
- **Stop size is conditional on chart/instrument**, not fixed: 2–4 ticks on range charts vs ~1 point on Nasdaq 15-range; "it depends on you and your trading plan / how much risk per trade" (v144 [17:27] vs v150 [12:33]; v147 [12:14]) [REPEATED].
- **PPOC "react every time" claim is hedged**: "it's beautiful when it happens like that, doesn't always happen that way … happens more often than not" — acknowledges failures (this one fails / doesn't work) (v144 [27:38]; v147 [06:47]) [REPEATED].
- **Profit potential after a multiple-buy-imbalance can be limited if the rally already happened** — he'd rather fade with a multiple-SELL imbalance than chase long after a big move (v144 [29:49]–[30:36]) [ONCE].
