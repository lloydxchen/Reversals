## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Divergence + Ratio** (bearish or bullish): Core daily go-to trade. Bearish = negative delta on a high bar + bearish ratio on next bar; bullish = positive delta on a low bar + bullish ratio on next bar. [REPEATED] (v71, [03:33]; v72, [03:29]; v73, [12:26]; v75, [03:28]; v77, [01:28])
- **Double bottom / double swing low with diminishing volume on second test**: Bullish reversal. Second test of a low with less volume than the first signals less interest in selling; "it's always always a key." (v71, [03:33]; v75, [01:46]) [REPEATED]
- **Point of Control at extreme (POC-on-extreme)**: POC printed at the high or low of a bar — strong signal that volume is stopping the move. Explicitly confirmed as bullish (POC at low) or bearish (POC at high). (v71, [05:48]; v73, [18:33]; v73, [19:28]; v77, [12:30]) [REPEATED]
- **Zero prints / single prints / gaps in the bar**: Treated like a gap — "trade them the way you would trade a normal gap." A pullback into the zero-print zone is a buying opportunity. (v71, [01:53]) [ONCE]
- **Three-in-a-row ratio trade (continuation)**: Three consecutive bars with bullish/bearish ratios, then a Red Bar pullback between two POCs is the entry zone. Bullish: three green bars with bullish ratios → fourth bar look for red bar pullback between two POCs → long. (v74, [12:05]; v77, [19:20]) [REPEATED]
- **Stacked buying imbalance at breakout**: Stacked buying imbalances occurring as price BREAKS OUT to new highs are treated more bullishly than stacked imbalances that form below the high. "When you're breaking out with aggressive buying stacked imbalances, to me it's stronger." (v74, [08:32]) [ONCE]
- **Multiple imbalances (non-stacked) in a bar**: Three buying or selling imbalances in a bar that are not technically stacked still carry significant weight. "We get so used to looking for stacked imbalances that sometimes we just overlook bars with multiple imbalances even though they're just as important." (v77, [10:29]) [ONCE]
- **Failed breakout / failed run at high with buying imbalances**: Market rallies toward a prior high with buying imbalances visible in the bar but cannot exceed the high; late buyers realize the breakout isn't coming and the market sells off sharply. (v73, [18:09]) [ONCE]
- **Delta Scalper signal**: A Delta-based indicator signal (buy/sell arrow) used as entry trigger or confluence. Discussed extensively but primarily as a tool; model-relevant rule = avoid signals on wrong-colored bars (buy on red candle = skip; sell on green candle = skip). (v71, [07:10]; v71, [07:40]; v72, [03:29]) [REPEATED]
- **Supportive-buying-driven rally to negative delta high (bearish)**: Price makes new highs but delta is negative because passive/supportive bids are holding price up while aggressive sellers are overrunning; once the supportive buying is exhausted, the selling takes over. Bearish reversal signal. (v77, [02:29]) [ONCE]
- **Ratio Bonds Low (stopping volume via ratio)**: A ratio printed at the low of a move, specifically called "ratio bonds low" — indicates stopping volume, a bullish sign. (v73, [18:33]; v77, [12:30]) [REPEATED]
- **Delta Surge at major highs**: Strong aggressive buying at highs (delta expanding to 144, 985, 1900, 2400 in successive bars) as price tests a failed-divergence high again — all signals say to buy, even into a high. Used to identify a trending/continuation move up. (v72, [07:45]) [ONCE]

## R. Contradictions / nuances (anything that conflicts with common belief, with his other statements, or that is conditional / "it depends")

- **Positive delta does NOT always mean bullish**: A high bar can close with positive delta because passive/supportive buyers are absorbing aggressive sellers. The net delta looks bullish, but the aggressive flow is bearish. He acknowledges this is a "synthetic divergence" requiring manual inspection. This is a direct nuance to the simple "positive delta = bullish" rule. (v73, [07:20]; v77, [02:29]) [ONCE]
- **Stacked imbalances: location relative to the extreme MATTERS for quality**: Stacked imbalances AT or BREAKING THROUGH the high = "I love it." Stacked imbalances below the high going into the high = "I don't like it as much." This is a quality nuance not explicitly stated with this level of specificity in the digest. (v74, [08:32]) [ONCE]
- **Multiple (non-stacked) imbalances = "just as important" as stacked**: He explicitly says practitioners over-focus on stacked imbalances and miss non-stacked 3-imbalance bars. This softens the stacked-only filter. (v77, [10:29]) [ONCE]
- **Candle color filter pre-dates the "new" Delta Scalper**: He implies the candle-color agreement rule is being baked INTO the updated Delta Scalper as a formal filter (v74, [00:49]). Previously it was presented as a manual override only.
- **After 3 losers, do NOT stop**: Contradicts a common trading-room rule. He explicitly rejects stopping after 3 consecutive losses if the method is sound and position size is appropriate. (v75, [12:41]) [ONCE]
- **15-minute footprint charts: Delta still valid, but ratio/divergence analysis is not**: "I don't like looking at footprint charts for 15-minute charts because I like ratios — you tend not to get ratio/divergence signals on longer-time bars... but delta itself is still valid." This is a chart-timeframe-specific applicability nuance for the indicator suite. (v77, [05:45]) [ONCE]
- **Win rate explicitly secondary to risk/reward**: He demonstrates scenarios with 50% or lower win rates producing profitable outcomes via 3:1–8:1 reward ratios. "Don't necessarily think of winning percentage unless you put it in context with what your winning trades are." (v74, [03:32]) [REPEATED]
- **FOMC / Fed testimony day = muted signals, smaller moves expected**: Even when all signals line up, price action during Fed testimony was muted ("just going sideways around 39 and a half"). He acknowledges the fundamental factor suppresses the signal effectiveness. (v73, [08:11]) [ONCE]
