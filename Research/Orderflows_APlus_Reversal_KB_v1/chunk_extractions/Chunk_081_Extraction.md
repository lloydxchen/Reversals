## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Absorption / stopping volume at swing low (bullish)**: Heavy volume prints at the low, point of control is prominent/bullish (cyan), aggressive selling absorbed by passive buyers; the setup is explicitly distinguished from a mere "pin bar." (v255, [43:25])
- **Stopping volume at swing high (bearish)**: Market pops to a level, prominent bearish POC (magenta) forms, volume is defending that price against continuation higher; triggers short entry. (v255, [42:12])
- **Bearish Prominent Point of Control** (named): POC where bearish order flow dominates; colored magenta by software. Distinct from neutral POC. Trades as resistance / short trigger. (v255, [40:14])
- **Bullish Prominent Point of Control** (named): POC with bullish absorption; colored cyan. Trades as support / long trigger. (v255, [43:25])
- **Abandoned Value Area** (named): Intrabar value area not retraced by the next bar; acts as a magnet / support-resistance level for subsequent pullbacks. Two sub-uses: (1) "go-with" trade after abandonment, (2) pullback-to-abandoned-VA entry. (v255, [48:37])
- **Delta divergence (price/delta)**: New high with negative delta = bearish signal. Explicitly shown on gold chart as combination trigger with bearish order flow reading. (v254, [39:37])
- **Trapped-trader setup**: Aggressive selling visible at a price level via footprint; market is 10+ ticks higher by the time the bar closes; those sellers are trapped and must cover. (v254, [34:54], v255, [38:09])
- **Multiple buying imbalances at swing low (bullish)**: Seven buying imbalances in a single bar off a swing low = edge; distinguishes from a plain green candle. (v255, [21:28])
- **Max/Min Delta internal bar analysis at turning points**: Reading the spread from Min Delta to close Delta (or Max Delta to close Delta) to gauge residual aggression; used at swing highs/lows to call turns or continuation. (v255, [26:23])

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Absorption at a low**: Passive buyers on the bid absorb all aggressive selling. "Someone's stepping up to the plate willing to buy everything that people are selling. Eventually, the selling's going to stop." The aggressive sellers exhaust; passive buyer is "strong." (v254, [13:23])
- **Stopping volume / absorption vs. price rejection**: "Price rejection would be the opposite — it'd be very thin volume. But this is heavy volume." Confusion of the two is a common trader mistake. The absorption scenario is fundamentally different: heavy volume at the extreme means defense, not weakness. (v255, [44:29])
- **Bearish prominent POC at HOD**: Passive sellers step in to absorb all the aggressive buying at the high; positive delta closes down = absorption signal. "You got passive sellers in there willing to step up and absorb all the buying pressure." (v254, [26:10])
- **Trapped sellers at a low**: Aggressive selling comes in; market is 10+ ticks higher → sellers are trapped → must cover → fuel for the up move. (v254, [34:54], v255, [38:09])
- **Abandoned value area as a magnet**: Buyers (or sellers) accepted value at a level; next bar abandons it, signaling value is transitioning. Market tends to return to that abandoned level later because "buyers' perception of the market has changed." (v255, [49:46])
- **Delta weakening into a high as pre-exhaustion read**: On the ES rally, Max Delta was +461 close was +22 → "gave back almost all the aggressive buying" → first legitimate sign of weakness before market turns. (v255, [32:06])
