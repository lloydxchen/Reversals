## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Abandoned / naked / virgin value area** (bullish or bearish depending on direction of gap). Price leaves a value area without the next bar retracing into it; the gap signals momentum and potential continuation. (v326, [01:56]; v331, [06:18])
- **Failed divergences / flow-driven market** (bullish bias). Repeated delta divergences that fail to produce meaningful pullbacks signal that larger, longer-horizon players ("smart money") are overriding the signal; the correct trade is to join the flow, not fade it. (v326, [05:48]; v326, [08:25])
- **Ratio + Divergence combo** (bullish or bearish). A bullish ratio (price exhaustion reading from the ratio indicator, ratio bounds low) coupled simultaneously with a delta divergence = "automatic trade." (v331, [04:49])
- **Absorption at HOD/LOD** (bearish at high, bullish at low). Resting liquidity or strong passive-bid/offer absorbs aggressive directional flow; bar closes against the aggressive side. Two sub-forms: (a) resting liquidity absorbing aggressive buying at high — bearish; (b) fresh large passive bids absorbing aggressive sellers as price rises — bullish. (v328, [00:00]; v328, [11:31])
- **Stopping volume** (potential reversal, direction context-dependent). Identified by heavy concentrated volume at an extreme; however, explicitly noted as frequently failing — market may absorb and continue. (v327, [09:31])
- **Sequencing / big-order detection** (directional, follow the order). A sequence of highlighted large prints on the same side working through multiple bars; do not trade against. (v332, [27:50])
- **Market weakness / aggressive-selling weakness** (bearish-to-bullish reversal). Directional aggressive trading is decreasing at a low or while market is selling off — a sign that selling pressure is exhausting; anticipate reversal. (v332, [14:46]; v332, [20:25])
- **Value area S/R (multi-bar)** (reversal or continuation at retest). Value areas formed on prior bars act as magnetic support/resistance when price returns to them; market "loves to trade around value." (v332, [35:07])
- **Stacked buying imbalance at low** (bullish reversal). Multiple stacked buying imbalances in the reversal bar or follow-through bars, combined with delta closing near max-delta and min-delta near zero — confirms aggressive buyers are in control. (v331, [16:14]; v331, [18:21])
- **"Power of three" value area gaps** (reversal warning). Three consecutive abandoned value areas in the same direction suggest the market is over-extended and has a tendency to pull back through all three. (v331, [12:31]) [ONCE]

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Abandoned value area (bullish):** Market leaves a zone where value was perceived, next bar doesn't reenter. Interpretation: buyers are unwilling to let price drift back; they are defending progress. Analogy: "the market is starting to pick up momentum." (v326, [01:56])
- **Flow-driven market / failed divergences:** Smart money ("other timeframe traders," "guys with deep pockets") are buyers who hold for weeks/months; their accumulation absorbs every attempted bearish divergence. Repeated small pullbacks (8 pts → 2 ticks → 6 ticks = shrinking pullbacks) = ammunition of shorts running out. (v326, [09:26]; v326, [11:06])
- **Absorption at HOD (bearish):** Resting liquidity at the high absorbed ALL aggressive buying (bar initially strongly positive delta, closed on low as red candle). Once the buyers were absorbed, there was no more buying to push price higher — only sellers remained. "The market is bluffing up here that it wants to go higher; the giveaway was what price was doing." (v328, [02:40]; v328, [04:16])
- **Fresh large passive bids at new highs (bullish):** Calculated institutional bids (working 28, 28.60, 29.30 etc.) at NEW highs — these are NOT FOMOing retail buyers lifting the offer; they are measured, multi-level bids. Result: market rallies on negative delta because supply coming in is absorbed by stronger passive demand. (v328, [07:26]; v330, [06:31])
- **Market can rally on aggressive selling:** Passive buyers absorbing aggressive sellers. Aggressive selling is not going down; it is being caught by passive bids. "Markets don't go up because there's more buyers than sellers… it's about passive vs. aggressive." Once aggressive sellers' "bullets run out," price rises. (v330, [07:06]; v330, [10:57])
- **Stacked buying imbalance at low (bullish reversal):** Aggressive traders "tripping over themselves" to get long; zero or near-zero min-delta = sellers never gained control intrabar; confirmation the reversal is genuine. (v331, [18:21])
