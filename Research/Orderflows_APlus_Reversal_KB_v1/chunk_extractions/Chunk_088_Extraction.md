## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Exhaustion print / single print at high of day (bearish)**: one lot (or very small volume) trading at the extreme of a move signals "the last buyer has bought." - **Delta divergence at high/low (bearish/bullish)**: at the HOD of soybeans (ZS), a large burst of positive delta (559) followed by declining / negative delta with no upside continuation, while the bar closes red = delta-bar divergence indicating supply absorbing buyers. (v304, "Commodity Markets", [05:21]–[06:00]) [REPEATED]

- **Delta Surge (bearish/bullish)**: three or more consecutive bars of growing-magnitude same-direction delta, with meaningful expansion between each bar, triggered after a directional move and away from the opening price. Entry via stop order in direction of surge. (v301, "Slow Day / Delta Surge", [02:17]–[15:25]) [REPEATED]

- **POC migration / cascading POC (bearish)**: three consecutive red bars each with a POC migrating lower and the POC of each bar landing below the low of the previous bar = strong bearish signal. - **Big-bid / big-ask absorption at support/resistance**: very large resting bids absorbed on pullback, tracked for retest. ## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Single print at HOD**: "that's the last buyer that's buying." One contract trading at the extreme with no follow-through = buyers are exhausted, no one willing to pay higher. (v298, "Viewer Comments", [08:32]–[08:59]) [REPEATED]

- **HOD re-test with candle-color agreement**: "I don't like taking bearish signals off green candles." Even if an exhaustion print appears, if the candle is still green the momentum is technically still up; bearish signal must align with a red candle. (v298, "Viewer Comments", [10:00]–[10:55]) [REPEATED]

- **Supply absorption in commodities**: At HOD on soybeans, the 177-lot volume at a price level = "people willing to sell it up here at this high … Supply coming in … there's people working limit orders up here … absorbing whatever buying is still" coming in. Passive limit sellers absorb aggressive buyers; price can't advance. (v304, "Commodity Markets", [05:32]–[06:31]) [REPEATED]

- **Big-bid / other-timeframe-participant story**: The 5,000-contract bid at 38.35–38.40 in ES is framed as "other time frame money, long-term money." These participants don't flip immediately on a 5-point adverse move; their stop is far away. A day trader should NOT fade them on DOM alone — instead, watch if the level holds on retest. If the big bid disappears on retest (small volume, aggressive sellers present), the long-term buyer is gone and price will "blow right through." (v305, "Big Buyers", [04:19]–[11:31]) [ONCE — detailed framework for reading DOM/big-bid levels]

- **Delta surge psychology (slow day)**: On slow / holiday days, markets rotate around the open; surges in that zone will stall. After a real directional move away from the open, a surge in the opposite direction signals the turn. (v301, "Slow Day", [04:10]–[04:34]) [REPEATED in principle; specific slow-day framing is new]

- **Commodity order flow**: Commercial hedgers and producers must trade; they can't exit freely like retail. This gives truer order flow signal in commodities than equities where money is merely "parked." (v304, "Commodity Markets", [11:32]–[12:05]) [ONCE]

## D. Exhaustion clues

- **Single print (1 lot) at HOD on ZN (10-year note)**: "that's the last buyer that's buying" — re-test of HOD, only 1 contract prints at the extreme, then immediate sell-off. (v298, "Viewer Comments", [08:32]–[08:59]) [REPEATED]

- **Zero at top of green candle**: The "zero" on the bid side at the top of a green (up) candle is normal (price never traded at that offer level) — this is NOT exhaustion by itself. Exhaustion is the TINY volume (e.g., 1 contract) at the extreme. The zero appears because the market never traded there, not because sellers stepped in. Important distinction: do not confuse a zero in a down bar's lower levels (unfinished auction) with exhaustion. (v298, "Viewer Comments", [12:56]–[16:58]) [ONCE — very precise clarification]

- **Unfinished auction vs. exhaustion**: Zero at the bottom of an offer side in a red candle = unfinished auction (market never offered there); this is NOT a sign the market should go lower — it's normal. Exhaustion is found at the TOP of green candles (or top of red candles) where aggressive buyers ran out. (v298, "Viewer Comments", [13:26]–[16:26]) [ONCE — precise model clarification]

- **Delta burst then silence**: Soybeans HOD: large delta spike (559 positive) then delta goes flat/small/negative with no upside follow-through = buying exhausted. (v304, "Commodity Markets", [04:54]–[05:32]) [REPEATED]

- **Zero at the bottom of a red bar is NOT exhaustion.** Common misconception: traders see a zero on the bid side at the bottom of a down bar and think "no sellers = exhaustion = buy."
