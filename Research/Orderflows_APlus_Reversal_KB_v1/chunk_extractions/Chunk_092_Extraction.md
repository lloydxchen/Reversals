## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Stopping volume** (bullish & bearish): large passive volume appearing at an extreme that arrests a directional move. First test trades heavy; second test trades thin — the asymmetry is the signal. (v333, [21:19])
- **Market exhaustion / exhaustion print**: near-zero volume at the top of a bar on a bullish move (or bottom on a bearish move) after a sustained trend, indicating buyers/sellers are spent. (v333, [25:11])
- **Out-of-balance / imbalance breakout**: multiple buying or selling imbalances (3+) in a bar that signals a market leaving one balance zone and seeking a new one. (v333, [30:21])
- **Absorption reversal**: strong negative (or positive) delta while price does NOT move in the direction of aggression; a passive trader is absorbing the flow. Signals potential reversal or continuation against the aggressors. (v334, [33:39])
- **Point-of-control-on-extreme**: POC sitting at the top of a red candle or bottom of a green candle (abnormal distribution). Mike describes this as "the simplest way to trade order flow." (v334, [29:24])
- **Delta-weakening divergence (trend top/bottom)**: price makes successive new highs (lows) while positive (negative) deltas shrink sequentially bar over bar. Reversal signal at swing high/low. (v334, [16:22])
- **Liquidity-driven trade**: passive supply (or demand) prints visible as large volume at the offer (bid) on a footprint during a directional move. Reading this as confirmation of directional sellers/buyers to stay with the trend or fade a bounce. (v334, [37:21])
- **Market weakness zone** (Orderflows Trader software output): automatically highlighted zones where buying or selling pressure is detected as fading; used as support/resistance and stop-placement level. (v333, [36:43])

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Stopping volume narrative**: institutional passive buyers (sellers) sit at an extreme with large bids (offers). Aggressive sellers (buyers) run into them and get absorbed. The first test prints heavy volume; the second test shows only a fraction — remaining trapped shorts (longs) recognize the wall, don't try again, and cover. Price reverses. (v333, [21:19] and [22:30])
- **Exhaustion narrative**: "Traders run out of energy." Buyers (sellers) have exhausted all their buying (selling) power. The last few contracts that print at the extreme (1–7 lots vs. 25,000–30,000 lot bars) indicate nobody else is willing to pay (sell) up there. Without more aggressive buyers, nothing moves price higher. (v333, [24:39])
- **Delta-weakening divergence narrative**: Market moving up on declining positive deltas (316 → 106 → 1) means each successive rally leg is driven by fewer aggressive buyers. "Who's going to take it higher? No one." Simultaneously negative deltas increase (−9 → −43 → −251), showing sellers getting more aggressive. Trapped longs who chased the rally must cover. (v334, [16:22])
- **Absorption reversal narrative**: Strong negative delta with price not moving lower means passive buyers are absorbing every aggressive seller. "Someone's absorbing it. If that absorption holds, market's going to go up." Trapped shorts who expected the move down to continue must cover. (v334, [33:39])
- **Passive supply liquidity narrative**: Large volume appearing at the offer during a bounce in a downtrend is a passive seller with supply ("they don't want to just come in and hit the bid"). This is NOT a reason to get long — it's a reason to get (or stay) short alongside the passive seller. (v334, [37:21])

## D. Exhaustion clues

## G. Delta / delta-divergence ideas

## R. Contradictions / nuances

- **Selling imbalances on green candles are BULLISH, not bearish**: "When you have the green candles with selling imbalances, that's actually a bullish sign. When you have red candle with selling imbalances, that's bearish." Candle color flips the meaning of an imbalance color. This is a direct nuance that contradicts a naive reading of imbalances. (v334, [25:01]) — [REPEATED, important]
- **Strong delta ≠ directional confirmation**: strong negative delta with price not moving down is actually a bullish absorption signal, not a bearish one. "You got to be able to make that distinction." The direction of the move trumps the direction of aggression. (v334, [33:39]) — [REPEATED]
- **POC-on-extreme is not always a reversal signal**: it is a reversal on a pullback, but if POC is at the bottom of a green candle at a new all-time high (fresh extreme of a rally), that is NOT a buy signal. Context (pullback vs. extension) completely changes the interpretation. (v334, [31:54]) — [ONCE]
- **Volume without context is insufficient**: "price could go down on light volume. It doesn't mean anything. But if price goes down AND the volume is migrating lower… probably it's going to keep going down." Volume matters in the context of POC direction (migrating), not just absolute magnitude. (v334, [49:21]) — [ONCE]
- **Passive sellers during a rally are NOT bearish for the rally**: selling imbalances on up-bars indicate passive sellers offering liquidity into a rising market — this is a sign of healthy upward momentum (buyers lifting offers), NOT topping. They only become bearish when they cluster at the extreme and block further progress. (v334, [23:36] and [25:01]) — [REPEATED]
- **Second-test stopping volume**: the "89 contracts on the second test" is a qualitative comparison (much less than the first test), not a fixed threshold. Each market's normal volume is different. NEEDS-OPERATIONALIZATION as a relative ratio. (v333, [22:30]) — [ONCE]
