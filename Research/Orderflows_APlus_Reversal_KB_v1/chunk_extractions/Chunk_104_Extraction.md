## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Ratio + divergence at a high**: new high means price accepted higher — but if delta is negative while price is making new high, the aggressive side (buyers) are NOT driving the move. Sellers are absorbing the buying. Price reverts. (v35, [06:25]) [REPEATED]
- **Failed divergences (grouped)**: 3+ failed divergences within ~30–40 minutes = "sign that the market could run higher." The repeated failed short signals mean buyers keep coming in, bumping the market higher each time it dips slightly. (v35, [07:12]) [REPEATED]
- **Single-tick follow-through failure at high**: aggressive buyers step in at a new high, but the market can only push one tick beyond before sellers overwhelm them — "you got the aggressive buyers coming in here, it couldn't take it any higher, took it one tick higher then it failed." Immediately followed by selling imbalances. Retail trapped long. (v35, [09:24]) [ONCE]
- **Strong negative delta at low with upward close (absorption/stopping volume)**: "Retail guys ... coming in that miss this move lower thinking hey we're going lower, going to hit limit down, sell it sell it ... but there's a strong commercial buyer here." Negative delta = sellers hitting bids into a large hidden buyer. Market doesn't break; price recovers. (v35, [16:45]) [ONCE]
- **"What doesn't happen is more important than what actually happens"**: when a setup that should produce a breakout/breakdown doesn't — that failure is the real signal. Great traders detect sentiment change via orderflow; losing traders miss it. (v68, [05:51]) [ONCE]
