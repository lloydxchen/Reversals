## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Delta exhaustion at high**: Market rallies with delta growing → positive delta weakens dramatically (drops ~80%+ in one bar, then near zero) → buyers have no more "bullets," took their foot off the accelerator → sellers take over → reversal. The key psychological narrative: "the higher prices attract more buyers until the last buyer has bought." (v251, [20:32]) [REPEATED]
- **Value area rejection at high**: Buyers and sellers both attracted to high price level (large volume, large value area forms) → close below value area = buyers rejected those high prices because passive supply was there → market retreats to prior balance level. Psychology: sellers had supply to offer at those prices, buyers realized they cannot push further. (v252, [17:02]) [REPEATED]
- **Order flow sequencing (directional)**: Large iceberg orders hidden at successive price levels → as market eats through each level (sequentially higher volume at each tick on the way up/down) → big trader intent revealed → "strong market" that will continue. Psychology: if a market can eat through heavy liquidity, the directional commitment is genuine. (v252, [27:24]) [ONCE]
- **Balance zone break**: Multiple overlapping value areas = trapped traders on both sides, no clear winner → first value area gap = one side winning, trend begins. (v252, [15:30]) [REPEATED]
- **DOM/resting liquidity as magnet, not wall**: Big size in the DOM is a magnet for big traders (they need size to fill their own orders), not necessarily a reversal barrier — market can eat straight through it. Only footprint-confirmed actual trades matter. (v252, [25:20]) [REPEATED]

## R. Contradictions / nuances

- **RSI/momentum overbought ≠ expensive/reversal signal**: explicitly states that "overbought means the price has gone up a lot" but does NOT mean the market will reverse — a market can stay overbought and keep rising. Valtos rejects using momentum oscillators as reversal triggers; value analysis (where price is relative to the bar's own value area) is the correct framework. This directly contradicts typical RSI-based reversal strategies. (v252, [35:19]) [ONCE explicit]
- **DOM/resting liquidity as magnet (not resistance)**: this chunk explicitly elaborates the mechanism: big traders are attracted to big DOM size because they NEED that liquidity to fill their own large orders. Thus big DOM = magnet, not wall. However, the same chunk also notes "sometimes it works sometimes it doesn't" with resting liquidity — acknowledging the strategy is not reliable. (v252, [25:20] and [23:15]) [REPEATED — reinforces digest nuance]
- **Iceberg orders mean DOM understates real size**: most orders above 10 contracts are icebergs; "just because you don't see any liquidity in here doesn't mean there's no liquidity here." The footprint chart reveals actual traded volume that the DOM hides. (v252, [23:47]) [ONCE explicit]
- **Order flow sequencing is BULLISH for the trend direction**: when a market eats through successively larger volume at each level = the move is STRONG and should be held/extended — this is a continuation signal, NOT an exhaustion/reversal signal. This is important nuance: large volume at price can be either absorption (reversal) or sequencing (continuation) depending on whether the market is eating through it (sequencing) vs stalling at it (absorption). (v252, [27:24]) [ONCE — key conceptual distinction]
- **Value area size alone is not the signal**: a large value area at a swing high is necessary but not sufficient — the bar must CLOSE BELOW its own value area (rejection) for the bearish signal. A large value area where price holds above it = bullish, not bearish. (v252, [17:28]) [ONCE]
- **"Same analysis applies everywhere"** — he claims value area analysis works on any market, any timeframe, any session (Asian, European, US). However, he also acknowledges Asian/European moves are "smaller" and the signals are "albeit smaller." (v251, [34:52] and [35:31]) — minor nuance on signal magnitude by session.
