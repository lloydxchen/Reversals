## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **SNR levels concept — underlying logic**: The indicator identifies bars where there is "strong selling" (resistance) or "strong buying" (support) in the volume/order flow. When price returns to that zone, the same participants who were active before are expected to defend it again. "The selling that was there is still there when you retest it." (v122, [23:19]) [REPEATED]

- **Resistance zone psychology**: Willing sellers accumulate offers at a price area where they previously sold aggressively. On retest, they re-defend. "If you know in the order flow they were strong sellers here, there might be resistance again." (v122, [23:19]) [REPEATED]

- **Support zone psychology**: Buyers who supported the market in a specific bar/zone are expected to re-bid when price returns. "If you know there's going to be support if you can make an educated guess with the information at hand." (v122, [32:58]) [REPEATED]

- **Why S/R flip works**: After a support zone breaks, those who were long get stopped out or puke; those sellers who broke it now defend the underside. "Failed support becomes resistance." (v125, [21:13]) [REPEATED]

- **ES cash-open reversal structure (v125)**: Market sold off → strong negative delta (last big push of selling) → delta weakens → positive delta bar appears → bullish ratio (price rejection) → buying imbalances form. The sequence tells the story: aggressive sellers exhaust, buyers absorb, aggressive buying emerges. "What's going on: you get the big push down, big last push of selling... then boom the buying comes in." (v125, [10:24]) [ONCE]

- **Stacked imbalance failure = trapped shorts**: When a stacked selling imbalance is immediately followed by a stacked buying imbalance, traders who went short on the stacked sell imbalance are now trapped. They "get killed" if they don't exit. (v125, [14:05]) [ONCE]

- **Why waiting for confirmation fails**: "Confirmation never comes sometimes." The trader who waits for a double bottom confirmation misses the actual move. Order flow lets you get in early because you already know where the support is. (v124, [04:12]) [ONCE]
