## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Ratio + divergence trade** — positive delta at a new/equal low (bullish) or negative delta at a new/equal high (bearish), combined with the order flow ratio (stopping volume) indicator. Described as "one of my bread and butter trades." (v229, "Strategic Order Flow," [33:14])
- **Absorption reversal at low** — negative delta in a green (bullish) candle = aggressive sellers meeting strong passive buyers; identified as the "first sign" of a reversal. (v229, [25:52])
- **Delta exhaustion / delta weakening into swing high or low** — buyers dropping off (decreasing positive delta) as market reaches a swing high; sellers disappearing (decreasing negative delta) as market reaches a swing low. (v230, "Strategies For Day Trading," [16:05]–[19:25])
- **Multiple-imbalance reversal** — bars with 3+ directional imbalances appearing at swing highs/lows; distinguished from stacked imbalances (same level). Explicitly described as a core signal. (v230, [33:22]–[36:59])
- **POC magnet / POC re-test entry** — price opens away from prior bar's POC, returns to test it, then reverses away from it. Short-risk entry used when POC acts as support/resistance. (v230, [29:39]–[31:41])
- **Point of control migration (trend-confirmation)** — successive bars showing POC migrating higher (bullish) or lower (bearish) used as early trend signal. (v230, [22:15]–[28:25])
- **Flow Breaks signal** — new indicator (v231); looks for heavier-than-normal aggressive volume in one direction followed by passive trading in the same direction ("volume breakout"). Bullish signal = heavy buy volume; bearish = heavy sell volume. (v231, "Time vs. Tick Based Charts," [00:28]–[04:08])
- **Big negative delta bar with no follow-through** — large negative delta bar (e.g., −1035) followed by multiple bars with small, shrinking deltas = aggressive selling exhausted; market is absorbing sellers. (v229, [23:47]–[25:20])

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Absorption reversal (low)**: Aggressive sellers sell into the bid. Strong passive buyers absorb all of it — they "are happy to sit here and bid." The negative delta on a green bar reveals this hidden buying. The absorption is the first sign; follow-through buying (large positive delta) is the confirmation. (v229, [25:52]–[26:21])
- **Delta exhaustion at swing high**: "Buyers drop off, drop off" as market grinds higher. Once buyers are depleted, "what's left is for the market to go back down." Sellers then step in with the strongest negative delta on the sequence. (v230, [18:20]–[18:52])
- **Delta exhaustion at swing low**: Large negative delta push down → sellers "took their foot off the gas" → small/vanishing negative deltas → first small positive deltas appear → market reverses. (v230, [16:05]–[17:08])
- **Big negative bar with no follow-through**: "This was it, this is the big push down, this is it — everyone's coming in selling. But then no follow-through in one, two, three, four, five, six bars." (v229, [24:19]) The trapped sellers are those who sold into the large negative delta bar and are now stuck as price holds.
- **POC magnet**: Volume at a price = fair value. Price always gravitates back to "where traders found a fair price." When bar opens away from prior POC, returns to test it, and is rejected, the previous bar's heavy participants defend that level. (v230, [31:41])
- **Multiple imbalances at extreme**: Market hits swing high with buying imbalances, then selling imbalances appear. "Where are your buying imbalances now?" — the trapped buyers from the top have no support; market falls. (v230, [33:22])
