## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Exhaustion/ratio setup at extremes (bullish & bearish):** A ratio that he "likes" at a swing high or low, combined with divergence, indicates price rejection (high) or price defense (low). (v36, [06:30]; v38, [02:13])
- **Ratio + Divergence combo:** Core signal pair. If divergence appears without ratio on the signal bar, look for ratio on the next bar. (v36, [10:06]; v37, [06:11]; v38, [08:50])
- **Stacked selling imbalance at high (bearish):** Multiple stacked selling imbalances at a swing high, combined with negative delta and a ratio. Called "high percentage trade" and "beautiful." (v36, [36:26], [38:20])
- **Three in a row (bearish/bullish):** Three consecutive bars with the same directional signal (bearish ratio or divergence). Strongly preferred off a high; expressly discouraged at the low after a large prior move. (v36, [25:55], [36:55])
- **Stopping volume / double bottom with less volume on retest:** Price retests a prior low with significantly less volume, signaling sellers are exhausted. Single print also present. (v36, [27:47], [28:16])
- **POC near top/bottom (bearish/bullish):** Point of control sitting near the bar extreme (top of bar = potential reversal down; bottom = support/reversal up). (v36, [10:06], [23:29]; v38, [12:42])
- **Stack buying imbalance at swing low / continuation (bullish):** Four stacked buying imbalances at a low indicating aggressive buyers stepping in. (v37, [06:11]; v38, [07:20])
- **Delta surge at low (bullish):** Delta picks up dramatically as price comes into a low, indicating buying momentum. Used by "Delta Surge" indicator (free tool). (v37, [06:40], [07:07]; v38, [07:20])
- **Delta Scalper signal with candle color confirmation:** Delta Scalper indicator fires a buy/sell; only acted on if the signal bar candle color matches the direction (green for buy, red for sell). (v36, [18:26], [19:32]; v38, [05:47])
- **Failed signal / "market defies you":** When price should sell off based on bearish signals and refuses to do so, it is a direct indication to exit; do not average in. (v36, [32:51], [33:21])
- **Overnight high/low breakout (bearish/bullish continuation):** Price probing prior overnight high or low; if it powers through with strong volume and imbalances, it is a real breakout. If it barely makes new extreme (1–2 ticks), it is a "half-hearted" breakout not worth trading. (v36, [23:29], [30:05])
- **Price Rejector signal:** Reads order flow for areas of price rejection; plots as a colored line. Can fire at highs (bearish) or lows (bullish). (v36, [20:43]; v38, [03:23])
- **Voltos U-Turn indicator signal:** Reads turning points; mentioned as firing at highs and lows. (v37, [21:56]; v38, [09:19], [10:55])
- **Transition indicator signal:** Delta-based trend indicator on bar chart (100-tick Dow in v37). Fires buy/sell at transitions. (v37, [21:56], [23:37])
- **5-yr / Bond inter-market confirmation (bullish):** When 5-year notes make new highs while bonds are far below their highs, bonds have "catchup" potential and can be entered long. (v37, [19:37], [20:49])

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Stopping volume at low → seller exhaustion → buyer absorption:** Price hits overnight low with some volume (e.g., 136 on bid); retests that low on dramatically less volume (e.g., "five lots"). Interpretation: "sellers are done, they got no more left to sell." Aggressive buyers appear via buying imbalances. (v36, [27:47]–[28:44]) [REPEATED]
- **Support-then-floodgates:** When significant volume has accumulated at a support level over multiple tests (e.g., 2,500 cumulative lots at CL 48.50), the last tranche fills. If it breaks, "the floodgates can open" because the support orders have all been taken. Stop-induced selling imbalance follows immediately. (v37, [09:15]–[09:48])
- **Exhausted sellers above trapped at prior resistance:** Prior resistance level (5-year 11809) has ~35,000 lots traded over days, acting first as resistance then flipping to support. Buyers who absorbed at that level provide support on retests. (v37, [16:00]–[18:26])
- **Strong positive delta but red bar = bearish divergence trap:** Large positive delta on a bar that closes red (e.g., 7,580 delta but bar closes down) is bearish — buyers tried but sellers overwhelmed them intrabar. Mike says this is "kind of bearish." (v37, [17:01]–[17:29]) [ONCE]
- **Delta weakening into low = absorption of sellers:**  Coming down with strong negative delta, then weakening to near-neutral (e.g., "minus 40") on the low bar signals selling is exhausting. Preferred pattern: each down bar's delta sequentially weaker. If this sequence is displaced by one bar (delta weakens one bar before the actual low), the reversal tends to be less clean (went sideways). (v36, [15:33]–[16:59])
- **At high: seller appears, absorbs aggressive buyers, then drives price down:** At ES/5yr highs, strong positive delta is followed by aggressive selling on the next bars, indicating the bid-side was supplied into, then supply overwhelmed demand. (v36, [35:56]–[36:26])

## G. Delta / delta-divergence ideas

- **Divergence (bullish):** Positive delta at a low (or new low) in a context where previous bars had negative delta. (v36, [10:06]; v37, [06:11])
- **"Blind divergence" warning:** Do not "buy every single blind divergence without any sort of confirmation." Divergence alone is not sufficient; wait for ratio in that bar or the next. (v36, [25:55]) [REPEATED]
- **Delta surge:** Sudden large increase in delta (buying or selling) as price comes into a key level. Free "Delta Surge" indicator. Bullish delta surge at a low = more conviction. (v37, [06:40], [07:07]; v38, [07:20])
- **Delta Scalper indicator:** Reads delta over recent bars and current bar, fires buy/sell. Only actionable when candle color matches direction (green for buy, red for sell). Mike did not include candle-color filter in the indicator code itself, leaving that judgment to the trader. (v36, [18:26]–[19:53]) [REPEATED]
- **Very strong positive delta / negative delta that does NOT produce price follow-through = bearish/bullish trap signal:** E.g., ES 10:00 bar — 39,000 contracts with strong positive delta and buying imbalances, but price goes sideways. Market interpreted as supply absorbing buyers until exhausted. (v36, [35:02]) [ONCE]
- **Sequential delta weakening pattern:** Sellers weakening bar-by-bar into a low (e.g., -2,200 → -1,206 → -40) is Mike's preferred pattern for a bullish reversal. A one-bar displacement (weakness appears one bar before the actual low) produces less clean follow-through. (v36, [15:33]–[16:59]) [ONCE, detailed]
- **Negative delta at a high following strong positive delta = distribution complete:** After buyers push price with strong positive delta, a strong negative delta on a subsequent up bar at the high signals distribution and impending reversal. (v36, [36:26])
