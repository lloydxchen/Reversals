## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

## D. Exhaustion clues

- **Delta deteriorating through a move's highs** — on the final bars into a high, delta weakens progressively: strong positive → weak positive → near-zero → then one big negative bar. This multi-bar sequence is the key exhaustion narrative. (v58, "June 14 2017 FOMC", [20:08]) [REPEATED]
- **Delta on final bar close near min/max delta** — "the bar had a big shift, from plus 5,000 to minus 8,000" and the negative 8,519 was close to the min delta of 8,638. Closeness of close-delta to min/max delta indicates exhaustion of one side. (v58, [12:47]) [ONCE]
- **Ratio bounds low (bullish) / ratio bounds high (bearish)** — ratio appearing at an extreme confirms price rejection/defense at that level. (v56, [19:04]; v57, [21:01]; v58, [06:47]) [REPEATED]
- **Buying (or selling) "drying up"** — a qualitative read: fewer contracts on the bid/offer side, imbalances weakening, delta numbers shrinking. Called "drying up." (v58, [31:42]) NEEDS-OPERATIONALIZATION [REPEATED]

## G. Delta / delta-divergence ideas

- **Delta increasing on a rally = continuation confirmation** — v57 ES: "delta increasing, you're coming down here delta's weakening, it's -2,000, -1,200, then it's +1,700, +2,000, +6,000. I know at this point buyers are coming into the market aggressively." (v57, [09:35]) [ONCE]
- **Divergence alone fails ~50% of the time** — "divergences fail more just about as often as they succeed" — the ratio adds the price-defense/rejection component that improves probability. (v58, [08:42]) [REPEATED]
- **Delta Surge usually follows Delta Scalper** — in the normal sequence Delta Scalper fires first, then Delta Surge appears; when both appear together in the same bar it is noted (v57, [23:27]) [ONCE]

## R. Contradictions / nuances

- **Stacked buying imbalance on a RED candle = bearish signal** — the typical expectation is that stacked buying imbalances are bullish. This instance is explicitly called bearish because selling came in at the end of the bar to push price lower, flipping the candle red. Contradicts the naive interpretation of buying imbalances as always bullish. (v58, [35:14]) [ONCE]
- **Divergence alone is nearly coin-flip** — "divergences fail more just about as often as they succeed." This is a significant calibration note: the ratio component is not optional decoration, it is what provides meaningful edge. A pure divergence system would not be reliable. (v58, [08:42]) [REPEATED]
- **Stacked imbalance at a high can fail even without a ratio** — crude oil example: stacked imbalance fired at a high but price came right back on the next bar; the ratio was absent. Confirms that stacked imbalance alone at highs is not sufficient. (v57, [13:10]) [ONCE]
- **Ratio + Divergence in consecutive bars (not same bar) is still tradeable** — "you don't always get it in that actual bar you had the Divergence… next bar you do have the ratio — to me that's still a trade." This nuance means the indicator should not require strict same-bar co-occurrence; a 2-bar window is valid. (v57, [06:15]) [REPEATED]
- **Big volume at a level can be bullish OR bearish depending on who is aggressive** — soybeans: "you've got a very bearish signal up here, but you've got a very bullish signal here — support coming in at 438." The same large volume at a price can be interpreted as demand absorbing supply (bullish) or supply absorbing demand (bearish) depending on the directional context. (v57, [15:15]) [ONCE]
- **DOM/bid showing on the order book is NOT a signal** — v56: "do I think this is a sweep in the sense that a buyer came and said hey, buy me 1,200 contracts? No. This is what a stop looks like." Large on-screen volume numbers at a price do not always mean institutional intent; stops can masquerade as institutional orders. (v56, [12:10]) [ONCE]
- **Currency markets: large volume at a price may be against a cash position** — in currencies, a large bid volume may be a hedge against a cash FX position, not a directional signal; "sometimes in the currencies I'm a bit hesitant because it could be against the cash position." (v58, [33:14]) [ONCE]
- **"Smallish" ratio numbers = reduced confidence, but not invalidation** — he doesn't say skip the trade, he says "be careful." The same setup with small numbers is still potentially valid, just lower-tier. (v58, [30:10]) [ONCE]
