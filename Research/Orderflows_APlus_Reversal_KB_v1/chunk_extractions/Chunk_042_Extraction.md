## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Ratio Bounce High / Price Rejection**: Traders no longer willing to pay high prices — buyers refuse to bid up. "If the sellers aren't interested in selling, what's going to happen? Buyers are going to take control. Markets rally." Conversely at a high: buyers stop showing interest, sellers cap. (v128, [19:53]) [REPEATED]

- **Stacked Imbalance Reversal story**: Aggressive sellers hit a high and overwhelm passive bids (selling imbalances stack). After a real move up, "for the market to go higher you need follow-through buying — if you don't see follow-through, that's not a good sign." Market hanging around after stacked imbalance = fail signal. (v132, [29:54]; [28:59]) [REPEATED]

- **Negative Delta + Support = Absorption**: Negative deltas in a low area but market not selling off = buyers absorbing all incoming sell aggression. "Someone is there absorbing all that selling... there's buyers willing to bid the market and the market's not selling off." This is absorption at work. Eventually sellers "run out of bullets / run out of ammunition" and price pops. (v132, [09:34]; [09:59]) [REPEATED]

- **Trapped Trader psychology (his nuanced view)**: He explicitly rejects the common "trapped trader" narrative of a forced stop-run. Instead, the signal tells him "there's no more interest [on that side]." Volume visible in the trapped bar is "enough for them to get out over the next couple bars." The real story is absence of follow-through, not a squeeze. (v132, [29:54]) [REPEATED — but with important nuance]

- **P-Shape = short covering, not new longs**: P-shape on volume profile = shorts covering after a big move, NOT new initiating long positions. The distinction matters: short-covering drives price but lacks new commitment. Look for whether new initiating buying follows the next day. (v131, [03:32]; [04:26]) [ONCE]

- **Supportive buying / resistant selling mid-move**: On a move up, bidders absorbing offers = "supportive buying" caps drawdown and sustains trend. On a move down, sellers capping rallies = "resistant selling." Both can appear inside moves, not just at extremes. (v128, [42:05]) [REPEATED]

## R. Contradictions / nuances

- **Trapped-trader signal ≠ forced squeeze**: He explicitly rejects the common interpretation. The green/red arrow (trapped trader indicator) does NOT mean those traders will be forced to cover and drive a spike. It means "no more interest" — they can and likely will exit quietly over the next 1–2 bars. The reversal thesis is based on exhaustion and absence of follow-through, not a stop-run. This contradicts how many retail educators frame trapped-trader setups. (v132, [29:54]) [REPEATED — key nuance]

- **Negative delta at a low is NOT automatically bearish**: Negative delta appearing at a swing low during a basing period = absorption, not weakness. The market not selling off despite negative delta is actually bullish. This directly nuances the raw "negative delta = bearish" reading. (v132, [09:34]) [REPEATED]

- **Stacked imbalances in context vs. out of context**: A stacked buying imbalance after a 50-cent rally is "not a great setup" — it could be a blow-off top. The same pattern after a 50-cent decline is "high percentage." The signal's tier is entirely determined by directional context, not by the pattern alone. (v132, [27:55]) [REPEATED]

- **3am signals are valid in form but low quality in outcome**: He acknowledges that a signal at 3am CT can be technically correct (price rejection, negative delta, swing high) but the outcome is typically small (5 ticks) and not worth the risk/attention.
