## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup
- Reversal-long story: at the low of day, aggressive buyers must overrun passive sellers; if fresh buyers don't keep stepping in, "the Market's not going to go any higher" and it goes sideways or makes a new low (v385, "Pitfalls Of Order Flow Delta", [04:18]). [REPEATED]
- Profit-taking vs fresh buying: a bounce off a low on weak/declining volume = old longs/shorts taking money off the table, NOT fresh sideline buyers; "it's not fresh buying or selling," so the market likely goes sideways rather than reverses (v387, "Identifying Chop", [04:40], [05:09], [05:42]). [REPEATED]
- Institutional supply-on-the-way-up: big sellers prefer to sell into a rising market where buy-side liquidity is, averaging their offers higher (95, 5 ticks higher, etc.); so big offer-side size on a rally is just supply distributing, not a special iceberg signal (v383, "Identifying Iceberg Orders", [06:18], [07:16], [08:40]). [REPEATED]
- Where an iceberg matters for reversals: a refreshing iceberg BID at new highs (demand) or a refreshing iceberg into the bid at a low = supportive absorption; an iceberg on the offer in a rising market "doesn't matter a little bit" (v383, "Identifying Iceberg Orders", [09:30], [12:36], [09:07]). [REPEATED]
- Two-way auction framing: markets go up when aggressive buyers overrun passive sellers, down when aggressive sellers clear out the bids and hit the next bid down; price falls because supply comes in, not because "aggressive sellers bought" (v382, "Aggressive Buyers...", [15:21], [16:01], [16:39]). [REPEATED]
- Chop/sideways story: after a big move the market stops, people get in/out at levels ("elevator" analogy); mixed order flow = nobody committing a fresh directional bet (v387, "Identifying Chop", [17:22], [17:53]). [ONCE]

## G. Delta / delta-divergence ideas

- Four Delta flavors he names: final/"headline" Delta; Max Delta (strongest positive point in the bar); Min/"M Delta" (always negative — strongest aggressive selling point in the bar); cumulative Delta (running daily total); plus Delta/volume ratio (v385, "Pitfalls Of Order Flow Delta", [01:23], [01:46], [02:13]). [REPEATED]
- Delta landing on zero = "more of a coincidence," not a signal (v385, "Pitfalls Of Order Flow Delta", [01:23]). [ONCE]
- Closing near Max Delta (positive) or near M Delta (negative) = strength held into the close = good; closing back near the zero line after a strong early Delta = weak/exhausting (v385, "Pitfalls Of Order Flow Delta", [10:12] "closing right on its Max Delta"; [18:36] "you'd like it to be closer to its Max Delta rather than to the zero line"). ## R. Contradictions / nuances

- Direction of Delta at lows is itself nuanced: he LIKES big positive Delta at lows, yet says a big NEGATIVE Delta at/into a low is normal and even tradeable if complimentary order flow follows — so "negative Delta at a low" is not automatically bearish (v385, [05:47], [06:49]). [REPEATED]
- "Can the market go lower/higher anyway? Yeah" — even a correct reversal read often gives only a few ticks against you first; being directionally right ≠ immediate payoff (v385, [09:37], [11:19]). [REPEATED]
- Icebergs: against common "guru" belief, big resting size / size-with-no-movement is usually just supply/absorption, not a meaningful iceberg; an iceberg on the offer in a rising market "doesn't matter," whereas a refreshing bid at highs does (v383, [08:40], [09:30], [13:00]). [REPEATED]

- He dislikes the word "chop" and reframes it as "sideways activity," and insists value/POC tools can flag it in real time — but also concedes "it takes a bit of experience" and won't reduce to a pure rule (v387, [02:18], [17:22]). [ONCE]
