## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

1. **Ratio + Divergence narrative**: Aggressive buyers declining at a low (positive delta shrinking or turning negative) = the buyers who pushed price down are exhausted; price defense or rejection kicks in (ratio); the last sellers have sold; reversal initiates. Same logic inverted at highs. (v83, [02:34], [11:27]) [REPEATED]

2. **Trapped trader narrative**: Traders go long near a high; market sells off; they are "long and wrong" and must exit their losing positions; their forced selling accelerates the move lower. "When Traders are trapped you got to look at what happens after Traders are trapped right — generally the market's going to come off." (v83, [13:23]) [REPEATED]

3. **Buyers stopping buying → turning sellers**: At a low, strong aggressive buying comes in (positive delta, buy imbalances); buying abruptly stops; then the same crowd turns sellers (sell imbalances appear in very next bars); the failed support attempt amplifies the next leg down. "People that were buying here on this way up are realizing hey this move is not happening — they turn sellers here." (v81, [14:09]) [REPEATED]

4. **Value-area low break narrative**: Two days of equal value area low creates a known support level; when it breaks, it triggers a cascade of sell orders from participants who held longs off that level — "you just naturally falls down." (v81, [05:55]) [REPEATED]

5. **Negative delta "stringing together" at highs**: Multiple consecutive bars of negative delta at a high, with price unable to extend, is bearish accumulation; eventually buyers give up and the move breaks. "From 246 to 338 all negative delta all the way... finally the selling is just too much, the buyers can't take it anymore, then you get your move." (v81, [16:27]) [REPEATED]

6. **Euro currency delta weakening sequence**: Strong positive delta on break to new high → weaker positive delta on further extension → near-neutral delta on final push → negative delta on turn bar → stronger negative delta on next bar = short trigger. Described as "simple simple stuff" — just reading delta intrabar sequence. (v83, [10:33]–[12:27]) [REPEATED]

## D. Exhaustion clues

1. **Delta declining into a high / strengthening into a low**: New high with weaker positive delta than prior bars = buyer exhaustion. New low with weaker negative delta than prior bars = seller exhaustion. Divergence arrow and/or ratio confirms. (v83, [11:27]) [REPEATED]

2. **Multiple consecutive negative delta bars (at high)**: Persistent negative delta while price holds near high = selling pressure accumulating but not yet breaking; exhaustion of buyers over time. (v81, [16:27]) [REPEATED]

3. **Failed divergence** (bounce attempt does not follow through): Divergence fires, small bounce, price makes new low — repeated 3+ times in ~1 hour = trend day, not exhaustion reversal (already in digest, confirming). (v81, [06:48]) [REPEATED]

4. **Ratio 27.5 near extreme**: Near-threshold ratio (27.5 vs. preferred ≥30) at a significant low alongside divergence still accepted as a mild exhaustion signal — "we'll call it as not a ratio but you get a ratio in the next bar zero." (v81, [16:04]) [ONCE]

## E. Absorption clues

1. **Strong buying at low fails to push price higher despite big positive delta**: Positive delta 4,000–5,000 in 5-years with price not advancing = supply absorbing demand; once buyers stop, the gap in demand causes price to fall. "You got positive delta 4,000, you should be trading 05 right now instead of 02." (v81, [14:09]) [REPEATED]

2. **Stacked buying imbalances at low quickly followed by stacked sell imbalances**: Buyers come in aggressively (buy imbalances 1,400, 5,000), then immediately sell imbalances appear (1,800, 1,400, 600) = buyers absorbed and turned sellers. (v81, [14:09]) [REPEATED]

3. **Ratio bounds low** = stopping volume / price defense at the low: heavy volume at the extreme defending the price, suggesting supply/demand exhaustion from the down side. Used as an exit trigger for short positions. (v83, [22:26]; v81, [22:26]) [REPEATED]

## G. Delta / delta-divergence ideas

1. **Neutral delta band**: "Less than 50 but you know in contracts like euro currency or contracts I trade less volume it's probably around 25." This extends/refines the known ±50 neutral band — in thin instruments (FX), neutral band narrows to approximately ±25. (v83, [11:00]) [ONCE]

2. **Delta divergence alone (no ratio)**: Lower-conviction setup; bounce occurs but is smaller. "You get a divergence, no ratio, it's just a black number" — smaller expected outcome vs ratio + divergence combo. (v81, [06:48]) [REPEATED]

3. **Sequential delta reading for trend reversal** (3-bar delta pattern): (1) Strong positive delta on new high; (2) weaker positive delta on marginally higher price; (3) near-neutral or negative delta on turn bar; (4) stronger negative delta next bar = progressive shift of control from bulls to bears, short trigger. (v83, [10:33]–[11:56]) [REPEATED]

4. **Bullish ratio immediately followed by bearish ratio = failure signal**: When you hold a position and the next bar prints an opposing-sign ratio, probability of follow-through drops significantly. "Don't stay in the trade — chances are it's not going to work out on you." (v83, [05:42]; v83, [26:41]) [REPEATED]

5. **Stringing negative delta over many bars at a high**: Sustained negative delta (v81 Bunds example: negative delta from 2:46 through 3:38 — nearly one hour) while price remains elevated is a slow-developing bearish exhaustion; not a sharp reversal but a grind.
