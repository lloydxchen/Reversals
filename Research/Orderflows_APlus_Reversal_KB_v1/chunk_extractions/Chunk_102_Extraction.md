## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup
- **Ratio+divergence (bullish):** market sells off to new lows; aggressive sellers are exhausting (exhaustion ratio prints) while aggressive buyers step in (positive delta) — "selling weakening, buying strengthening" → reversal up (v471, [04:20]–[04:49]). [REPEATED]
- **Bearish high-of-day:** a market in motion should keep going until aggressive buying weakens, aggressive selling enters, OR it hits exhaustion / price defense (v470/v471, [05:20]–[05:50]). Negative delta at the high = aggressive sellers have stepped in.
- **Crude abandoned POC/VA (v479):** in a sideways/rising market, a passive seller (or few) repeatedly works 25-lot offers to distribute; bids stay light (~2.5x heavier offers than bids); when consolidation breaks, value area + POC are abandoned and price cascades lower (v479, [05:46]–[10:29]).
- **Cascading stops:** "stops tripping over themselves, competing for the same bid... a queue system" — once the level breaks there's no liquidity, fills cascade far through the stop (v479, [14:29]–[15:00]; also v466 "stop cascades add fuel to the fire," [03:33]).
- **Trapped traders:** stopped out of positions; their stops cluster around certain areas and get wiped out, "which adds fuel to the fire" (v466, [05:00]–[05:27]). [REPEATED]
- **Pivot psychology (v470):** traders who see selling imbalances stay married short and ignore aggressive buying coming back in; the selling was likely just profit-taking from an earlier rally, not new aggressive selling — you must pivot bullish (v470, [06:34]–[09:17]).
- **Profit-taking vs new selling distinction:** selling imbalances after a long rally up = "people taking profits," not a reversal, unless other traders "sit up and start selling" with follow-through (v470, [05:05]–[09:17]).
