## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Value-area abandonment / abandoned value area (bullish or bearish)**: a bar's value area is not retraced into by the immediately following bar. Called "abandoned value area" — Mike explicitly prefers this term over "virgin" or "naked" value area. (v317, [09:22]; v319, [05:55]; v325, [02:51]) [REPEATED]
- **Engulfing value area**: a slightly more powerful version of the abandoned value area, carrying over the candlestick engulfing concept. (v317, [05:39]) [ONCE]
- **Gap in value areas**: a gap between two consecutive bars' value areas (e.g., value area high of bar N and value area low of bar N+1 do not overlap). Distinct from a price gap — it is a gap in the value-area rectangles. (v317, [08:52]; v325, [04:21]) [REPEATED]
- **Balance + gap + abandoned value area (three-condition trigger)**: the full triggering composite: (1) overlapping value areas across multiple bars indicating balance, (2) a gap in value areas to one side, and (3) an abandoned/virgin value area not yet retraced. All three together = "strong signal" / "ghost signal." (v317, [08:52]–[10:21]) [REPEATED]
- **Prominent POC on extreme (bearish/bullish)**: POC near the top of a red candle (bearish) or near the bottom of a green candle (bullish) = stopping volume signal; already known but re-applied to low-volume markets. (v318, [04:47]–[05:17]) [REPEATED]
- **Ratio + divergence (ratio-divergence)**: standard bullish/bearish ratios with delta divergence; applied equally to low-volume/alternative markets. (v318, [04:12]) [REPEATED]
- **Flow-driven market**: a market in which other-timeframe / institutional traders are dominating and a series of failed divergences is the clearest signal. Entry = stop order just beyond the most recent extreme after confirming 2–3 failed divergences in a short period. (v324, [15:17]–[19:00]) [REPEATED — but defined more explicitly here]
- **Big-offer / passive-supply absorption at lows (continuation short)**: big sellers working passive offers on the way down into a low (as opposed to hitting bids aggressively), seen as a sign supply is still present and the move will continue lower. (v322, [03:29]–[08:47]) [ONCE — specific mechanics not previously explicit]
- **Two consecutive abandoned value areas**: two abandoned (bullish or bearish) value areas back-to-back. Mike states this is "quite strong" and he has done tests on it. (v325, [11:17]–[11:46]) [ONCE — quantified strength claim]
- **Stacked buying imbalance / stacked selling imbalance**: re-applied repeatedly as a confirmation tool; "stack sell imbalance off high of day" as bearish signal (v323, [04:55]); used in combination with negative delta at high. [REPEATED]
- **Delta divergence at HOD/LOD**: new equal or near-equal high with negative delta — bearish; already known; multiple instances re-confirmed. (v323, [04:55]–[05:24]) [REPEATED]

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Abandoned value area**: "95 percent [of bars] get retraced back into the value area... a clear sign of rejection of a value area is when it doesn't trade back into it." (v325, [06:23]) The market is moving so directionally that value doesn't get revisited — trapped traders on the wrong side must eventually follow or be stopped out. [REPEATED]
- **Flow-driven market**: other-timeframe / "longer-term traders with deeper pockets" are actively selling (or buying) with conviction. Divergences that would normally work fail because these traders keep adding pressure. "When everything is telling you it's bullish but the market is selling off — pay attention to that." ## R. Contradictions / nuances

- **Big seller at a level ≠ short signal**: when a large offer (e.g., 4,000 contracts) is absorbed and price trades THROUGH it with stacked buying imbalances, the correct read is LONG, not short. "A lot of people say well there's four thousand traded there — we should sell off. But you see 4,000 got taken and then you see more aggressive buying." This directly contradicts a naive DOM-based "big seller = resistance" approach. (v323, [03:36]–[04:29]) [ONCE — explicitly stated as correcting a common misconception]
- **Neutral delta band stated as ±50 in e-mini**: "50 contracts is nothing in the S&Ps — it could easily flip from negative to positive." Reinforces known digest threshold but stated in the context of dismissing small delta readings mid-sequence. (v324, [14:40]) [REPEATED]
- **Stacked imbalance failure ≠ still waiting for the move**: two stacked sell imbalances at HOD that market keeps trading back into are NOT "still valid" — they are effectively invalidated after ~5 bars and the market is probably going higher. Don't keep waiting for the bearish move just because the imbalances are there. (v323, [06:58]–[07:25]) [REPEATED — but more sharply stated here]
- **Flow-driven day: divergences and other indicators should be discarded**: in a flow-driven market, "take out whatever other indicators you're using — you can just throw them out the window and go along for the ride." This contradicts the general confluence model (indicators add confidence) — on flow-driven days, single-direction execution with stop order is preferred. (v324, [18:34]–[19:00]) [ONCE — conditional override of the confluence model]
- **Value area gap is NOT a price gap**: "it's not a gap between the bars, it's a gap between the value areas." The distinction matters for implementation. (v317, [08:52]–[09:22]) [ONCE]
- **Passive offer at lows (Bunds) = bearish continuation, not exhaustion support**: six consecutive positive-delta bars at the low of the day with big offers coming in is NOT a "bullish absorption" signal — the positive delta is because sellers are working passive offers (not buyers absorbing). Result: market keeps going down. Contradicts the general rule that "positive delta at lows = bullish." (v322, [06:22]–[08:47]) [ONCE — important nuance: direction of passivity matters]
- **Stop-limit vs. market stop**: institutional stops may be stop-limit orders with a defined limit, meaning the market does NOT always spike far past the stop-trigger level. Stop-run analysis based on "market runs for the stops" may overstate the magnitude of the sweep. (v324, [02:51]–[03:18]) [ONCE]
