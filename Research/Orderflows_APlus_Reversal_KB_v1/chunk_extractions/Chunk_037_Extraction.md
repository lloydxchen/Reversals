## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Divergence at HOD — who is trapped**: Market makes a new high on negative delta (or sharply weakening max-to-close delta swing). Late buyers came in at the high; as delta flips, those buyers are trapped. "Everyone that was piling in here buying it... they're gonna sell... sooner rather than later." (v99, [12:19]) [REPEATED]

- **Trapped traders psychology detail**: "Trapped traders are more of the short-term scalper-type traders that are buying... they have very tight stops and as soon as they realize the trade is not working out they get out." Explicitly NOT buy-and-hold traders. Short-lived fuel, not a sustained squeeze. (v99, [13:09]) [ONCE — refines the "trapped quantity is SMALL" digest note]

- **Stopping volume story**: At a swing low, aggressive sellers hit the market hard (large negative min delta), but then buyers absorb and close near neutral or positive. "The selling the... it's not there anymore — instead you're seeing aggressive buying." Sellers are exhausted within the single bar. (v97, [27:26]; v96, [05:28]) [REPEATED]

- **Opening price as magnet on quiet days**: Sellers above the open meet buyers below the open; neither side wins. When a bearish signal appears just above the opening price and can't sell off, it confirms a directionless/rotational regime. "The fact that it's happening right above the opening price and you can't sell off, it's just telling me this market's directionless." (v98, [12:05]) [ONCE]

- **Failed divergences = hidden demand/supply**: Four failed divergences means buyers are so strong that each wave of aggressive selling is absorbed; the "sleeping giant" is coiling. (v97, [08:52]; [10:42]) [ONCE — extends known "failed divergence" note]

- **Bar color filter**: "I want the bar to be trading lower" before getting short on a divergence. A green (up-closing) bar with negative delta is NOT a divergence in his model. "The price action of the bar" must agree. (v97, [12:04]) [REPEATED]

## G. Delta / delta-divergence ideas

- **Max delta vs. final delta — the correct divergence read**: Divergence judgment must account for the full range (max delta to final delta), not just the final delta number. "You could analyze it and know it's weakening... even though it's positive delta." (v97, [16:25]) [REPEATED]

- **Bar color must agree with divergence direction**: Green/up-closing bar with negative delta is NOT a divergence signal; he explicitly ignores it. - **Delta near zero is not inherently meaningful**: "Don't get all excited — it's a bit random whether it ends up at 1, 0, minus 1... dependent on the volume in the bar." Final delta of 0 or 1 on a high-volume bar is noise; the move from min-to-max is the signal. (v96, [05:28]) [ONCE]

- **Failed divergences = trend-day flag**: "Three failed divergences in less than 2 hours, that's a sign [the trend continues]"; "four failed divergences already... a sleeping giant... watch out." 3–4 failures within ~2 hrs = switch from fading to playing the breakout. (v97, [08:52]; [09:23]) [REPEATED]

## R. Contradictions / nuances

- **"I don't add extra power when confluence stacks"**: Confluence of Delta Scalper + Price Rejector + divergence does NOT multiply signal strength in his model. "I don't put plus one plus one plus one... I've seen everything lined up and the trade fails." He likes it but doesn't score it arithmetically. This nuances the "confluence is a screen not arithmetic" digest entry — he explicitly rejects power-scaling. (v99, [08:40]) [ONCE — refines known nuance]

- **Bar color AND delta divergence required together**: Known digest: "candle-color agreement." This chunk clarifies: the bar-color rule is not just a bonus filter — it is a hard exclusion. Negative delta on a green/up-closing bar is NEVER a valid divergence signal in his model. (v97, [12:04]) [REPEATED — stronger statement than digest]

- **Divergence + good setup near opening price = lower-quality signal**: Digest notes "catalyst away from open" as a quality booster; this chunk extends it: a bearish setup that fires right above the opening price on a quiet day is likely to fail because "the open is a magnet." Proximity to opening price actively downgrades a setup, not just absence of it upgrades. (v98, [12:05]) [ONCE — extends known filter]

- **Trapped traders ≠ large fuel source**: Digest already captures "trapped quantity is SMALL." This chunk adds the mechanism: they are scalpers with tight stops who exit within bars/minutes, not position traders covering over 30 minutes. The move from trapped traders is therefore brief and should not be held as a full trend trade. (v99, [13:09]) [REPEATED — confirms + explains]

- **Delta zero or near-zero on a high-volume bar is NOISE**: Common belief might be that delta = 0 means "equilibrium" or is meaningful. He explicitly rejects this: "Don't get all excited — it's a bit random whether it ends up at 1, 0, minus 1." Only meaningful in relation to volume. (v96, [05:28]) [ONCE — anti-over-interpretation rule]

- **Failed divergence series in a downtrend (crude, v99)**: Three failed divergences during a downmove from 4860 to 4816 — he notes this looks like a potential buy but "I don't know how much of this move is left honestly." Failed divergences during an existing trend are weaker buy signals than failed divergences at the start of a new potential trend. Context matters — the known digest says 3 failures = trend day, but this case shows nuance: 3 failures WITHIN a downtrend do not clearly mean buy the exhaustion.
