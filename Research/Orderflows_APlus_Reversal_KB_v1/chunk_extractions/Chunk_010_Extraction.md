## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup
- Stacked imbalance = a burst of overly aggressive one-sided trading creates a **market-generated support/resistance**; market tends to come back and test it — question is whether it bounces or whether it was "just… a big order going through… get me out… buys 300 and just sweeps" (v339, [04:36]→[05:33]). [REPEATED]
- Sweep psychology: a fast 1-sided rip "where there's no counter trade… a bunch of zeros being left in its wake" = aggressive party with size, little opposition (v320, [03:29]→[04:01]). [ONCE]
- Inverse imbalance psychology: red candle with buying imbalance = **supply hitting buyers who sit on the bid to absorb**; green candle with selling imbalance = demand absorbing aggressive sellers; "people being caught off sides" / "trap Traders" (v340, [01:00], [02:34], [07:51], [08:57]). [REPEATED]
- Exhaustion at high/low: "the last seller has sold" — once only 1 contract is willing to trade the new low, "probably not gonna go any lower"; for highs, "where is the fresh buying… it's not there… the move got exhausted… nobody's interested" (v358, [07:29], [08:30]; v346, [04:17]). [REPEATED]
- Flow-driven market: longer-term / other-timeframe traders "buy and hold… buy and sit" come in and absorb intraday selling so price keeps rising on negative delta (v329, [07:35], [08:28], [10:33]). [REPEATED]
- On a 60-point rally, sellers appearing at the top = profit-taking ("books and profits"), not necessarily new shorts: "just get me out taking their profits" (v329, [16:13]→[17:16]). [ONCE]

## D. Exhaustion clues
- **Single contract (or very few: 1, 3, 4) traded at a new high/low of day** while surrounding bars trade heavy volume — "it's screaming at you hey there's no interest up here" (v346, [03:46]→[04:17]; v358, [03:24], [07:29]). [REPEATED]
- Pattern: make high → come off → run back to high on **diminishing volume** (3, then later 4 contracts) → sells off; repeats (v346, [05:15], [09:04]). [REPEATED]
- Exhaustion at top of **red candles** = bearish; exhaustion off the low in **bullish** bars = bullish (v346, [07:31]). [ONCE]
- Orderflows Trader "**exhaustion prints**" setting highlights these low-volume bars; he sets the threshold to 1 (liquid), or 5 / 9 on bigger contracts (he uses 9 in recipes) (v346, [07:31], [08:28]; v358, [02:51], [10:58]). [REPEATED]
- Strongly market-dependent: common in interest rates (10yr, ultra bonds), gold, occasionally crude; rare in ES — "I can't remember the last time I saw… high of the day low of the day where it traded just one contract" in e-minis (v358, [09:31]→[10:05]). [REPEATED]

## F. Stacked imbalance ideas
- Definition: **3+ imbalances stacked neatly on top of each other**, same side (v320, [01:26]; v339, [02:40]). [REPEATED]
- Imbalance = aggressive buy vs sell in two-way auction beyond a ratio: standard **4:1 (400%)**; others use 3:1, 3.5:1, 5:1, **10:1 (1000%)** (v320, [00:53], [02:22]; v339, [02:14]). Higher ratio = fewer imbalances, fewer stacks (v320, [02:51]). [REPEATED]
- Footprint color: **blue = buying imbalance, red = selling imbalance**, black = normal (below threshold) (v320, [00:53]; v339, [01:19], [01:44]). [REPEATED]
- Practical drawing: in Orderflows Trader set stacked imbalance to "**until tested**" to auto-draw a green (buy) / red (sell) zone; "let the computer do it for you" (v339, [05:58]→[06:21]). [REPEATED]
- He relaxes the "3" minimum when using a higher ratio: at 10:1 he uses **minimum of 2** stacked (sometimes 1 on thin markets like bonds/10yr, but 1 "not probably going to be very effective" on e-minis) (v320, [09:04], [09:33]). [ONCE]
- Trade only the ones where price **moves away then comes back to test**, not ones where the next bar immediately trades back in (v320, [10:05]). [REPEATED]
- If price does **not** rotate back into a stacked imbalance within a certain time → sign of a **trending / strong directional** market (v339, [07:56]). [ONCE]
- Best used **immediately** as it forms (sooner = better entry); consider a 30-second chart to catch the zone earlier; 1-min fine for most (v339, [08:28]→[10:56]). [REPEATED]
- A bar with **multiple imbalances (5–6) while taking out a high** = momentum confirmation on a breakout (v329, [04:56], [11:44]). [REPEATED]

## G. Delta / delta-divergence ideas

- He stacks/counts divergences: multiple **failed** divergences (price won't sell off despite negative-delta highs) flips read to bullish/absorption — "every five ten minutes we're getting a Divergence… and the market just keeps on trucking" (v329, [10:33], [11:44]). [REPEATED]
- **Bearish ratio + divergence on the same bar** = "automatic trade… get short" (top trigger) (v329, [06:34]→[07:04]). [ONCE]

## H. Failed breakout / breakdown / stop-run / liquidity-sweep ideas
- **Sweep** = fast one-directional move with **no counter-trade, zeros left behind** (e.g., 0 vs 70, 0 vs 45); 200 contracts shouldn't move e-minis "unless there's no liquidity" (v320, [03:29]→[04:28]). [REPEATED]
- A round level (4000 ES) that the market reaches on **light volume (35 contracts), nobody defending** = weak; "nobody's really up there really defending it" — later it gets cleared easily (v329, [02:08]→[04:29]). [ONCE]
- **Abandoned value area** = a value area price moves away from and does not return to; bullish when price leaves it on a strong up-bar and doesn't come back (you don't know it's abandoned "until this next bar closes") (v329, [04:29], [11:44], [17:46]). [REPEATED]
- Failed divergences (above) are the chunk's main "failed-breakdown of the bearish thesis" pattern. [REPEATED]

## I. Trapped-trader ideas
- Inverse imbalance = trapped traders caught off sides; e.g., NQ "12 against one, four against 19… these guys got trapped in here" (v340, [07:51]→[08:29]). [REPEATED]
- Crucial nuance: trapped-trader **count is usually tiny and not the mechanical cause of the move** — "it's only 31 contracts… not 350… this bar traded 1800 contracts." It's a **shift signal**, not a squeeze-fuel count (v340, [08:57]→[09:27]). [ONCE] — flag against the common "trapped traders must cover → big move" belief.

## J. Entry triggers (the exact "go" event)
- **Reversal Scalper:** the **triangle prints** = the actual signal (NOT the colored validity bar). Without trade-entry-signal it prints when the bar after the validity bar closes; with trade-entry-signal it fires intrabar as soon as price follows through (v321, [54:41]→[55:19], [41:36], [42:14]). [REPEATED]
- **Trade Entry Signal** filter: requires follow-through order flow — he uses **"2 and 2" = price must move 2 ticks over the next 2 bars** (bullish: above the high; bearish: below the low). Also tunable (1&1, "2 ticks over next 5 bars"); 2&2 "works best for me" (v321, [08:32]→[09:41], [35:03], [36:13]). [REPEATED]
- **Stacked imbalance entry:** put a bid (longs) / sell at the drawn zone when price returns; or buy-stop above the trigger; example one-tick stop fills (v320, [06:32], [10:05]). [REPEATED]
- **Inverse imbalance entry:** drop a **buy stop a tick or two above the high** of the inverse-imbalance bar and "let the market take you in"; requires next bar to trade **away** (lower for a bullish inverse / not back into it) (v340, [05:09]→[05:31]). [REPEATED]
- **Exhaustion / single-print entry:** the single-contract print at the high/low is the "go sign"; combine with aggressive buying + bullish PoC + bullish ratio for confirmation (v358, [07:57]→[08:30]). [REPEATED]
- **Ratio + divergence:** bearish ratio on a divergence bar = immediate short (v329, [06:34]). [ONCE]

## K. Confirmation — what SHOULD happen quickly after entry/trigger
- Price should **move 2 ticks within 2 bars** in your direction (Reversal Scalper trade-entry confirmation) (v321, [08:32]). [REPEATED]
- For inverse imbalance: the **next bar should head away** from the level (lower for a bullish setup) and then run; if it opens mid-range and trades back up into the bar, no go (v340, [04:13], [05:09], [09:58]). [REPEATED]
- For stacked imbalance: market should come back, **bounce off the zone** (e.g., "three point bounce" 63→66¼) and hold above (v339, [06:21]→[06:52]). [ONCE]
- On a breakout you "like to see that **momentum pick up**" via buying imbalances + zero prints + abandoned value (v329, [04:56], [13:24]). [REPEATED]
- General reversal logic: you should be able to tell quickly if you're wrong — reversals let you "discern that you're wrong pretty quick" (v321, [02:33]). [REPEATED]

## L. Invalidation — what should NOT happen / what kills the thesis
- **Reversal Scalper / validity bar:** if a bullish signal's validity bar is **violated (price trades below its low)**, "get out of the trade immediately"; bearish = trades above the high (v321, [52:52]→[53:26]). [REPEATED]
- **Inverse imbalance invalid** if next bar trades sideways-to-higher / back into the bar instead of away (v340, [06:55], [09:58], [10:23]). [REPEATED]
- **Time risk:** if price just goes sideways and won't move after the signal (one, two, three chances, more signals), **cut it — don't wait to be stopped**: "at some point you got to say are we going to make this move or not" (v321, [27:46]→[29:21]). [REPEATED]
- A reversal/exit thesis appears when contradictory order flow shows up, e.g., a **bullish Prominent Point of Control** after a move down = sign to exit a short (v321, [45:25]). [ONCE]
- Stacked imbalance that gets traded straight through next bar = was likely just a big order, not real S/R (v339, [05:08], [06:21]). [REPEATED]
