## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup
- Reversal-at-high story: at the high, FOMO buyers pile in ("FOMO baby FOMO"), but the last buyer has bought; aggressive buyers can't push higher on retests, buying volume "dries up" → market must roll over because "if no one is buying at high prices... it's gonna sell off" (v173, "...Exhaustion", [34:45]–[38:28]). [REPEATED]
- Reversal-at-low story: sellers exhausted, "the last seller has sold," very little selling interest remains at the low → buying outnumbers selling, market rises (v173, "...Exhaustion", [32:51]–[35:39]). [REPEATED]
- Trapped/exhausted at high: traders "exhausted from buying these highs thinking we're going higher," then market sells off (v173, "...Exhaustion", [42:19]). [ONCE]
- Big traders deliberately HIDE aggression: they sell a piece, "let the market breathe," sell more at a better price — produces *multiple* (not stacked) imbalances; that's why execution algos exist (v173, "...Exhaustion", [21:23]–[26:41]). [REPEATED]
- Passive vs aggressive framing (car-dealer, fisherman, seesaw analogies): markets only move when someone is aggressive (lifts offer / hits bid); imbalance = aggression of one side outweighs the other (v172, [05:52]–[07:16]; v173, [05:23]–[09:54]). [REPEATED]

## D. Exhaustion clues
- **Small prints** = very low volume traded at the extreme of a move/bar — the core exhaustion signal (v173, "...Exhaustion", [33:48]–[35:39]). [REPEATED] Thresholds are market-relative (see §O).
- **Decreasing volume on each retest of the high/low** — each touch trades less than the last (e.g., 2 → 4 → 3 contracts on Nasdaq highs) (v173, "...Exhaustion", [37:34]). [REPEATED]
- Must be paired with a **reversal candle**: red candle at a reversal high, green candle at a reversal low (v173, "...Exhaustion", [34:17]–[34:45]). [REPEATED]
- **No follow-through in the next bar** at the extreme — prior bar traded 114 at a level, next bar only 3, "immediately comes off"; on minute charts "always... be looking for follow-through in the next bar" (v173, "...Exhaustion", [44:31]–[45:07]). [REPEATED]
- Prices being **rejected** at a level on repeated tries = exhaustion zone; "don't expect the market to come back to those right away" (v173, "...Exhaustion", [40:31]–[42:19]). [ONCE]
