## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Ratio + Divergence** ("one of my bread-and-butter trades"): new/equal high with negative delta OR new/equal low with positive delta, PLUS price action confirmation AND a bearish/bullish ratio. Picks highs/lows of the day. (v153, "What You Need To Know," [43:27]; v155, Weekly Training #2, [11:00])
- **Prominent POC + Divergence** (newer combo setup): prominent/primary point of control acting as support or resistance combined with divergence. Described as a distinct named setup. (v153, [44:44]; v155, [13:49])
- **Multiple Imbalances** (named feature/setup): 3+ imbalances in a single bar, NOT necessarily stacked on top of each other. Fires earlier than a stacked imbalance setup. Indicated by a magenta box around the bar. Bullish/bearish depending on context. (v153, [20:50], [21:18])
- **Stacked Imbalances** (existing): still used; neat and "pretty" but sometimes does not appear when multiple imbalances does. (v153, [31:42])
- **Market Sweeps** (named feature): big traders sweep through multiple price levels in one aggressive order. Often precedes a directional move. Bearish sweep at high = short opportunity; bullish sweep at low = long. (v153, [17:37], [19:22])
- **Trap Traders** (named feature): traders long and wrong (late buyers at high) or short in the hole (late sellers at low) who must eventually exit. Market tends to continue against trapped traders. (v153, [36:35])
- **Single Prints** (named feature, price rejection): single-digit volume at the top of a red bar or bottom of a green bar. Interpreted as price rejection (not "exhaustion"). Most effective at swing highs or swing lows. In the middle of a move, indicates weak counter-trend participation, confirming the move. (v155, [26:10], [27:12], [28:23])
- **Prominent POC as support/resistance** (standalone): primary/prominent POC colored to indicate support or resistance; market often reacts at these levels. Double bottom or test of prominent POC = entry opportunity. (v153, [16:21], [28:15])
- **Sore Thumb Trade** (named concept, bullish or bearish): abnormally large volume at a single price level that sticks out relative to the rest of the bar. At a low = support / buying signal; at a high = resistance / selling signal. (v153, [26:38])

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Multiple imbalances at a low**: "big buyers it could be one it could be multiple people coming in here thinking this is a low price and they're being aggressive they're tripping over each other in their aggressiveness buying it sort of skipping different price levels" — represents genuine commitment vs intention (DOM). - **Trap traders**: late buyers who get long near a high "thinking the reasons for this rally are still valid" — when wrong, market continues against them forcing exits. "The market will continue in that direction when they're wrong." (v153, [36:35]) [REPEATED in prior chunks]
- **Market sweeps psychology**: institutional traders only care about average price, not individual fills. "If you want to get them on as fast as possible you're gonna sweep the market." - **DOM vs volume commitment**: "Unfilled orders indicated traders' intention; filled orders indicate a trader's commitment." DOM bids/offers are pulled as market approaches; actual traded volume is the real information. (v153, [24:46], [25:25]) [REPEATED concept, clearer articulation]
- **Sore thumb volume**: 3,000 contracts on the bid at a low vs everywhere else 500–700 — "sticks out like a sore thumb" — tells you a big committed buyer was present at that level. (v153, [25:48], [26:38]) [ONCE]

## G. Delta / delta-divergence ideas
