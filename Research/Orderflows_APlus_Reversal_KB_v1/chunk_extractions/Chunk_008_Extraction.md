## A. Setup types & names he uses
- **Inverse imbalance** — HIS COINED TERM. A buying imbalance appearing at/just-off a swing high (bearish) or selling imbalance at/just-off a swing low (bullish); = traders caught offside. "a term for this that I've coined many years ago it's called an inverse imbalance" (v286, "Inverse Imbalances", [02:57]). [REPEATED]
- **Imbalance reversal** — a tool/setup in Orderflows Trader: selling imbalances in green up candles, or buying imbalances in red down candles, as a sign of supply/support (absorption) coming in (v289, "Different Types Of Traders", [07:26]). [REPEATED]
- **Stacked imbalance** — three or more imbalances stacked neatly on top of each other (same direction) in one bar; bullish (buy) or bearish (sell) (v286 [02:57]; v299 "Imbalance Trading Strategies" [09:24]; v300 "Multiple Imbalances" [01:14]). [REPEATED]
- **Multiple imbalances** — HIS TERM: three or more imbalances in a bar that are NOT necessarily stacked neatly (spread out); same bullish/bearish meaning (v300, "Multiple Imbalances", [01:14], [02:16]). [REPEATED]
- **Absorption-at-low / low-of-the-day setup** — green candle(s) with negative delta + cumulative-delta-vs-price divergence at a tested low → buy off the low (v296, "Cumulative Delta To Determine The Low", [04:41]–[07:53]). [REPEATED]
- **Point-of-control (POC) migration setup** — trend/continuation read, not a reversal trigger per se: POC migrating to bottoms of bars = acceptance lower (bearish continuation); POC near highs of bars at a high = supply (v282, "Volume Migrating Lower", [01:30], [02:57]). [REPEATED]

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup
- **Inverse imbalance (offside traders):** early in the bar aggressive buyers FOMO into a swing high ("jumping over themselves fomoing"), then "conditions change," rug gets pulled, aggressive selling hits the bottom half of the same candle → those late buyers are now long-and-wrong (v286 [04:20], [05:17]). [REPEATED]
- **Absorption at a low (bullish):** sellers keep hitting bids (strong negative delta) but a passive buyer with "deep pockets" steps in to absorb; price drifts up while cumulative delta still falls → shorts will have to cover, buyers add aggressively (v296 [05:13], [06:41], [14:02]). [REPEATED]
- **Buy imbalance in red candle = supply/distribution:** people who are short up higher start to cover OR dip-buyers buy the quick drop, but their buying "is being absorbed by passive sellers" who'd "rather sell as much as I can up here" → market keeps dropping (v283 [04:55]; v289 [07:56]; v299 [05:43]). [REPEATED]
- **Sell imbalance in green candle = supportive/passive buying:** buyers "stepping up essentially to support this market from dropping further," absorbing aggressive selling (v289 [05:22]; v299 [07:44], [10:14]). [REPEATED]
- **Resting-liquidity break (dome traders trapped):** big resting bid (e.g. 1500 lots) attracts dome traders to "jump in front of it" and place stops just below; as the bid trades out (1000→900→500→below ~300) it can be whacked, stops trigger, "one-way runaway train," no counter-trade, and price often does NOT retrace back to it soon (v282 [10:42]–[13:02]). Drawn from his JP Morgan experience scaling out sells. [REPEATED]

## E. Absorption clues

## F. Stacked imbalance ideas

## R. Contradictions / nuances
- **Imbalances are intentionally dual-meaning:** the SAME imbalance can mean aggression (continuation) OR absorption (reversal) depending on candle color and location — "imbalances have... dual meanings" (v289 [11:34]; v299 [02:36]). The model must read context, not the imbalance alone.
- **"Don't take it on face value":** sell imbalances on green candles / buy imbalances on red candles are explicitly NOT a naive "fade it" signal — could be absorption supporting continuation (v289 [05:22]). Conditional/"it depends."
- **Stacked imbalance at resistance is NOT automatically a stop:** "if I've got very strong aggressive buying coming in... I'm already thinking we can easily get past this area" rather than blindly selling resistance (v299 [14:47]). Conflicts with textbook S/R fading.
- **Range vs time bars change meaning:** a stacked imbalance on a 1-min TIME bar implies sustained pressure, but on a range bar "someone could sweep right through three price levels and you form another bar instantly" — so the same pattern is weaker on range charts (v299 [11:44], [12:10]). Nuance vs his own 30-sec/4-range chart use.
- **Setup frequency is regime/instrument-dependent:** inverse imbalances "not something that happens every day," more on volatile days, and basically absent in deep markets like bonds (he scanned bonds live and "I'm not seeing any") (v286 [06:32], [11:49], [12:23]).
- **He rejects prediction framing repeatedly** ("order flow is not about predicting where the low is going to come into place") yet the videos are titled "...Determine The Low Of The Day" — the model is reactive/confirmational, not predictive (v296 [01:02], [12:35]).
- **Anti-"trapped trader" stance** runs against common order-flow marketing; he insists on volume magnitude and prefers "stuck/offside" (v286 [00:00]; v289 [06:49]).
