## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup
- **Stacked/multiple imbalance:** aggressive buyers (or sellers) repeatedly hitting the offer (or bid) by 4:1+; signals real aggression and that "there's more to this move" (added insurance when already in) (v145, "Stacked Imbalances", [08:36]). [REPEATED]
- **Price rejection (top):** buyers collectively decide price is "too expensive," stop buying; last buyer has bought → no more upside → market reverses down. Analogies: winter-jacket seller lowering price, jewelry/diamond ring too expensive, watermelon at Walmart vs Meijer, baseball cards (v158, "Price Rejection Webinar", [07:29], [07:55], [14:35], [21:20]). [REPEATED]
- **Price rejection (bottom):** sellers decide price is "too cheap," stop selling; paper-towel-under-faucet analogy — when the selling "stream" turns to "drip drip drip," the level holds and market rallies (v158, "Price Rejection Webinar", [08:53], [09:21]). [REPEATED]
- **Trapped trader:** aggressors buy the new high (or sell the new low) by a couple ticks ("premature ejaculation… these guys are trapped"); market immediately comes off, trapping them, forcing the reverse move (v146, "Prominent POCs Trapped Trader", [05:33]). [ONCE]
- **"Last buyer / last seller" doctrine** (credited to Tom DeMark): "when the last buyer has bought the market's not gonna go higher anymore; when the last seller has sold it's not gonna sell off anymore" (v158, "Price Rejection Webinar", [25:18]). [ONCE]
- **Support→resistance flip:** after a stacked sell imbalance off a high, prior support "becomes resistance" and is "blown out of the water" (v145, "Stacked Imbalances", [06:14], [06:43]). [ONCE]

## D. Exhaustion clues
- **Single print (0–9 contracts)** at the extreme of a bar = buyers/sellers "dried up"; he and Tom DeMark call these areas "price exhaustion"; other vendors later named it "single print exhaustion" (v158, "Price Rejection Webinar", [25:18], [35:12], [34:40]). [REPEATED]
- **Buying drying up by steps:** "50 contracts bought on the offer, then 20, then 10, then 5, then that's it" — last buyer has bought, market reverses (qualitative sequence, illustrative numbers; NEEDS-OPERATIONALIZATION) (v158, "Price Rejection Webinar", [11:12]). [ONCE]
- **Negative deltas stacking at a high** while price grinds up = exhaustion warning before a stacked sell imbalance off the high (v145, "Stacked Imbalances", [09:01]). [ONCE]
- **First sign of selling** appears where price "goes sideways / gets chunky" with a sell imbalance after a clean impulsive up-move (v154, "Ratio Divergence POC", [09:13]). [ONCE]

## E. Absorption clues
- **Heavy volume holding a level = support/absorption, NOT rejection:** ~6,000 contracts traded at a level to "support this market," then it can't get offered down again → strong support (v146, "Prominent POCs Trapped Trader", [07:16], [07:43]). [ONCE]
- **Big seller absorbing at resistance:** a seller "whacking it," hitting sell button on each test; over 2–3 tests he has "only a little bit left to sell," then buyers realize it and the market rallies — this is the absorption-then-flip story (v158, "Price Rejection Webinar", [10:20], [10:46]). [REPEATED]
- Distinguishing absorption/support from rejection requires looking at VOLUME: heavy/consistent volume on tests = level likely breaks eventually; light volume = bounce (v158, "Price Rejection Webinar", [17:18], [17:45]). [REPEATED]
