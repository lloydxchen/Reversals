## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Delta Surge + candle-color confirmation** (bullish/bearish): Delta Surge firing in the direction of the bar close; he stresses the bar must close in the direction of the signal (red bar for sell, green bar for buy). (v42, [05:49])
- **Delta Scalper + candle-color confirmation** (bullish/bearish): Same rule — take the scalper signal only when bar color agrees; dismisses opposite-color signals as not worth taking. (v42, [06:16])
- **Ratio + Divergence combo** (bullish/bearish): Price Rejector ratio (bearish or bullish) combined with delta divergence at a swing high/low. Referred to as the "go-to" combo; both signals are preferred together but each is conditionally actionable alone. (v42, [19:16]; v43, [09:01])
- **O2X trade** (bearish): High with a ratio, then pullback within ~2 ticks of that high for entry; references "unfinished business" to explain why the re-test is likely. (v42, [19:16])
- **Three-in-a-row pullback setup** (bullish/bearish): Three consecutive ratio prints in the same direction, then a fourth-bar pullback to the point-of-control of bars 2–3 as entry; "ideally" the pullback occurs in bar 4, but bar 5 is acceptable. (v44, [08:37]; v45, [05:10])
- **POC-at-extreme** (bullish/bearish): Point of control at the bottom of a bar at a swing low = bullish signal; POC at the top of a bar at a swing high = bearish signal. Consistently called "important." (v43, [13:24]; v44, [11:04])
- **Stacked imbalance continuation / reversal**: Stacked buying imbalances at a low with Delta Surge = continuation lower (selling picking up), or as a reversal trigger when combined with other signals. (v45, [03:23])
- **Transition indicator signal** (bullish/bearish): Proprietary indicator detecting shift from demand-driven to supply-driven market (or vice versa); named because "the market is transitioning from buyers to sellers." (v42, [27:22]; v43, [15:30])
- **Voltos U-turn** (bullish/bearish): Another proprietary indicator, named after himself; fires at highs and lows. (v44, [31:18]; v45, [08:16])
- **Volume drying up at re-test** (bearish example shown): At a double top/high retest, second visit trades dramatically lower volume (example: 21 lots first visit, 1 lot second visit) + ratio = high-probability sell. (v45, [19:31])
- **Delta divergence + ratio at swing low** (bullish): Classic combo — delta waning on approach to low, then strong positive delta spike (v43, [20:24]); or negative delta at a low with green candle + ratio = bullish (v43, [13:24]).
- **Stacked imbalance + Delta Surge** (continuation): Stacked imbalance aligns with Delta Surge in same direction; described as confirming the move will continue. (v42, [12:30])

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Volume drying up at re-test**: First visit to a high trades significant volume (21 lots in the euro example); second visit trades trivially (1 lot). The interpretation is "buying is drying up" — the buyers who drove price to that level have been exhausted; no fresh buyers are willing to pay up again. This makes the second-test rejection high-probability. (v45, [19:31]) [ONCE]
- **POC at extreme as "who's still here" signal**: POC at the bottom of a bar means most transactions occurred at the low = people were actively trading there; if POC migrates higher (or lower for shorts), the defense is softening. Watching whether POC moves into or away from the level tells you if buyers/sellers are still present. (v43, [04:51]; v44, [11:11]) [REPEATED]
- **Stacked imbalance — continued aggression vs. capitulation**: When a stacked buying imbalance appears, he expects continued aggressive buying in the next 1–2 bars. If aggressive selling appears instead (sellers overpower the imbalance), the setup fails: "you don't want to see selling come in and price rejection." (v44, [20:59]) [REPEATED]
- **Professional probing at highs/lows**: Professionals place small test orders at highs/lows (3 lots, 1 lot, 5 lots) to test whether the level will trigger stop-runs or larger participation. If the probe doesn't trigger fresh aggression, the level holds. (v45, [06:49]) [ONCE]
- **Tight range / no institutional size**: In a tight range, institutional traders are absent (little size in bars, no range expansion). Question to ask: who is active? No institutional flow = low-quality signal environment. (v44, [07:26]) [ONCE]
- **Markets attracted to size / size attracts size**: Large orders at a price level attract other large-order traders looking for liquidity. This means a big volume cluster is NOT automatically resistance; it can be absorbed and price can continue through if other large traders want to trade there. (v45, [10:53]) [ONCE]
- **Absorption context for POC signal**: When a big cluster appears and defenders run out of inventory, price drops away; if defenders take on more, it becomes support. He describes this as "if these buyers are done buying, what's going to happen to the market?" (v43, [04:51]) [REPEATED]

## D. Exhaustion clues

- **Delta waning into a low**: Delta goes from large negative to progressively smaller negative values (e.g., -399, -138, -83, -51) then spikes strongly positive. The shrinking negative delta = sellers exhausting; the positive spike = fresh buyers taking control. (v43, [20:24]) [REPEATED]
- **Volume drying up on second test**: Much lower volume on the second visit to a high/low vs. the first = the aggressor is exhausted; they cannot repeat their prior commitment. (v45, [19:31]) [ONCE]
- **Failed divergences as trending signal**: Three failed delta divergences in approximately 30 minutes (at the same high level) = a sign the market wants to go higher; he starts interpreting the failures as a sign the market is bullish, not that reversals are failing. (v44, [06:09]) [REPEATED — also in digest, but specificity of "3 divergences in ~30 min at same level → trend day" is worth noting as a stronger form of the general rule]

## R. Contradictions / nuances (anything that conflicts with common belief, with his other statements, or that is conditional)

- **Size at a price level is NOT automatically resistance/support**: Contradicts the common scalper assumption that large size on the DOM or in footprint = the level will hold. He says "markets attracted to size" because big traders need size to trade against; size attracts more size. A 15,000 contract buying imbalance in 5-year treasury doesn't mean the level will reject — it can attract more buyers through it. (v45, [10:53]; v45, [16:55]) [REPEATED]
- **Jumping in front of large orders is often wrong**: Scalpers who see 1,000 lots on the offer and sell it short expecting the market to drop are often wrong because another large buyer can just absorb it. "Those people that jumped in front all of a sudden are short at seven and the big offer at eight, each trade nine bid." (v45, [11:16]) [ONCE]
- **Ratio alone is not "good enough"**: This appears to contradict his earlier (single-signal) usage of the Delta Scalper, but he reconciles it: each signal alone is "actionable" but having a combo "probably" improves odds. He is not saying you need all signals simultaneously, just that ratio without divergence or POC is lower quality. (v43, [11:04]) [REPEATED]
- **He explicitly rejects the "star system" (counting confluence signals) yet recommends confluence**: He says he doesn't like counting how many signals are firing (the horse-racing "squiggly marks" analogy), but then says "you know yeah you can make the argument of confluence... is it better to get a few, probably." This is a tension in his model: confluence improves odds but counting them mechanically is wrong. (v44, [16:28]) [ONCE]
- **Doji bars are "kind of neutral" for orderflow analysis**: He says he "doesn't overlook them but finds them neutral"; he passes on setups that only have a doji bar signal. This contrasts with the implication that any signal at a swing level is actionable. (v45, [27:29]) [ONCE]

- **Failed divergences at the same level in 30 minutes → trend day (don't fade)**: This is a conditional inversion of the divergence rule; three failures at the same high = the high will probably break, not hold. The number "3 in ~30 minutes at same level" is specific enough to be operationalized. - **Transition indicator described as "usually a pretty good indicator"**: He says it's "usually pretty good" but shows examples where it produced 2 losses and 1 no-result in a single session. This is honest but confirms the indicator is not a standalone signal. (v44, [31:46]) [ONCE]
