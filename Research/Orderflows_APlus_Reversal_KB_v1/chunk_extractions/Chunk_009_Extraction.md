## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup
- **Low-of-day buy:** market over-supplied, prices drop until demand appears; basic support = "if it wasn't support the market would have kept dropping" (v302, [04:01]). Green candles after a sell-off may just be **shorts covering / locking profits**, NOT real demand — "doesn't mean the selling is over" (v302, [12:31]).
- **Red candle with positive delta at a low = supply still present**: aggressive buying (positive delta) but price still down means sellers are absorbing/supplying — a reason NOT to buy yet (v302, [09:03]).
- **Cum-delta divergence at high:** buyers keep trying to push the high; on the other side there is **absorption / distribution** — traders happy to "sell it to everybody coming in to get long." Key insight: "the **easiest way to sell** [supply] is into a rising market, not once it starts dropping" (because dropping = chasing) (v309, [04:25]).
- **Reversal trigger psychology:** "everybody that was buying ... once they realize this market isn't going higher, **that's when they became sellers**" (v309, [11:40]).
- **FOMC context:** intraday/prop/internet traders square up ~1 hr before FOMC minutes; only "retail traders covering their ass" trade the actual release (v309, [03:27], [11:40]).
- **Trend/flow day:** failed bearish divergences = longer-timeframe "other timeframe traders" (deep pockets, Market/Volume Profile crowd) holding and pushing → highs are "not strong," expect higher highs (v312, [04:40], [06:52]).

## D. Exhaustion clues
- **Exhaustion print:** a **single (1) contract** trading on the extreme side of a bar after a large directional move — red candle highlights top/offer side (bearish at high); green at low (bullish). "Sticks out like a sore thumb." His own concept, copied by other software (v314, [14:34], [15:12], [16:09]) [REPEATED].
- **Lack of volume = exhaustion:** final push to a high trades only **2 lots** at a level that earlier had several thousand contracts — "market is struggling to make its move" (v314, [08:21], [09:23]). Generalized: "when you have lack of volume[,] it's a sign of exhaustion" — order flow is also about areas of NO volume/interest (v314, [21:49]) [REPEATED].
- **Declining volume into re-tests:** each return to the low/high trades less volume (e.g., 55 → 44 → 24) — selling "starting to wear off" (v302, [23:40]).
- **Sequential decline in delta** going into a high (e.g., 147 → 188 → 256 as he names "one of the old NOFT signals" — note: numbers appear ascending in transcript, treat as garbled NEEDS-OPERATIONALIZATION) (v314, [09:56]).
- **Time-based exhaustion (his personal rule):** if the move doesn't happen within ~2 bars / "sooner rather than later," the reason is "worn off" — cut it (v302, [11:06], [21:02]).

## E. Absorption clues
- **Aggressive buying present but price won't advance = absorption/distribution by supply** (v309, [03:54], [04:25]) [REPEATED]. Distinguishes "absorption" vs "distribution" — says people confuse them; at a high it's really distribution (supply absorbing buys).
- **Red candle + positive delta** = buyers aggressive but supply absorbing → price still falls (v302, [09:03]) [REPEATED].
- **Big resting offer acting as resistance** (e.g., ~3,500 contracts: 415/500/732/1200/800 on the offer) holds the market; when that offer is finally consumed/"removed," market pops up — removed resistance → upmove (v314, [12:07], [13:05]). Mirror: stacked buying imbalance acts as support; if it holds and price moves away, buy (v303, [10:40]).

Mirror at highs: expect buying imbalances into the high; look for SELLING imbalances to short (v307, [16:31]).

## G. Delta / delta-divergence ideas
- **Per-bar Delta Divergence (his strict definition):** (1) new or equal high/low of day, (2) negative delta (for a sell signal), AND (3) **bar must close down / be a red bar** (price action confirms). He adds bar-color as an extra software feature; says divergence without the down-close is "fundamentally wrong" (v312, [01:27], [02:03], [02:34]) [REPEATED v314].
- **Cumulative Delta Divergence:** new high in cumulative delta but price fails to make a new high (or vice versa) → reversal (v309, [02:19]) [ONCE].
- **"Failed" divergences signal a TREND day:** 3 bearish divergences that get *taken out* (highs exceeded) within ~1 hour → flow-driven up-day; buy the breakout above the 3rd divergence's high (v312, [04:07], [04:40]) [REPEATED v314]. Mirror for lows.
- **Delta strength reading for confirmation:** want positive delta to get **stronger / more directional** bar over bar off a low; weakening/declining positive delta while price ticks up is a warning ("going up on declining delta off the low ... I don't like that") (v302, [22:10], [28:24]).
- **Delta/Volume ratio** ("delta divided by volume"): normal ~5–10%; magenta-flagged extremes cited at **−28%, −27%** mark very strong selling (v309, [06:27], [09:04]) [ONCE — exact numbers].
- **Min Delta / Max Delta:** bar closing on/near its Min Delta (e.g., −923 vs Min −948) = selling "overwhelming"; closing near Max Delta (cyan) = buying conviction (v309, [08:26]; v302, [12:58], [21:33]).
- **Small delta (~±50 on e-minis)** = "trade is evenly matched," buyers/sellers balanced — no signal (v302, [14:12]).

## H. Failed breakout / breakdown / stop-run / liquidity-sweep ideas
- **Failed high re-test → short:** market makes high, pulls back, makes 2nd/3rd run that fails (can't take out high) while FOMC looms → reversal (v309, [03:54]).
- **"Failed divergences" being taken out** = a *bullish* failed-breakdown read (highs repeatedly exceeded) → trend continuation up (v312, [04:40]) — note this is the constructive mirror of a failed setup.
- **Resting liquidity at new highs:** when making new highs, "unless a stop gets triggered there tends to be resting liquidity" — affects fill on a buy-stop (v314, [04:24]).
- **Removed resistance pop:** big offer that capped the market gets consumed → look for pop back up; then prior offer level should act as support (v314, [13:05]).

## R. Contradictions / nuances
- **Selling/buying imbalances are context-dependent, not directional on their own:** imbalances INTO a low (selling) are *expected* and not a short; only imbalances AFTER the extreme matter — directly contradicts naive "stacked imbalance = trade it" thinking (v303, [02:17]; v307, [16:31]) [REPEATED].
- **A single delta divergence is NOT a trade** — only a *cluster of 3 failed* ones flips meaning from "fade" to "trend-continuation buy." Same primitive, opposite action depending on whether prior divergences failed (v312, [04:40]; v314, [02:31]).
- **Stacked vs multiple imbalances:** he downplays the popular "stacked" signal in favor of his "multiple" (3+, unstacked) signal because stacked fires too late (v307, [13:53]) — contrarian to common order-flow teaching.
- **Positive delta can be bearish:** red candle + positive delta = supply absorbing → don't buy (v302, [09:03]) — counter to "positive delta = bullish."
- **Time window is deliberately fuzzy:** insists 3-divergences-in-~1-hour must NOT be hard-coded; a trader extends "one or two minutes" where a programmer would reject it (v312, [05:51], [06:22]; v314, [03:24]) — explicit tension between his discretion and codifying the model.
- **"It depends" on chart type:** the SAME order flow shows a divergence/stacked imbalance on a range or tick chart but NOT on a time chart — signal presence is chart-dependent, so an indicator's output depends on bar construction (v313, [04:36], [09:26], [10:02]) [REPEATED] — important caveat for a single-chart NinjaTrader indicator.
- **Don't fish:** at a low, the bullish reason should be "poking you in the eye"; "don't go fishing for reasons" — high bar for entry quality (v307, [10:56]).
- **Pin-bar/hammer skepticism:** rejects candlestick reversal signals that the order-flow crowd often still uses (v302, [05:37]).
