## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Delta weakness at highs/lows** (bearish at high, bullish at low): Market makes new high/low; delta no longer confirms — specifically, delta has already peaked and is "petering out" or closing well inside its intrabar extreme. Bearish setup: new high with weakening/negative delta. Bullish: new low with delta flipping from strong negative toward positive. (v256, "Hidden Trading Opportunities", [44:02]–[46:16])

- **Aggressive selling near a high / aggressive buying near a low**: Market has made a directional move; trader looks at imbalances + delta simultaneously to confirm the reversal side. "Aggressive sellers are coming in both by looking at the imbalances by looking at the delta." Bullish counterpart: aggressive buying at a low with positive delta after strong negative delta run. (v256, [42:58]–[43:26])

- **Delta surge (bullish or bearish)**: Delta incrementally increasing (bar-over-bar) in one direction after a prior directional move — confirms continuation or reversal momentum. (v256, [34:09])

- **Back-to-back POCs (same price level)**: Two or more consecutive bars share the same point of control (highest-volume price). When the market then moves away from that level and later returns to it, the back-to-back POC level acts as a magnetic support/resistance zone for a low-risk entry. (v259, [00:25]–[03:38])

- **Flow-driven market (fade-is-wrong regime)**: Market keeps invalidating divergence signals — bears order flow taken out in the next bar immediately. This is a recognition setup, not a reversal setup: trader switches from fading to following. (v256, [35:14]–[37:47])

- **Orderflows Stratum signal** (new indicator, v257): Analyzes distribution of volume within the value area on a per-bar basis; outputs bullish/bearish signal on bar close, with optional follow-through confirmation ("trade entry signal" = signal fires on next bar after close, requiring the market to move at least a minimum number of ticks in the signal direction over the next two bars). (v257, [07:27]–[08:33], [49:17]–[51:37])

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Delta intrabar flip (hidden reversal)**: Large negative delta reaches a peak (e.g., −2,200) then reverses to close near zero or positive (+278 in example). Story: strong sellers sold aggressively to establish the move, but equally strong buyers absorbed them all within the same bar. "You had strong selling down here…but then you actually had 2,000 buyers come into the market." The hidden reversal is concealed in the headline delta figure. Trapped shorts who were riding the move down are now squeezed as buyers overwhelm them intrabar. (v256, [22:09]–[23:44])

- **Delta weakening at HOD/LOD**: Aggressive buyers push to a new high with strong delta (1,100, 1,400). As the market keeps probing higher, delta shrinks (e.g., closes at only +40 after hitting +952 max delta intrabar). Story: the last buyers tried to come in but sellers absorbed them; aggressive buying is exhausted, the remaining bullish pressure is symbolic. "Why aren't they coming in?" — the buyers who should be present to continue the move are not showing up. (v256, [44:34]–[45:40])

- **Aggressive selling at HOD (imbalances + delta)**: Market makes new high; now sees selling imbalances in bars AND negative delta simultaneously. Story: aggressive sellers have replaced aggressive buyers at the extreme. This is the first unambiguous signal that direction has changed. (v256, [43:26]–[44:02])

- **Back-to-back POCs**: Two+ consecutive bars transact the most volume at the exact same price. Story: market participants are in agreement about value at that level; it is heavily "defended" by passive interest. When the market moves away and returns, that price acts like a magnet because passive orders likely still rest there. (v259, [00:25]–[01:49])

- **Flow-driven market**: Bearish order flow signals (delta divergence, imbalances) appear but market runs over them immediately. Story: "bigger things at play…more macro views changing." The normal aggressors-exhaust story is overwhelmed by a larger directional participant. Attempting to fade signals cost back-to-back stop-outs. (v256, [35:42]–[37:18])

## D. Exhaustion clues

- **Delta peaked and closing far inside its intrabar extreme**: Bar closes at delta of +40 but reached +952 intrabar max delta. "It gave back almost a thousand of its aggressive buying, turns to aggressive selling." Close far inside max delta = exhaustion of that directional aggression within the bar. (v256, [45:07])

- **New high on strong positive delta, then new high on weak delta (+40) or negative delta**: Sequence of deteriorating delta at successive new highs = "petering out." (v256, [44:34]–[45:07])

- **Aggressive selling at highs — "not negative like ah, it's just a small number — it's quite strong": 600, 500, 900, 1,000 delta on bars at the HOD. When strong negative deltas appear at the HOD with no bullish follow-through, exhaustion of buyers is confirmed. (v256, [20:10]–[20:39])

- **Thin volume at prior high (price rejection)**: On a test of a resistance level, only 5 contracts traded where normally thousands trade (10yr example). "Traders aren't interested at this level anymore." Zero-volume/near-zero at a retest = exhaustion/rejection without strong sellers even needing to appear. (v256, [40:24]–[40:57])

## E. Absorption clues

- **Heavy buying absorbing heavy selling within a single bar (intrabar absorption)**: Min delta −2,200 but bar closes at −278 (or near zero). Explicit description: "you had strong sellers in that bar but you know that heavy strong buyers came in." Aggressive buying absorbed the entire aggressive sell within one bar — strongest form of absorption. (v256, [22:09]–[23:44])

- **Big volume at a level that holds**: Market comes into support, trades a "lot of volume," absorbs sellers, then bounces. Contrasted with low-volume resistance test that fails to absorb buyers; here heavy volume at a level with a hold = absorption confirmed. (v256, [39:53]–[40:24])

- **Back-to-back POCs as absorption marker**: Repeated highest-volume concentration at the exact same price across consecutive bars implies passive absorption occurring at that price; price cannot move away from it because buyers/sellers are actively absorbing at that level. (v259, [00:59]–[01:23])

## J. Entry triggers (the exact "go" event)

## K. Confirmation — what SHOULD happen quickly after entry/trigger

- **Immediate continuation of delta in the entry direction**: "Maybe you're not convinced in this bar you look at the next bar positive delta 300…then positive positive positive more positive delta coming in." Delta should build progressively in the entry direction. (v256, [24:14])

- **Delta grows stronger, not weaker**: After entry on delta surge, subsequent bars should show incrementally larger delta in the direction of the trade. (v256, [26:54])

- **Stratum follow-through (trade entry signal)**: Within 1–2 bars after the signal bar closes, price should move at least the minimum tick threshold in the signal direction before the signal prints — this IS the confirmation gate. If it doesn't fire, the trade is avoided. (v257, [49:54])

- **Market should NOT come back into the Stratum zone (stop zone)**: The blue/red zone below/above the signal bar is the buffer. If price re-enters that zone it is "getting weaker" and the trade is at risk. Price staying away from the zone = healthy. (v257, [1:09:04]–[1:09:38])

## R. Contradictions / nuances

- **Stratum is NOT delta-based**: Explicitly "it doesn't deal with the delta" — it analyzes bar volume distribution within the value area. This is an entirely different axis of order-flow analysis from everything else in his toolkit (which is predominantly delta and imbalance driven). The model thus has at least two orthogonal order-flow dimensions: (1) delta/imbalance-based exhaustion and (2) volume-distribution-in-value (Stratum). Using both as confirmation could be powerful but he explicitly does not recommend stacking indicators in a mechanical way. (v257, [1:05:49]) [nuance]

- **Close delta can be misleading — headline delta misleads**: He specifically warns against interpreting the closing delta at face value: a bar with delta = −278 is bearish on the surface but is actually bullish if min delta was −2,200 (meaning buyers overwhelmed sellers intrabar). This is a direct contradiction of naive delta-reading: "Well yeah it's got negative delta. Well yeah it's got negative delta but…" Traders using only close-bar delta will misread these bars. (v256, [23:17]) [CONTRADICTION vs. simple delta reading]

- **Flow-driven day = delta divergence fails as a standalone signal**: On flow-driven days, the otherwise reliable "new high + negative delta" signal fails repeatedly. He acknowledges people can be sold a $500 system that fires this signal and it will work on "normal rotational markets" but blow up on flow-driven days. The divergence signal has a hard regime dependency. (v256, [36:13]–[37:18]) [REPEATED — critical conditional]

- **More signals is not better**: Explicitly stated re: Stratum at lower magnitude settings — "is more really better…would you rather have less that are more accurate or more." This contradicts the instinct to run the most sensitive settings. Lower magnitude (more signals) includes more noise and marginal setups. (v257, [43:59])

- **Back-to-back POCs that persist (market stays at the level) = NOT the setup**: "I would like to look for where the market moves away from those two pointed controls" — if the market just keeps trading at the POC level for many bars, it is NOT the setup. The setup requires price to leave the POC level and then return. (v259, [01:49]–[02:19]) [important condition/nuance]

- **Stratum signal timing — on-bar vs. next-bar**: With trade entry signal DISABLED, the Stratum signal appears intrabar and can repaint (disappear if bar turns against the signal). With trade entry signal ENABLED, it fires on the next bar after close with follow-through confirmation, no repaint. Using the intrabar signal is explicitly framed as inferior. (v257, [51:37]–[52:09]) [relevant to NT implementation — repaint risk]
