## A. Setup types & names he uses

1. **Delta Surge / Accumulation-Distribution Transition** (bullish and bearish) — recognizing when delta changes direction at a swing high/low, signaling a market transition from demand-driven to supply-driven (or vice versa). Entry is early, before the breakout. (v177, [24:19], [27:12])

2. **Stopping Volume / Prominent POC Reversal** (bullish and bearish) — a bar whose POC stands out as a "prominent point of control," highlighted by the software as magenta (resistance) or cyan (support). It caps or floors a move and can signal a reversal entry. (v177, [45:21], [48:21])

3. **Price Rejection / Single-Digit Print Reversal** (bullish and bearish) — finding bars at a swing extreme where the edge-of-bar volume is abnormally small (single-digit or near it), indicating exhaustion/rejection of that price level. Can be used as a reversal trigger or to stay in an existing trade. (v177, [51:47], [59:56], [1:01:25])

4. **Bunched POC Migration / Value-Area Expansion Reversal** — when multiple POCs (POC1, POC2, POC3) from consecutive bars cluster together and migrate in a direction, it indicates directional value movement and impending breakout; once price trades back to that cluster, it becomes support/resistance. (v177, [1:09:45], [1:10:36])

5. **Stacked Imbalance Continuation/Reversal** — briefly referenced in a trade example (3 selling imbalances stacked, then price rejection confirming direction). (v177, [58:23])

6. **Distribution/Accumulation at Tops and Bottoms** (bullish and bearish) — sideways price action with persistently negative (or positive) delta revealing directional selling (or buying) hidden in the congestion, before the eventual breakout. Low-risk entry because stop is tight at nearby swing. (v177, [32:16], [35:14])

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

**Accumulation/Distribution at a Top (Bearish):**
- As price consolidates sideways near highs, aggressive sellers repeatedly hit passive bids. Passive buyers think they're getting free money as market bounces. But sellers keep hitting bids, luring buyers in (false sense of security), then overwhelm them. Final tell: a big bar with the strongest negative delta in the sequence — "where's the bidders? They're gone." (v177, [34:24], [35:41], [36:13])

**Delta Transition at a Low (Bullish):**
- At a swing low, institutional buyers/algos try to accumulate without moving price. Delta is mostly positive even though price is at lows — "aggressive sellers aren't in control of this low, aggressive buyers are." The rest of the market eventually picks up on it and price pops. (v177, [42:04], [42:31])

**Stopping Volume at Highs (Bearish):**
- A "big offer coming into the market to stop the move up." The Prominent POC acts as a ceiling. Market keeps returning to that level, then "pukes out and falls over" once patience of passive buyers is exhausted. (v177, [46:48], [47:19])

**Price Rejection at Extremes (Reversal):**
- Traders "tire" of a price level — very little volume traded there means acceptance is near-zero. "Traders are tired of that price and don't want to trade it anymore." This is "price exhaustion." (v177, [1:02:54])

**Third-Strike delta pattern (Bearish/Bullish):**
- "After the third strong delta in a direction either positive or negative, that's when the move happens." Three waves of aggressive delta in one direction before the break. [ONCE / NEEDS-OPERATIONALIZATION — he does not define "strong"] (v177, [29:50])

**"Herd mentality" amplification:**
- Once institutional sellers start, others see it and join. The narrative context: "trading sometimes is a herd mentality." (v177, [25:18])

## D. Exhaustion clues

1. **Delta fails to build:** After a strong positive delta bar at a high, the next bar cannot sustain or exceed it — "the next bar couldn't build on it." Classic exhaustion of buyers at the top. (v177, [35:41], [37:50])

2. **Delta diminishes on successive attempts:** Third attempt at the high has only delta of "minus 55, minus 122, minus 647" when prior runs had delta of "800, 900, 1,200, 1,900." The comparative shrinkage of positive delta on each swing-high test signals buyer exhaustion. (v177, [39:31], [40:07])

3. **Single-digit / near-zero prints at bar extreme:** Volume at the extreme of the bar drops to near-zero (e.g., 1, 4, 7 contracts). "Traders are tired of that price." [REPEATED — matches digest's price-rejection / exhaustion-print concept] (v177, [59:56], [1:01:25], [1:02:21])

4. **Last strong negative delta bar at a low:** "Minus 1349 — that's like the last push of sellers coming in." The largest single negative delta bar often marks exhaustion bottom. (v177, [26:44])

5. **Third strong-delta-in-direction rule:** After approximately 3 strong delta bars in one direction, expect the move. [ONCE, NEEDS-OPERATIONALIZATION] (v177, [29:50])

## E. Absorption clues

1. **Positive delta at sideways-to-lower prices near a low:** Price not rising despite positive delta = buyers absorbing sellers without moving price (institutional accumulation model). "They want to trade as much of a position as possible without impacting the price." (v177, [42:04])

2. **Negative delta at sideways-to-higher price near a high:** Price not falling despite negative delta = sellers absorbing buyers. "Aggressive sellers are hitting the bids… they let the market go back up. Gives people a false sense of security." (v177, [34:24], [34:48])

3. **Prominent POC as absorption marker:** A POC that attracts and stops a move is where a "big offer" or "big bid" is absorbing the opposite side. (v177, [45:21], [46:48])

## G. Delta / delta-divergence ideas

1. **Cumulative delta direction matches trend; divergence at extreme = reversal:** As market goes up → positive deltas; as market goes down → negative deltas. Divergence (e.g., negative delta at highs while price hasn't fallen yet) is the early warning. (v177, [22:28], [23:51])

2. **Three consecutive strong delta bars in the same direction precede the breakout:** [ONCE, NEEDS-OPERATIONALIZATION] "After the third strong delta in a direction either positive or negative, that's when the move happens." (v177, [29:50])

3. **Failure of delta to build on prior extreme = key exhaustion signal:** "You had a big strong positive delta, but the next bar couldn't build on it." (v177, [35:41])

4. **Delta Scalper indicator:** Described as analyzing "current delta versus delta in the immediate past" for "quick hits." It is distinct from delta surge. [ONCE — implementation note] (v177, [1:35:07])

5. **Max/min delta interpretation:** Max delta positive = at some point buyers were in control; zero max delta on a straight-down bar = buyers never in control at any tick. (v177, [17:41], [18:12])

## H. Failed breakout / breakdown / stop-run / liquidity-sweep ideas

1. **False sense of security / distribution trap:** Sellers repeatedly hit bids, let market bounce to lure passive buyers, then overwhelm them. This is framed as a slow-motion distribution trap, not an instantaneous stop-run. (v177, [34:48])

2. **POC resistance tested multiple times before breakdown:** Market "keeps coming back up there, keeps coming back up there, keeps coming back up there before it finally pukes out and falls over." Repeated tests of a Prominent POC = exhausting of buyers, leading to breakdown. (v177, [46:48])

3. **Sweep of stacked POC resistance:** A sweep through a bunched-POC resistance zone accompanied by a large buying imbalance — "someone swept through that market" — is acknowledged but presented as the continuation signal (breakout), not a reversal trap. (v177, [1:11:04], [1:11:32])

## I. Trapped-trader ideas

2. **Price-action traders entering late:** "Price action traders would get short underneath the swing low, but order flow traders are going to be getting short up here in this congestion area." PA traders trapped entering after the move. (v177, [35:14]) [ONCE]
