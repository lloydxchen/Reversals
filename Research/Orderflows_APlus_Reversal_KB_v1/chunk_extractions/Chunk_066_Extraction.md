## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Multiple imbalances (non-stacked)** — bars with 3+ buying or selling imbalances spread out throughout the bar (not neatly stacked); explicitly distinguished from stacked imbalances and treated as at least equally important. Bullish or bearish depending on imbalance direction. (v205, [42:28]; v206, [28:49])
- **Stacked imbalances** — 3+ imbalances neatly stacked on consecutive price levels; the "classic" setup everybody already knows about. Covered briefly as baseline for comparison. (v205, [40:27]; v206, [26:16])
- **Prominent POC (support/resistance)** — bearish (magenta/red) or bullish (blue) POC highlighted by software at swing extremes; used as context filter / confluence for reversals. (v205, [23:01]; v206, [32:31])
- **Market weakness / delta weakening** — delta decaying on a directional move signals the move is exhausting; a blue up-arrow (bullish) or equivalent signal is shown by software. Applies both at trend highs (selling weakens) and trend lows (buying weakens). (v205, [51:14]; v206, [11:49])
- **Value area expansion (EVA — Extended Value Area)** — bearish or bullish: value area opens above/below prior bar's value area and closes in the opposite direction (engulfing-EVA); used as a turn signal. (v205, [31:18]; [32:42])
- **Absorption at a low (green candle with negative delta)** — market closes green despite negative delta because passive buyers absorbed aggressive sellers; treated as a bullish reversal sign. (v205, [1:00:52])
- **Delta divergence at a high/low** — strong negative delta at HOD or positive delta bar that closes lower than expected signals trapped traders and likely reversal. (v206, [15:10]; [18:43])
- **Stopping / market-reversal off a second test / failed breakout** — second test of a level that achieves only a "one tick new high/low" with renewed aggressive-opposite delta is flagged as a likely reversal (v206, [24:11]; [24:43])

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Multiple buying imbalances at a low** — aggressive buyers "wake up and come into the market" after a sell-off; the aggressive sellers have exhausted supply; buyers step in and absorb, leaving imbalance footprints. Market reversal follows as supply is removed. (v205, [43:38]; v206, [36:43])
- **Multiple selling imbalances at a high** — large institutional players "have supply on the books, happy to sell at 950"; they "stack the order book, working their offers"; retail buyers "gobble up" supply; once they realize "we're not going any higher" they cover, feeding the selloff. (v206, [19:43])
- **Delta divergence (positive delta, downward price at HOD)** — buyers pile in on a breakout attempt, but institutional sellers are providing liquidity; trapped longs realize no follow-through and must exit, accelerating the selloff. (v206, [18:43]; [21:11])
- **Delta weakening on a downmove** — aggressive selling "runs through its course," "exhausted itself"; removal of selling pressure causes natural reversion upward. Absorbers remove supply; once sellers are done ("no more bullets left"), buyers take control. (v205, [55:13]; [1:01:25])
- **Green candle with negative delta** — passive buyers absorbed aggressive selling intrabar; "somebody absorbing that selling… all those sellers are pretty much done, removed from the market… what happened? market went back up." Classic absorption story. (v205, [1:00:52])
- **One-tick new high with renewed aggressive selling** — trapped bulls from first test and new buyers all caught offside simultaneously; aggressive sellers who missed the first leg return; trapped bulls must cover, accelerating the selloff. (v206, [24:43])
