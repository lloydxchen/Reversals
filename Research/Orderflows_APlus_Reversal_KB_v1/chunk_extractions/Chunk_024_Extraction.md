## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **O2X trade setup** (bearish): Rally to a high, pull back to within two ticks of the high, then short entry. Stop placed just behind the high; mentioned as a named, known setup (v30, "March 14 Recap", [05:57])
- **Delta divergence** (bullish and bearish): Positive delta at new/equal low as bullish; strong negative delta at new/equal high as bearish. Repeatedly applied across ES, ZF, ZS, FDAX, ZB, 6E (v30–v34, multiple timestamps)
- **Stacked imbalance with ratio combo** (bullish): Stacked imbalance + ratio in same area = potential reversal/continuation signal. "Maybe this is it" trigger (v30, [10:02])
- **Absorption / heavy bid-volume support** (bullish): Large bid-side volume (1,000–1,084 contracts per price level in ZS) interpreted as institutional defense of a level; "slam dunk" when imbalance arrow also fires (v33, [03:54]; v34, [08:38])
- **Overlapping stacked imbalances** (directional): When a second opposite-color stacked imbalance forms within the five-bar zone of the first, the second imbalance wins — very high percentage trade (v33, [18:23]) — NAMED: "overlapping stacked imbalances"
- **Ratio at reversal low/high** (bullish/bearish): Blue ratio number at swing low (green bar, ratio below) or swing high (red bar, ratio above) acts as confirmation filter on divergence (v34, [10:08], [08:13])
- **Three failed divergences → trend day**: Three or more failed divergences in quick succession (within ~13 minutes on 10-range, or 10 minutes on 3-range) signal trend continuation; avoid counter-trend longs/shorts (v30, [14:06]; v33, [09:08])
- **Lower/higher migrating POC → trend filter**: Sequence of progressively lower POCs = bearish trend day; higher POCs = bullish trend day (v33, [07:18])
- **Delta surge** (directional momentum): Small-to-large sequential delta picking up on one side signals potential directional move (v30, [11:46], [15:06])
- **Imbalance arrow (single) at key level** ("slam dunk"): A single selling or buying imbalance at the bottom of a green bar or top of a red bar, when at a swing low/high, described as very high-percentage short-term trade (v34, [08:38])
- **Transition indicator signals** (bullish/bearish): Proprietary indicator reading order flow to identify buyer→seller / seller→buyer transitions; generates buy/sell signals on chart (v32, [03:16]; v33, [22:51])
- **U-turn indicator signals**: Proprietary indicator measuring inflection points; does not fire on every turn, fires on meaningful turning-point order flow (v32, [00:55])
- **Volto Flip indicator signals**: Short-term scalping indicator; series of losses then a big winner; designed for very short-term scalpers (v31, [22:34]; v32, [04:35])

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **O2X / resistance test**: Big volume transacted at a level means "people are happy to transact" there. When price revisits that level, traders who bought there defend it; if it fails decisively (price closes below the heavy volume), those buyers have no one behind them → market falls (v30, [05:57], [05:17])
- **Absorption at a low (ZS example v33)**: Large bid-side prints (1,000+ per level) = institutional buyers defending the level "for whatever reason." Selling imbalance at POC of the bar + price refusing to break = sellers exhausted, buyers absorbing. Once price rallies "straight up" — confirms absorption was the story (v33, [03:54]–[05:24])
- **Overlapping stacked imbalances**: First stacked imbalance sets a 5-bar zone. When an opposite-color stacked imbalance fires within that zone, the second group of aggressors are overpowering the first → directional momentum belongs to the second imbalance (v33, [18:23])
- **Trapped bulls at high (ES v34)**: Price hits high with strong negative delta, hangs around, makes marginal new highs on weakening positive delta (max delta falls from 2,420 → 977 → 927). Buyers running dry; when negative delta takes over "there should be really be thinking hey we're not going back up to new highs" — longs trapped (v34, [13:00]–[14:05])
- **Failed divergence as trend-day psychology**: Buyers step in at what looks like a low, but selling is so dominant that even "decent" positive deltas (1,000–1,500) are met with 2,000–4,000 negative deltas. Buyers keep failing → trend-day tells you shorts rule (v33, [10:48])
- **ZF absorption rejection (v31)**: Strong negative delta at a price (–11,000+) but price refuses to drop → "buyers standing there palms in" absorbing all selling → market pops rapidly (v31, [03:07])
