## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Thin prints / zero prints / thin volume setup** (bullish or bearish): price levels inside a bar where zero or near-zero contracts traded on one side of the two-way auction; signals fast-moving market and a gap in order flow that price tends to repair or extend from. Described as "one of my favorite trade set ups." (v342, [00:00]; v343, [00:29]) [REPEATED]
- **Multiple thin prints in a single bar**: a bar containing more than one zero/thin volume level; viewed as stronger signal than a single thin print; the Orderflows Trader draws a box around such bars to highlight them. (v342, [08:57]; v343, [09:08]) [REPEATED]
- **Thin print as breakout confirmation / momentum entry** (bullish or bearish): after a ranging market fails to break, a bar with thin prints on the successful breakout bar is the "trigger" to enter. (v343, [09:08]) [ONCE]
- **Prominent POC (bearish or bullish)**: featured as one of his three most important order-flow tools; bearish prominent POC near a swing high signals supply; bullish prominent POC near swing low signals demand. (v337, [05:28]; v342, [03:44]) [REPEATED]
- **Bearish/Bullish abandoned value area**: value area formed at a high/low that is not tested in subsequent bars; gap in value signals directional commitment. (v337, [06:29]) [REPEATED]
- **Stacked imbalances (standard reversal / continuation)**: minimum of 3 for most markets; minimum of **2** for range-based charts (4-range on ZN/treasuries/grains/crude) where each bar has only 5 price levels. (v345, [03:20]; v345, [04:44]) [REPEATED / REFINED]
- **Multiple imbalances (non-stacked)**: buying or selling imbalances spread across a bar but not stacked contiguously; still important even if not meeting the stacked threshold; useful when no stacked signal exists. (v345, [06:37]; v345, [07:18]) [REPEATED]
- **Orderflows Combo signal** (bullish or bearish): indicator that combines POC, value area, imbalances, and price action (explicitly **no Delta**) on a normal bar/candlestick chart; reads conditions inside the bar. (v335, [01:48]; v335, [02:19]) [ONCE in this chunk]
- **Cumulative Delta divergence at swing high**: cumulative Delta declining from the bar at the high while price goes sideways — used as confirmation that high is likely to hold; not itself the trigger but the backdrop. (v337, [07:24]) [REPEATED]
- **Delta trend at swing high (sustained negative Delta)**: multiple consecutive bars at or below the high showing predominantly negative Delta is a sign the high is legitimate and sellers are in control. (v337, [04:05]; v337, [05:28]) [REPEATED]

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Thin print = fast market, lack of participation**: when market moves quickly, traders cannot react fast enough to fill all levels; zero/thin prints are the footprint of a fast move; counter-traders are absent at those levels. (v342, [01:27]; v343, [02:55]) [REPEATED]
- **Thin print repair**: price often returns to the thin print level to "repair poor order flow" — a predictable magnet/pullback level. (v342, [02:48]) [REPEATED]
- **Thin print as extension signal**: alternatively, once price has established a thin print and the next bar trades in the direction of the move, that continuation is the momentum entry — no repair needed. (v342, [09:52]; v343, [09:08]) [REPEATED]
- **Big volume order does NOT always mean "follow it"**: large block sweeping through multiple levels can pull back and reverse before going in the direction of the order; DOM traders who chase the big order get trapped. (v337, [12:17]) [ONCE — explicit warning]
- **Cumulative Delta declining at highs while price is flat**: aggressive sellers are appearing and sustaining; even as price drifts sideways the sellers are piling in; eventually the "tipping point" is reached and the rug is pulled. (v337, [07:56]) [REPEATED]
- **Inside bar after setup bar is bearish for the setup**: if the bar following a potential signal bar trades inside (does not extend), the market has not decided; should wait for below-low break before committing short. (v337, [04:32]) [REPEATED]
