## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Prominent Point of Control (POC) — bullish/bearish**: A POC that meets certain criteria (price context + volume) highlighting support (blue) or resistance (magenta/red). Bullish = supportive buying at a low; bearish = resistance selling at a high. (v161, [18:46]; v162, [26:12]) [REPEATED]
- **Multiple Imbalances setup**: Bar with 3+ imbalances, not necessarily neatly stacked; marked with a box (magenta/pink). Fires earlier than stacked imbalance; seen at swing highs/lows before moves. (v161, [37:56]; v162, [38:41]) [REPEATED]
- **Stacked Imbalances**: 3+ imbalances neatly stacked; near-term S/R only. (v161, [36:53]; v162, [23:45]) [REPEATED]
- **Delta divergence (ratio + divergence)**: New high with negative delta (bearish); new low with positive delta (bullish). Called "bread and butter" by Mike in v162. (v161, [34:28]; v162, [40:10]) [REPEATED]
- **Ratio setup**: Uses bullish/bearish ratios on the footprint; includes a "three in a row" ratio pattern where three consecutive bars show the same ratio directional signal. (v162, [40:36]; v162, [41:31]) [ONCE — "three in a row" variant]
- **Trapped Traders setup**: Areas where traders are long and wrong or short and in a hole; identified via volume, not just bar chart. Causes short-term fuel in opposite direction. (v162, [29:27]) [REPEATED]
- **Market Sweep**: Large order sweeping through several price levels; a leading indicator of potential directional move. Bullish sweep = aggressive buying through offers; bearish = aggressive selling through bids. (v162, [31:23]; v162, [34:49]) [REPEATED]
- **Prominent POC + Multiple Imbalances + Divergence + Ratio confluence**: Full confluence setup at a swing low or high; all signals pointing in same direction = higher conviction. (v162, [41:03]) [REPEATED]

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **At lows**: Aggressive buyers arrive, absorbing passive sellers. POC turns bullish. Multiple buying imbalances appear. Positive delta or delta divergence. This = "the last seller has sold" (implied). (v161, [30:53]; v162, [23:20]) [REPEATED]
- **At highs**: Aggressive sellers take over. Delta goes negative. Bearish prominent POC marks resistance. Multiple selling imbalances appear. This = "the last buyer has bought" (implied). (v161, [41:36]; v162, [43:25]) [REPEATED]
- **Trapped traders contribute fuel**: When trapped longs sell out and trapped shorts cover, it adds to the reversal move — "sort of like adding fuel to the fire." (v162, [29:27]) [REPEATED]
- **Sweeps = institutional commitment**: Institutions with large ticket sizes (thousands of contracts) sweep the market when they want to get into a position NOW. They don't care about average fill price as long as it's below where market will end up. "They buy everything there." (v162, [31:51]; v162, [33:17]) [ONCE — explicit institutional motivation for sweeps]
- **DOM = intentions, footprint = commitments**: Depth of market shows what people intend; volume footprint shows what actually traded = commitment. "It's like being in a relationship." (v161, [26:41]) [ONCE — conceptual reinforcement]

## G. Delta / delta-divergence ideas

- Positive delta on a new low = bullish divergence; go signal when combined with ratio. (v162, [40:10]) [REPEATED]
