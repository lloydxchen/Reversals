## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup
- Strength-at-low: market already selling off, so strong NEGATIVE delta is "expected"; strong POSITIVE delta at the low is the surprise = aggressive buyers deciding the move is "over exaggerated" and "coming out in full force" (v308, [15:10]–[16:49]). [REPEATED]
- Weakness-at-high: after a move down, positive-delta bars are often just profit-taking / shorts covering, NOT new conviction buying — so they don't sustain (v308, [16:15]). [REPEATED]
- Dip-buy psychology: as market sells into a dip, sellers "falling off by the wayside" each bar; once aggressive selling is gone "the market is going to revert back to where it should be" (v308, [38:18]–[38:51]). [REPEATED]
- FOMO trap into a level: after a run from 38.00 to 38.50, late longs "tripping over themselves to get long," "fomo into the market… we broke out," running into resting liquidity → they get trapped and market sells off (v310, [11:00]–[11:59]). [ONCE]
- Sequencing story: a big aggressor "doesn't care what's there in the market, he's clearing it all out" — clearing the book "with impunity" = institutional conviction (v308, [30:02]–[30:32]). [REPEATED]
- Flow-driven story: strong sell-offs near the high "reverse right back on you pretty quick" because the selling is day-traders covering longs, not new shorts; new longs keep stepping in (v311, [15:00]–[15:30]). [ONCE]
- Big-bid trigger: a 748-contract bid at one level both "supports the market" and "triggers off" heat-map watchers who pile in once they see it on the depth/heat map (v311, [10:09]–[10:46]). [ONCE]
- Exhaustion story: "if the last person sold or there's nobody interested in selling anymore, what do you think the market's going to do?" → it goes back up (v316, [09:52]–[10:30]). [REPEATED]

## E. Absorption clues
- Best-seen on a DELTA footprint (horizontal delta = bid vol vs offer vol at a price) (v310, [00:28]–[01:31]). [REPEATED]
- Look for "pockets of big volume" at a price = "strong resting liquidity at the market" (v310, [02:04]–[02:36]). [REPEATED]

- Distinguishes absorption from an ordinary up-bar: a green bar normally has liquidity only at the bottom (~3 price levels); ABSORPTION is when it's "about half of the bar" of opposing volume and it still reverses your way (v310, [07:55]–[08:20]). [ONCE]
