## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup
- **Exhaustion = "the last buyer has bought in an uptrend or the last seller has sold in a downtrend"** (v485, [00:27]). Aggressive participants "run out of ammunition" / "have no more contracts to sell" / "price is just getting too low for us to continue to sell" (v472, [02:09]; v481, [02:40]). [REPEATED]
- Once the aggressive side that was MOVING price is removed, "what's going to move the market higher? Nothing" → gravity / reversal takes over (v478, [01:32]; v488, [03:25]). [REPEATED]
- **Green Delta Trap psychology:** strong positive delta into/through resistance means buyers were *aggressive*, not *successful*; they lifted into resting liquidity that absorbed them; ask "are these aggressive buyers getting paid?" If no continuation, they are trapped (v478, [00:28]–[02:01]). [REPEATED]
- **Red candle with positive delta at a high** = early-bar aggressive buying that got reversed within the bar; buyers "made their last-ditch attempt," ran into resting liquidity, then got trapped (v488, [00:55]–[02:09]). [REPEATED]
- **"Residual / leftover selling (or buying)"** — when a good-looking exhaustion stops you out, it's just a little extra one-bar of opposite aggression that "had to be facilitated/executed" before the real reversal; re-entry valid (v481, [06:19]; v483, [08:53]; v488, [05:05]). [REPEATED]
- Small deltas (≈≤50) near a low/high = aggressive buying and selling "evenly matched," no one in control → neutral; but at a LOW with small delta he infers decent passive buying is absorbing the aggressive selling (v482, [07:49]–[09:25]). [REPEATED]

## D. Exhaustion clues
- **Tiny volume print at the bar edge at a swing extreme** — the core clue. Examples he cites: 3 contracts at the low when surrounding bars traded 1,261 / 994 / 1,530 etc. (v472, [01:30]); 1 contract on a low (v482, [02:01]); 2 contracts at a low where the bar traded 19,000 in 10-years (v488, [07:37]). [REPEATED]
- **Decreasing momentum with high volume** — new highs/lows made but the aggressive volume needed to push further diminishes; "a lot of volume trades at the top but price struggles to advance" (v462, [01:32]; v490, [02:40]). [REPEATED]
- **Weakening counter-delta sequence into the level** — e.g. selling into a low getting progressively weaker: −386, −337, −220, −63 then flip positive (v482, [05:53]); buying into a high weakening: 730, 463, 48, then −52 (v481, [04:41]). [REPEATED]
- **Volume-decline staircase into the extreme**, capped by the tiny print: "423, 398, 161, 103… capped off with just the five" (v481, [02:40]). [REPEATED]
- He notes exhaustion prints appear in ALL markets but are **easier to read in high-volume-at-price markets** (bonds, ultra bonds, tens, ultra tens) than in NASDAQ where volume is "spread out" (v462, [03:25]; v472, [04:58]). [REPEATED]

## E. Absorption clues
- **Prominent Point of Control (POC)** in the bar = sign of absorption; "bearish prominent point of control" / "bullish POC toward the bottom of the bar" (v481, [05:14]; v482, [09:25]; v487, [03:28]). [REPEATED]
- **Value Area Absorption** — value area drawn out (blue=bullish, red/pink=bearish); strongest when the value area is **NOT traded into in the next bar** (v481, [05:49]; v485, [05:04]; v490, [22:57]). [REPEATED]
- **Resting liquidity at the level** absorbing aggressive orders — large resting limit orders soak up the aggressive buying/selling at the extreme (v478, [00:59]; v488, [01:31]). [REPEATED]
- **Strong counter-delta that fails to move price** = absorption: "−455 delta… you expect the market to go lower and it doesn't" (v482, [02:31]); "−700 minus delta… we can't push lower" in ultra bonds (v489, [10:23]). [REPEATED]
- His model statement: **"You're looking for exhaustion FOLLOWING signs of absorption coming in at a level"** (v482, [10:25]). [REPEATED]

## G. Delta / delta-divergence ideas
- **Orderflows Delta Divergence** — making new highs while delta turns/weakens (e.g. new high on −145 then −373 delta; or red candle with positive delta) = "a delta order flows delta divergence" (v489, [01:47]; v488, [00:55]). [REPEATED]
- **Positive delta ≠ buyers winning** — strong positive delta with NO continuation at a high is a reversal sign (the Green Delta Trap) (v478, [00:00]–[02:40]). [REPEATED]
- **Delta should stay consistently strong in the trend direction**; if making new highs you want positive delta increasing — "if you're expecting this market to continue up, it's going to keep putting in consistently strong positive deltas" (v481, [04:41]; v489, [01:47]). [REPEATED]
- **Delta surge** — increasing negative delta (−124, −282, −917) into support that then fails to break lower = exhausting sellers / absorption (v483, [05:04]–[06:44]). [REPEATED]
- **Small delta (~≤50) = neutral / evenly matched**; can be set by a single trade on a 1-min bar (v482, [08:18]–[08:53]). [REPEATED]
- **"Negative delta surge" vs decreasing delta** is read together with the exhaustion print — you want decreasing counter-delta into the turn, or a delta flip on the catalyst bar (v483, [11:00]; v490, [04:46]). [REPEATED]

## J. Entry triggers (the exact "go" event)
- **Primary trigger:** the exhaustion (signal) bar CLOSES, then the **next bar trades beyond the signal bar's extreme in your direction** — for a short, the next bar trades BELOW the low of the exhaustion bar; for a long, the next bar trades ABOVE the high of the exhaustion bar (v490, [03:11]; v485, [01:21]; v489, [05:43]). [REPEATED — most repeated rule in chunk]
- "As soon as the bar closes, you get long. As soon as the next bar starts opening up and trading in your direction, that's your sign… You don't have to wait for any other confirmation other than the bar to close, which confirms the exhaustion print" (v472, [09:40]). [REPEATED]
- He uses a **1–2 bar window**: "this bar here, one of these two bars, I like to use two bars, has to trade lower than the low of the bar with exhaustion" (v489, [05:43]); "if it doesn't happen within one bar or two bars, definitely not a trade I'm looking to take" (v490, [07:55]). [REPEATED]
- **"Let the market take you in"** — do NOT pre-empt the exhaustion print by getting short/long before the follow-through bar; better to be stopped out than to anticipate (v489, [06:19]; v490, [08:25]). [REPEATED]
- **Second-chance / re-entry** — if stopped out but "nothing's really changed in the order flow," re-enter at the same level once you see further absorption + exhaustion (v481, [06:19]; v488, [05:59]). [REPEATED]

## K. Confirmation — what SHOULD happen quickly after entry/trigger

## L. Invalidation — what should NOT happen / what kills the thesis
- **Next bar trades INSIDE the prior bar (no follow-through)** = "a terrible sign" / "not a good sign"; if it can't take out the exhaustion bar's extreme, there's "no trade" (v485, [01:55]; v490, [07:55]; v487, [01:32]). [REPEATED]
- **Opposite delta reappears** after the exhaustion (e.g. bullish exhaustion at high but next bar has positive delta, or buying imbalance) = thesis dead (v485, [02:24]; v483, [03:57]). [REPEATED]
- **Going sideways / consolidating after entry** — if it doesn't move, it's failing; he applies a **~10-minute time stop** (see M) (v490, [05:19]; v489, [04:17]). [REPEATED]
- **Trading at/near "unchanged" or the open** = no catalyst → exhaustion prints there are not tradable; "why would you be taking a trade when nothing is happening?" (v487, [09:51]–[11:54]). [REPEATED]
- **Not a genuine swing** (too little time/space to the left, sideways market) → "I'm not really looking for an exhaustion print in a market that's going sideways" (v485, [04:47]; v487, [06:01]). [REPEATED]
- **The move into the level is already too big** (e.g. a 15-pt elongated bar; "how much more can it go… using up a lot of energy") lowers conviction (v487, [05:03]). [ONCE]
