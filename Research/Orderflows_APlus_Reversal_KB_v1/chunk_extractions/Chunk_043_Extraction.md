## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Ratio (bearish / bullish) signal** — blue number above a red candle (bearish ratio, ratio-balance high) or below a green candle (bullish ratio, ratio-balance low); threshold ≥28 (he says "30 is the number I like" but uses 28 to catch borderline cases); <0.6 (or 0.7 used interchangeably) for bullish defense. (v133, [19:06])
- **Trapped-trader signal ("trap traders")** — software arrow indicating participants are long/short into the wrong direction; strongest at swing highs/lows, weakest mid-move. (v137, [01:44], [02:08], [17:44])
- **Multiple imbalances** (pink-box feature in Orderflows Trader 2.0) — imbalances spread across a bar that are NOT strictly stacked (not literally on top of each other) but still regarded as "just as important." Distinct from stacked imbalances. (v137, [02:33], [02:56])
- **Prominent Point of Control (POC)** — highlighted magenta (resistance) or cyan (support) by software auto-analysis; used as both entry confluence and read-through zone. (v137, [01:16], [06:52])
- **Divergence + ratio + prominent POC** — the full-confluence reversal; naked divergence alone considered insufficient. (v137, [09:05], [11:16], [31:47])
- **Single print** — naked/unfilled price level; used as a confluence factor for reversals, especially at highs. (v137, [07:19], [14:13])
- **Stacked imbalance (reversal)** — same-side imbalances literally stacked; used as directional continuation signal or, when opposite color appears, a reversal sign. (v133, [20:15]; v137, [21:19])
- **Sweep** — aggressive order sweeping through levels; software flags this; used as a bearish signal when appearing at swing highs, bullish when at swing lows. (v137, [04:58], [16:00], [26:44])
- **Zero-print indicator** — flags a sweep or aggressive clearing of a side; bearish read when present above the bar at a high. (v137, [05:26])
- **Delta divergence** — price makes new high/low while delta goes opposite direction; never trade on naked divergence alone; must be paired with ratio and/or prominent POC. (v136, [01:17]; v137, [09:05])
- **Supportive buying / resistance selling** — absorption signal; cited as "support of buying" (bid support) or "resistance selling" (offer resistance) with POC alignment. (v137, [04:58], [05:17])
- **Trap-trader + multiple-imbalance combo at swing high/low** — described as a "ten out of ten" short setup after a big move up. (v137, [22:46])
- **Bullish ratio + divergence + prominent POC + multiple imbalances** — described as "market screaming at you to buy"; highest-confidence long setup. (v137, [31:47])

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Trapped traders at swing highs**: buyers who entered for a breakout, take heat, capitulate (sell), and may even reverse short — are now "short in the hole" — that covering/reversing flow drives the sell-off. (v137, [13:50])
- **Trap traders mid-move**: he explicitly says he PREFERS trap-trader signals at swing highs/lows, NOT in the middle of a move; mid-move signals are lower quality. (v133, [17:44])
- **Sideways market + stacked imbalance**: a large participant moving a big order through a thin/consolidating market; imbalance may vanish quickly with no directional follow-through. He explicitly says this context degrades the stacked-imbalance signal. (v133, [20:15], [20:46])
- **Mixed ratios (bearish then bullish quickly)**: indicates a directionless market; he will not trade it. "A bearish ratio followed by a bullish ratio is a market that's not going to give me the move I want." (v133, [21:10])
- **Bullish ratios lined up on a move**: "bullish bullish bullish" without interspersed red candles = strong momentum; mixed candle colors weaken confidence. (v133, [22:23])
- **Absorption at lows**: "supportive buying" = bids absorbing sellers; POC holds; trapped shorts must cover → rallies. (v137, [04:58], [30:18])
