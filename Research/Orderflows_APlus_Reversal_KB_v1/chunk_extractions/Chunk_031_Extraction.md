## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Ratio with Divergence** — core reversal setup; bearish = blue ratio number on a red bar at a high with negative delta (divergence arrow); bullish = blue ratio on a green bar at a low with positive delta. Mentioned across all three videos (v59, v60, v61) as the primary go-to. [REPEATED]
- **Delta Scalper signal** — indicator-generated buy/sell at highs/lows, reads delta shifts from supply-driven to demand-driven market (and reverse). Frequently used as a confluence tool, not standalone. (v60, [01:23]; v61, [03:34]) [REPEATED]
- **Stacked Imbalance** — multiple buying/selling imbalances stacked on the same side; bearish stacked imbalance followed by a bullish ratio = bearish signal confirmed / "all systems go." (v60, [21:09]; v61, [32:55]) [REPEATED]
- **Trap Traders** — price prints trap buyers at highs or sellers at lows; referenced as a confluence element alongside ratio + stacked imbalances. (v60, [08:35]; v61, [32:55]) [REPEATED]
- **Single Print / Zero Print** — lone or zero-lot area treated as a gap; plays involve watching whether price fills it and continues or reverses. (v60, [03:54]; v61, [07:07]) [REPEATED]
- **Point of Control (POC) at extreme** — bullish: POC at the very bottom of a bar; bearish: POC at the very top of a bar. Described as "very bullish/bearish" bar. (v60, [09:06]; v61, [04:31]; v61, [22:29]) [REPEATED]
- **Delta Surge signal** — separate indicator reading delta in isolation; fires earlier but does NOT factor in the broader context the Delta Scalper uses. Must be validated by candle color and context. (v60, [11:07]) [ONCE]
- **Double Top/Bottom with Diminishing Volume** — when price retests a high/low with less volume the second time, it signals the buying/selling is drying up and a reversal is likely. (v61, [16:06]) [ONCE]
- **Failed Divergence → Trend Day signal** — three or more failed divergences grouped together signal a trending market; look for breakout on next test of high/low rather than fade. (v60, [06:08]) [REPEATED]
- **Imbalance at extreme of bar** — imbalance at the bottom of a bar = buying support; imbalance at top = selling resistance. Used as a bullish/bearish bar qualifier. (v61, [04:31]) [REPEATED]

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Trap Traders narrative**: Retail buyers late to a move buy at the high; market turns and they have to sell. Or retail sellers short at a low; market rallies and they have to cover. He explicitly clarifies trap traders CAN get out — the point is they WILL get out (pain threshold), creating selling/buying flow. (v59, [12:07]) [REPEATED]
- **Passive buyer absorption at lows**: Multiple bars with strong passive buying (big numbers on bid side) holding a low; when those passive buyers become exhausted, market sells off — "where's the buyers? They're gone." The former support buyers may now become sellers hitting the bid on the way down. (v60, [15:31]) [ONCE]
- **Delta Surge on green bar = supportive buying**: Negative delta on a rising (green) bar means passive buyers are absorbing aggressive selling. "It's not telling you to short — it's telling you there's supportive buying." Market can continue higher. The key is WHAT doesn't happen (the market not going down despite sell signal) is as important as what does happen. (v60, [12:16]) [ONCE]
- **Double top exhaustion**: Second approach to a high with less volume on both bid and offer sides = buying drying up. Each price level shows less volume on both sides — demand waning, supply still present. (v61, [17:26]) [ONCE]
- **Supply-driven → demand-driven shift**: Delta Scalper's primary function — alerting to market shifting from one regime to another in real time. (v60, [01:23]) [REPEATED]
- **Failed divergence psychology**: When market defies a bearish divergence and keeps rallying, latecomers who shorted must cover, adding to bullish pressure. Three failures confirm underlying trend strength. (v60, [06:08]) [REPEATED]

## E. Absorption clues

- **Large passive buying at a level** — repeated big numbers on the bid side across multiple bars at the same price; those passive buyers are absorbing all the aggressive selling. "There's more selling going on… but there's more selling into the bid than buying from the offer." Negative delta = passive buyers absorbing. (v60, [12:42]; v60, [15:31]) [ONCE]
- **POC right at the bottom of a bar** — point of control at the very bottom of a down bar means the most volume traded right at the low, suggesting active buyers stepped in there (absorption of sellers). (v61, [04:31]) [REPEATED]
- **Imbalance at extreme of bar** — a buying imbalance right at the bottom of a bar confirms "active buying down here… big passive buyers supporting this market." (v61, [05:49]) [REPEATED]
- **When passive buying exhausts** — once the passive buyers supporting a level are "gone," the sellers who were stopped get momentum; those same passive buyers may flip to hitting bids on the way down. (v60, [16:25]) [ONCE]

## G. Delta / delta-divergence ideas

- **Ratio + Divergence = core reversal signal**: Blue ratio number on a bar + divergence arrow (negative delta on high / positive delta on low) is the primary two-factor signal. He reiterates this in every market recap. (v60, [02:18]; v61, [07:29]) [REPEATED]
- **Delta Surge vs. Delta Scalper distinction**: Delta Surge reads delta alone; Delta Scalper reads delta + other factors. Delta Surge fires on green bars going up with negative delta, but that's just passive buying — should NOT be shorted on a green bar. "You're not understanding the indicator." (v60, [11:07]) [ONCE]
- **Negative delta on a GREEN (rising) bar = supportive passive buying, NOT a short signal**: Passive buyers absorbing aggressive sellers. The market can continue up. This is a nuance that contradicts naive delta interpretation. (v60, [12:16]; v60, [14:37]) [ONCE]
- **Strong positive delta bar that closes neutral/negative = bearish divergence**: Intrabar delta goes to a high (+1541 in the bunds example) then finishes at −2; that close is a bearish signal. (v61, [11:43]) [ONCE]
- **Cumulative failed divergences = trend signal**: Three grouped failed divergences → don't fade the market, look for buying on breakout instead. "Failed divergences are a sign the market has some trending underway." (v60, [06:08]) [REPEATED]
- **Divergence "one bar removed"**: Divergence and ratio are ideally in the same bar; having the ratio one bar later is acceptable but weaker (he says "I would like it in this bar"). (v60, [20:11]) [ONCE]

## H. Failed breakout / breakdown / stop-run / liquidity-sweep ideas

- **Single print / thin spot = gap to fill**: Price areas with very thin volume (few lots at a price) are treated as "gaps" — expect price to return to fill them; watch whether it fills and reverses or fills and continues direction. (v60, [03:54]) [ONCE]
- **Failed divergence = continuation signal** — a divergence that fails (price continues through the level) is not just noise; three+ failures in a cluster = trend day, look for long breakout on next high test. (v60, [06:32]) [REPEATED]
- **Double top / bottom with volume confirmation** — not a simple price pattern; requires volume audit: less volume on second test = real reversal setup. Without volume, a double top can break higher. (v61, [16:06]) [ONCE]
- **Opening price as rotation axis on directionless days** — on quiet/sideways days market tends to rotate around the opening price; signals near the opening price are less clean than signals away from it. (v61, [06:16]; v61, [27:10]) [REPEATED]

## I. Trapped-trader ideas

- **Trap Traders definition clarified**: Traders long-and-wrong or caught-short-and-rallying who eventually capitulate. They are NOT actually stuck (can always get out); they are "choosing" when to take pain. Trap Traders "show up late to the party." (v59, [12:07]) [REPEATED]
- **Scalpers vs. retail trapped traders**: Scalpers realize they're wrong immediately and cut; retail traders hold longer before capitulating. The delta flow from capitulating retail is what shows up in the order flow. (v59, [14:18]) [ONCE]
- **Trap Traders as cluster signal**: "Trap traders" showing up in the order flow contributes to multi-signal cluster quality (alongside ratio + stacked imbalance + delta scalper + single print). (v60, [20:39]; v61, [32:55]) [REPEATED]

## R. Contradictions / nuances

1. **Negative delta on green bar ≠ short signal**: A Delta Surge firing on a green bar with negative delta is NOT a short signal — it means passive buyers are absorbing aggressive sellers. This contradicts naive "negative delta = bearish" logic. He explicitly criticizes traders who short on this signal and lose. (v60, [11:07]; v60, [12:16]) [ONCE]

2. **Trap traders "can always get out"**: Mike pushes back on the "trapped = can't move" narrative. They are not mechanically trapped — they just haven't pulled the trigger yet. This nuance means the "squeeze" effect is behavioral (pain-driven), not structural (mechanically forced). (v59, [12:07]) [REPEATED]

3. **Bullish ratio immediately after bearish stacked imbalance = bearish setup CANCELLED**: The follow-through bar's signal overrides the prior bar's signal. This is bidirectional — a bullish stacked imbalance followed by a bearish ratio in the next bar would also cancel the bullish thesis. (v60, [21:35]) [ONCE]

4. **Failed divergences = DO NOT fade, buy the breakout**: Three grouped failed divergences are a trend signal — the opposite of using divergences as reversal signals. The same divergence tool that gives reversal entries becomes a trend-continuation signal when it keeps failing. (v60, [06:08]) [REPEATED — nuance already partially in digest but now confirmed with specific framing: "grouped together on a chart"]

5. **"Divergence one bar removed" is explicitly weaker**: When ratio and divergence are in adjacent but separate bars, he says "I would like it in this bar" — not ideal, but usable. This is a grading distinction for implementation. (v60, [20:11]) [ONCE]

6. **Signal near opening price on quiet day = lower quality**: On directionless days, price rotates around the open; a ratio+divergence right at the open on a sideways day can still give a "ratio + divergence all systems go" signal that then just goes sideways. Context filter overrides signal quality. (v61, [27:10]) [ONCE]

7. **Strong intrabar positive delta that ENDS neutral/negative = bearish, not bullish**: Intrabar delta of +1541 that closes at −2 is a bearish bar signal — the buying was absorbed. Naive reading would call it bullish (positive delta). (v61, [11:43]) [ONCE]

8. **Range chart construction matters**: A 4-range chart on a volatile day (e.g., 40-point ES range day) will look different and may generate different signals than a 10-range chart. Implies chart construction is a variable that affects whether a signal qualifies, not just aesthetics. (v59, [07:56]) [ONCE]
