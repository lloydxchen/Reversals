## A. Setup types & names he uses
- **Exposed / untested value-area zone ("until tested" zone)** — bullish ("green box/zone") or bearish ("red box/zone"). The signal is a value area that the NEXT bar (and subsequent bars) does NOT trade back into; price has gapped away from it, i.e. "price is leading value." Trade in the direction the value is moving: above a green/bullish zone, below a red/bearish zone (v242, "value area until tested", [13:04]–[16:45]; [22:42]). [REPEATED]
- **Inverse imbalance** — his signature/"different way" setup. A *stacked* imbalance that is the INVERSE of the candle direction: e.g. a green up-candle containing stacked SELLING imbalances (negative delta) = bullish inverse imbalance ("blue zone"); a red down-candle containing stacked BUYING imbalances = bearish inverse imbalance ("red zone"). Caused by absorption / supportive volume (v242, "inverse imbalance", [34:26]–[39:06]). [REPEATED]
- **Stacked imbalance** — consecutive imbalances at adjacent price levels ("this 5, 14, 13 is a stacked imbalance… they make for some great trading opportunities") (v242, [36:13]). [REPEATED]
- **Exhaustion print** — in a thick/liquid market (10-yr ZN, bonds), a tiny volume print (e.g. "trades three contracts" where it normally trades 1100–3000) at a swing low/high; "great spots for reversals" (v243, "exhaustion prints", [19:53]–[21:08]). [REPEATED]
- **Selling tail / buying tail** — tail at a high (selling tail, "I see the two zero highlighted") or at a low (buying tail); combined with value migration for entry (v243, "tails", [00:38]–[01:47]). [ONCE]
- **Second-slot imbalance** — a buying imbalance appearing near the TOP of a red candle = bearish signal (drawn red) (v243, "second slot imbalance", [04:06]). [ONCE]
- **EBA (bullish/bearish EBA)** — software-drawn value-area marker referenced repeatedly during live reads (e.g. "bullish eva", "bearish eva") used like the exposed-zone signal (v243, [10:47], [33:06]). [REPEATED] — NEEDS-OPERATIONALIZATION (acronym not defined in chunk; appears to be the value-area "until tested" zone).
- **Absorption at swing high (continuation)** — supportive bids stacking as price pops to new swing highs; "when you see absorption on a move higher chances are the market's going to move higher" (v241, [16:26]–[17:32]). [ONCE]
- **Supply-at-resistance reversal (Bitcoin)** — repeated rallies into the same overhead supply level that reject ("ran into supply… reverses quite dramatically") (v241, [04:13]–[05:16]). [REPEATED within v241]

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup
- **Supply absorbing eager dip-buyers (Bitcoin, bearish):** big seller has more supply than the support-buyers can absorb; he lets bids get hit ("happy to absorb all their buying"), then WORKS offers on the other side to push through "support" so trapped support-buyers are wrong and market drops further; he wants price low to attract more buyers to sell into (v241, [07:03]–[08:22]). [REPEATED]
- **Trapped traders are NOT the mover — KEY NUANCE:** "the amount of traders that are trapped is so insignificant it's not going to cause the market to reverse… they're people that got in late to the party and are long and wrong or short in the hole." Trapped traders are really a SIGNAL of **trading activity drying up** in that direction, not a reversal cause (v242, [33:14]–[34:26]). [ONCE — strong/important]
- **Inverse imbalance psychology:** negative delta on a green candle = "supportive volume" — passive bidders absorbing aggressive sellers, so price rises despite sellers being aggressive (v242, [37:50]–[38:28]). [REPEATED]
- **Big size at a low that does NOT push lower = not new selling:** likely margin calls/blowouts/stops or trapped longs puking, "a big seller… isn't gonna sell after you just dropped about two thousand dollars… he's the one that's gonna be up near the high selling" (v241, [09:31]–[10:43]). [ONCE — important read]
- **"If it prints there, it can trade there":** a spike that quickly reverses (panic sellers cover at the spike) marks a level the market is likely to revisit (v241, [19:42]–[20:33]). [REPEATED phrase]

## D. Exhaustion clues
- **Exhaustion print:** abnormally small volume (e.g. 3 or 5 lots) at a swing low/high in a market that normally trades hundreds–thousands per level → reversal level. Tunable "exhaustion print" setting (set to 9 in ZN; lowered to 3 in soy meal because lighter volume) (v243, [19:53]–[21:08]; [27:38]–[28:55]). [REPEATED]
- **Delta drying up at a high:** deltas shrinking to "+3" / "+0.7 bitcoin" on lighter volume (81 BTC vs prior 137) → "this move is pretty much over… positive delta but it's not strong anymore… really weak to neutral." Wants to see "legitimate numbers… 23, 21, 19" to trust continuation (v241, [13:47]–[14:51]). [REPEATED]
- **Big-delta bar followed by tiny-delta bar** (e.g. +171 then +4; or +600-vol then near-zero delta) → market goes sideways / finds balance, momentum exhausted (v241, [17:32]–[18:13]; [19:23]). [REPEATED]
