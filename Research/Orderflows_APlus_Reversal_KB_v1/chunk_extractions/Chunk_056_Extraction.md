## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Multiple imbalances** (bullish or bearish): three or more buying or selling imbalances spread out (non-stacked) within a single bar, highlighted by a purple/violet box in Orderflows Trader software. Distinct from stacked imbalances. (v185, "Order Flow Techniques That Work", [28:02]; v186, "Order Flow Nuances and Techniques", [48:11]) [REPEATED]
- **Delta surge** (bearish or bullish): a steady, directional increase in delta magnitude over several bars, signaling a rapid shift from one side to the other. A free standalone indicator at orderflows.com, works on plain candlestick charts in NT8. (v185, [37:24]) [REPEATED]
- **Stacked imbalances** (bullish or bearish): three or more imbalances stacked neatly on top of each other in a bar. Standard setup; noted as less early than multiple imbalances. (v185, [27:29]; v186, [44:18]) [REPEATED]
- **Overlapping imbalance** (bullish example given): a stacked selling imbalance followed within a certain number of bars by a stacked buying imbalance — signals the low is likely to hold. Described as an "inner circle" nuance. (v186, [45:47]) [ONCE]
- **Absorption** (bullish example given): a green candle with strong negative delta — aggressive sellers are selling but passive bids absorb all supply, bar closes higher. Explicit named setup. (v186, [30:26]) [REPEATED]
- **POC clustering / value area definition**: three or more bars sharing the same Point of Control price level signals the market is "defining value." Breaking above or below that POC cluster is a directional trigger. (v186, [57:32]–[1:00:21]) [ONCE, extended treatment]
- **POC migration**: rising POC sequence on uptrend, falling POC sequence on downtrend. Three consecutive POCs moving against your position = exit signal. (v186, [1:00:49]–[1:02:46]) [ONCE]
- **Distribution at highs (institutional selling)**: market goes sideways at HOD while cumulative delta shrinks — institutions distributing. Leads to break lower. (v185, [13:03]; v186, [39:02]) [REPEATED]
- **Sideways-then-multiple-imbalance breakout**: bonds and e-minis going sideways, then multiple selling (or buying) imbalances appear at the range boundary — precede the actual stacked imbalance bars, allowing earlier entry. (v186, [51:20]–[53:24]) [ONCE]

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Institutional selling at highs**: big institutions cannot hide their trades. They sell aggressively as price rallies (easier to move size into liquidity). They sell in iceberg fashion — the full position is hidden but the traded volume shows on the footprint. Negative delta while price goes sideways at HOD is distribution. (v185, [13:34]–[14:38]; v186, [11:50]–[13:09]) [REPEATED]
- **Absorption**: aggressive sellers dump into the market, but passive buyers absorb all supply. Bar is green (price rises) despite negative delta. The "big buyer stepped in … not on my watch." (v186, [30:01]–[31:01]) [REPEATED]
- **Overlapping imbalance psychology**: sellers capitulate first (stacked sell), then buyers recognize the low will hold, come in aggressively within a short window — "realizing this low is probably going to hold for now." (v186, [45:20]–[45:47]) [ONCE]
- **Multiple imbalances — FOMO selling into lows**: after a big down move, traders panic-sell the low; this is the mirror-image of what to look for. Aggressive buying coming up off the low (multiple buying imbalances) is what you want to catch. (v186, [47:46]–[48:11]) [ONCE]
- **Stacked imbalance after trend already in motion**: could be capitulatory/FOMO late sellers, not the start of a new leg — explicitly a reason to prefer multiple imbalances, which identify the early aggressive participants. (v186, [46:52]–[47:18]) [ONCE]
- **Max/min delta bar story**: at a swing low, min delta strong → max delta takes over → bar closes near max delta → next bar has near-zero min delta → aggressive buyers dominate. Sequential reading of internal delta tells the full story of who wrestled control. (v186, [27:02]–[28:52]) [ONCE, extended treatment]

## G. Delta / delta-divergence ideas

- **Four distinct delta measures**: (1) Final delta (headline), (2) Max delta (peak aggressive buying in bar), (3) Min delta (peak aggressive selling in bar), (4) Cumulative delta (running total from open). All four used in combination. (v185, [22:57]; v186, [18:30]–[19:04]) [REPEATED]
- **Max delta = zero**: at no point were aggressive buyers in control of that bar — bearish nuance (v186, [22:28]) [ONCE]
- **Min delta = zero or near-zero** (e.g., minus 1, minus 3): aggressive selling essentially absent — bullish nuance (v186, [24:10]; [27:43]) [ONCE]
- **Closing near max delta**: final delta near max delta = aggressive buyers dominant through close of bar (v186, [23:00]–[23:36]) [ONCE]
- **Spillover effect**: strong aggressive buying or selling late in a time-based bar spills into the next bar — "you often see the spillover into the next bar" (v186, [24:45]–[25:17]; [35:44]) [REPEATED]
- **Delta divergence (cumulative)**: price makes new high, cumulative delta is lower — distribution happening. Explicit example: HOD re-test, cumulative delta 11,013 vs prior 11,216 (~200 lower on new high). (v185, [16:59]–[17:34]) [REPEATED]
- **Delta weakening into swing high**: sequence 238→123→61→negative 242 into a mini swing high — delta declining while price going sideways then making new tick high still with negative delta (v186, [20:03]–[21:28]) [ONCE, step-by-step detail]
- **Cumulative delta turning positive at lows**: "if you're at the low and the delta is just kind of around zero that's not a good sign" — strong negative cum delta going in, then flipping positive = bullish (v186, [36:55]–[37:54]) [ONCE]
- **Neutral delta band**: a final delta of "around 50 or less" treated as neutral in bar analysis. (v186, [33:26]) [ONCE] — NOTE: digest has ±50, this confirms the number explicitly.
- **Absorption nuance**: green bar with negative delta ≠ bearish — passive buyers absorbing aggressive sellers. "Just because you have a negative delta or a positive delta take it in context with what's happening in the bar." (v186, [29:24]–[31:32]) [REPEATED]
