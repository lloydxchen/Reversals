## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Flowsmasher signal (buy/sell)** — a relative order flow signal fired when volume activity within a defined region of a bar compares favorably (increases) against volume in preceding bars; not triggered by absolute volume level alone. Available in "Region 1" and "Region 2" variants. (v253, "Flowsmasher Workshop", [06:09])
- **Trade Entry Signal (Flowsmasher sub-mode)** — a confirmation-gated variant of the Flowsmasher signal; requires follow-through order flow within a defined time window after initial signal conditions are met; does NOT repaint after bar closes. Bearish/bullish per direction. (v253, [47:22])
- **Double distribution play (Aaron Korbs — volume profile)** — when the prior session's profile shows two separated distributions, the LVN (low-volume node) between them acts as a support/resistance pivot the next day; directional bias based on which distribution is held/broken. Bullish if lower distribution holds; trade targets the other distribution's POC. (v258, [14:13])
- **Afternoon range-extension play (Korbs)** — on balanced/rotational days: market extends range one direction, that extension fails, range extends the other way; the "true direction" afternoon break sets up a coast-to-coast value area trade. Requires the setup to form around midday (~12:00 CT), not too early or too late. (v258, [38:17])
- **POC migration / trend day confirmation (Korbs)** — on a potential trend day, if the developing POC shifts LOWER (bearish) after price has been climbing, that is a warning signal that the trend may be failing. The inverse for shorts. (v258, [26:55])
- **VWAP double-tap entry (Korbs)** — on trend days that gap open and drive aggressively, two tests of VWAP early in the move (slight break or near-miss, then second test) create good-risk long entries for expected trend continuation. (v258, [24:10])

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

**Flowsmasher (v253):**
- The indicator detects institutional/algo order accumulation before a move by comparing current bar's directional volume within a price region to immediately preceding bars' same region. The "massaging" of orders by execution algos (TWAP/VWAP-style) creates a subtle, gradual volume increase — not a spike — that the indicator catches. (v253, [37:04])
- Volume "eating through" resistance to keep going = healthy, directional market. Volume just "present" but not increasing relative to recent history = no trade. (v253, [04:08])

**Double distribution (v258, Korbs):**
- The LVN between two distributions represents a price zone that was rejected quickly; neither buyer nor seller wanted to transact there. When price returns to this zone, it tends to either hold (sending price back toward the prior distribution) or break (sending price to the other distribution's POC as target). (v258, [14:41])

**POC migration warning (v258, Korbs):**
- If price is driving up (trend day) but the developing POC starts shifting lower, it means traders are accepting lower prices as fair value — a sign the "trend" is illusory and supply is dominating. Not necessarily a trade signal, but a protective warning. (v258, [28:38])

## D. Exhaustion clues

## J. Entry triggers (the exact "go" event)

**Flowsmasher — two modes:**
1. **Without Trade Entry Signal (raw mode):** Signal fires as soon as conditions are met within the open bar; arrow appears but can disappear/repaint until bar closes. Enter immediately when conditions fire if comfortable with intrabar entries. (v253, [51:36])
2. **With Trade Entry Signal (confirmation mode):** Signal fires only after bar closes AND follow-through order flow is confirmed in that same bar. No repainting after close. Enter on bar close. (v253, [47:22])

**Korbs — double distribution:**
- Entry: short into the LVN when price pushes up to it from below (or long into the LVN from above). (v258, [16:19])
- Entry: long when price holds LVN from above and pulls back. (v258, [17:16])

**Korbs — afternoon range extension (balanced day):**
- Entry: in the direction of the morning's "winning" range extension; wait for a pullback after the afternoon break; enter long/short looking for coast-to-coast value area move. (v258, [40:46])

## P. NinjaTrader / indicator implementation ideas he mentions or implies

- **Flowsmasher NinjaTrader implementation:** Standard NT8 indicator; requires tick replay. Operates on bar-by-bar relative volume comparison within defined price regions. Region 1 and Region 2 can be loaded as two separate indicator instances on the same chart with different colors. (v253, [07:08])
- **Signals:** Blue triangle up = buy; red triangle down = sell. Plot markers placed at signal bar. (v253, [41:02])
- **Trade Entry Signal mode:** Implemented as a sub-feature within Flowsmasher; gates signal on both condition-met AND follow-through order flow. Zones are drawn when condition met but not yet confirmed; triangle only after confirmation. Can be toggled. (v253, [47:22])
- **Markers (automation):** Compatible with "Markers" (a third-party NT8 automation tool from "Pablo"). Setup: add two instances of Markers Copy (one buy, one sell) + one instance of Markers Force. Set Fast Signal mode; link Input Series to Flowsmasher with correct region/plot settings. Calculate on each tick. (v253, [40:30])
- **Standalone use:** Flowsmasher works on regular bar charts (no footprint required), though footprint is preferred. (v253, [52:06])
- **Signal repaint behavior:** Signals are provisional while bar is open (intrabar); locked once bar closes. The Trade Entry Signal only fires post-close. Implementation must respect this to avoid lookahead. (v253, [51:36])
- **Per-market parameter guidance (implied):**
  - Bunds: Region 1, no Flow Expansion, no Swing Filter (stable market). (v253, [22:46])
  - Ultra bonds: Region 2 preferred, no Flow Expansion on 50-tick chart. (v253, [25:47])
  - NQ / volatile: Flow Expansion on, possibly Swing Filter. (v253, [08:36])
  - Euro currency: Region 2 gives more frequent and cleaner signals than Region 1. (v253, [34:35])
