## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Order Flow Sequencing** — bullish/bearish: market must "work through" progressively larger offer/bid sizes level by level; bullish = escalating offer sizes eaten as price rises; bearish = escalating bid sizes eaten as price falls. Displayed as cyan (bullish) / magenta (bearish) labels on the chart. [ONCE] (v203, "Part 3 Everything You Wanted To Know About Orderflows", [00:00])

- **Ratio Bounds High (Price Exhaustion)** — blue-colored ratio ≥30, appearing at swing highs (bearish) or swing lows (bullish); signals exhaustion. [REPEATED] (v203, [04:12])

- **Ratio Bounds Low (Price Defense)** — blue-colored ratio ≤0.69 (range: 0.69→0), appearing at swing lows (bullish) or swing highs (bearish); signals passive defense/support. [REPEATED] (v203, [04:49])

- **Ratio + Divergence combo** — ratio (blue, bounds high or low) coinciding with delta divergence; described as "especially strong." [REPEATED] (v203, [07:35])

- **Prominent POC (Prominent Point of Control)** — POC appearing near the top (bearish, magenta) or bottom (bullish, light blue) of a bar; acts as intrabar S/R. Three levels configurable; slingshot POC overrides prominent POC in display. [REPEATED] (v203, [13:38])

- **EVA (Expanded Value Area) + Slingshot combo** — combining EVA signal with slingshot POC in same bar for added confidence. [ONCE] (v203, [21:03])

- **Multiple POCs in a bar (3-POC confluence)** — three POC levels scrunched together near top (bearish, red candle) or bottom (bullish, green candle) of a bar; indicates volume concentration acting as S/R; combined with ratio and imbalance reversal for higher confluence. [ONCE] (v203, [22:52])

- **Absorption** — passive buyers/sellers absorbing aggressive flow at extremes; described as a "u-shaped" recovery pattern rather than v-shaped; distinct from exhaustion. [ONCE] (v204, [34:58])

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Order Flow Sequencing psychology**: Market must "work" through progressively larger resting orders at each price level. Escalating offer sizes absorbed on the way up = bulls are in control (effort was required). Escalating bid sizes absorbed on the way down = bears in control. If the market cannot sustain this work, it stalls. (v203, [01:38]) [ONCE]

- **Exhaustion psychology** (recap): "Last buyer has bought" → no one left to push price higher → market must drop. "Last seller sold" → market must rise. (v204, [34:34]) [REPEATED]

- **Absorption psychology**: Passive participants (not market orders) step in at a level and absorb incoming aggression. Market does NOT reverse immediately (u-shape, not v-shape); it goes sideways and works its way back. Analogy: airplane landing gradually, not dropping straight down. (v204, [35:32]) [ONCE]

- **Cumulative delta divergence story**: Rising cumulative delta + price not going higher = supply absorbing every rally attempt ("gets whacked" with 1000-lot selling each time delta builds). Eventually absorption zone breaks and market "races" in direction of the sustained delta. (v204, [13:32]) [ONCE]

- **Prominent POC at HOD/LOD edge of bar**: The fact that the highest-volume price is at the very extreme of the bar signals that participants were most active at that extreme — defensive positioning or exhaustion depending on context. "Even stronger" when POC is at the edge of the bar at the HOD/LOD. (v203, [16:23]) [ONCE]

## G. Delta / delta-divergence ideas

- **Max delta / min delta distinction** (intrabar): max delta = highest delta reached during bar; min delta = most negative delta reached during bar; final delta = delta at bar close. (v204, [00:00]) [ONCE]
  - If final delta ≈ max delta on a bullish bar AND min delta ≈ 0 → "very strong buying" — bar never turned net negative. [ONCE] (v204, [01:03])
  - If final delta ≈ min delta on a bearish bar AND max delta = 0 → "very strong selling" — bar never turned net positive. [ONCE] (v204, [01:03])

- **Cumulative delta divergence**: Rising cumulative delta + stagnant/falling price → supply absorbing; expect eventual upside resolution when sellers exhausted. [REPEATED] (v204, [11:36])
  - Cumulative delta resets at the start of each trading day (not at midnight). [ONCE] (v204, [21:33])
  - Pre-market cumulative delta is distorted by low volume — one large bar can "wreak havoc" on cumulative delta; do NOT over-read pre-market cumulative delta. [ONCE] (v204, [12:56])

- **Neutral delta threshold on heavy-volume markets**: Delta values < ~150 considered "neutral" on high-volume bars (e.g., e-mini bars trading 6,000–9,000); contrast with ±50 threshold for normal/low-volume bars. (v204, [15:18]) [ONCE — NEEDS-OPERATIONALIZATION: threshold scales with volume, not fixed]

- **Small delta signal for direction**: Seeing very small deltas (100–160 range) after large delta bars = market going "back and forth," neither side dominant; wait for directional expansion. (v204, [15:49]) [ONCE]

- Ratio + divergence combo is "especially strong" vs either alone. (v203, [07:35]) [REPEATED]

- Delta divergence alone (no ratio) can fail; example given where divergence with no ratio = failed signal. (v203, [09:34]) [ONCE]
