## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Stacked selling/buying imbalances** — three or more imbalances neatly stacked on top of each other; acts as a S/R zone; market returns to the zone and reacts off it (bearish example: market sold off, rallied back to stacked sell imbalance zone, sold off again; bullish example: market hit zone from above, rallied ~15–20 points). (v228, [19:21] and [20:50])
- **Multiple imbalance bars** — bars with 3 or more imbalances that are NOT necessarily neatly stacked; used as an "imbalanced turning point" signal, fires earlier / fires at swing turning points; distinct from stacked imbalance. (v228, [24:31])
- **Diverging delta at swing extreme (trapped traders via delta divergence)** — red/down candle with positive delta at a swing high = absorption of aggressive buyers by supply; green/up candle with negative delta at a swing low = absorption of aggressive sellers by demand. Used to identify trapped trader condition. (v228, [30:00]–[37:29])
- **Tick-chart reversal / flowedges signal** — FlowEdges indicator applied to tick-based charts; fires a buy/sell triangle when follow-through order flow is confirmed within the next 1–2 bars (trade entry signal). Bullish/bearish. (v226, [04:53]; v227, [04:27])
- **Flow Edge confluence with Power Bars** — combining FlowEdges signal bars (blue/dark red) with Power Bar magenta triangles on the same chart for additional confirmation; they fire on different bars. (v226, [09:55])

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Positive delta at swing high (trapped buyers)**:
  - Story: market rallied → buyers get excited (FOMO) → aggressive buyers buy at the high → a larger seller / supply is absorbing all their buying → once aggressive buying exhausts ("once that aggressive buying ends") → no more buyers → market falls naturally. (v228, [31:06]–[34:16])
  - Key phrase: "they're doing it to themselves" — buyers trap themselves, not deliberately trapped by others. (v228, [31:37])
  - The sell-off after is fast and immediate: "once this market starts dropping here you get in, doesn't come all the way back up here, doesn't test the high, just immediately moves the way it's supposed to move." (v228, [36:25])

- **Negative delta at swing low (trapped sellers)**:
  - Story: aggressive sellers selling into bids at the low → bidders (demand) absorbing all their selling → once sellers are done, no more sell pressure → market rallies. (v228, [36:55])
  - Example: green candle with negative delta at the low → rallied 75 cents in 15 minutes. (v228, [37:29])

- **Stacked imbalance revisit**:
  - Story: area of aggressive selling/buying earlier in session → price returns to that area → sellers/buyers reassert → market continues in original direction off the zone. (v228, [19:21])
  - The market need not stop exactly ON the zone — "sometimes you breach the area by a few ticks." (v228, [19:51])
  - Collective self-fulfilling: "you want other people to see what you're seeing…that's going to help you get those moves." (v228, [24:04])

- **Multiple imbalance bar at turning point**:
  - Aggressive buyers/sellers stepping in right at the extreme; the concentration of imbalances in a single bar (even if not neatly stacked) signals momentum is shifting. (v228, [25:01])
  - "You're not trying to predict the bottom but once you see the aggressive buying come in you say hey maybe this bottom can hold." (v228, [25:33])

## E. Absorption clues

- **Positive delta on a down bar at swing high** = supply absorbing aggressive buying; this IS the absorption signal. Large ask volume (aggressive buyers) roughly matched by large bid volume (supply sellers). Example: 12,586 ask vs 12,432 bid = +154 delta on a red bar. (v228, [31:37])
- **Negative delta on an up bar at swing low** = demand absorbing aggressive selling; bids absorbing all selling. (v228, [36:55])
- Key distinction: "someone there absorbing all their buying" / "bidders were happy to absorb all their selling." (v228, [31:37] and [37:29])
- Absorption can persist across multiple bars: "came back up, starts coming back down, you have positive delta…over the next three bars before falling over." (v228, [32:40])

## I. Trapped-trader ideas

- **Delta-divergence = trapped trader signal** (distinct from the inverse-imbalance trapped trader setup already catalogued):
  - Red bar + positive delta at high: buyers trapped long at the high.
  - Green bar + negative delta at low: sellers trapped short at the low.
  - (v228, [29:30]–[37:29]) [REPEATED in this chunk]
- The "trapping" is not engineered by a larger player to create a stop-run; it is organic: buyers who see a 15-point rally say "market's going back up" and buy into supply. (v228, [32:08]–[32:40])
- FOMO framing: "FOMO get me in okay they got in well unfortunately that was the end of the move." (v228, [34:49])
- Signal strength increases when trapped-trader (diverging delta) is followed immediately by stacked/multiple imbalances in the same direction: trapped buyers + stacked sell imbalances = compound signal. (v228, [38:28])

## J. Entry triggers (the exact "go" event)

- **Stacked imbalance zone**: place a limit/offer at the top of the zone with a point-to-two-point stop above; "you just throw an offer in here at 33 and a half, stop maybe a point, two points, point and a half higher." (v228, [19:51])
- **Multiple imbalance bar**: buying (going long) once you see the multiple-imbalance bar form at the swing low; stop just below that low. (v228, [25:33])
- **Diverging delta at swing extreme**: enter on the diverging delta bar, or on the bar immediately after; "get in here at 46…just a quick 10 points in five minutes." (v228, [38:28]); "once this market starts dropping here you get in." (v228, [36:25])
- **FlowEdges on tick chart**: blue up-triangle = buy trigger; dark-red/down-triangle = sell trigger; only prints after follow-through order flow confirmed in next 1–2 bars; non-repainting after print. (v226, [04:53]; v227, [04:53])
- **Getting in at the swing extreme vs. breakout**: "ideally if you get out [short] after you hit a swing high it's going to give you a better entry and if you're wrong it's going to limit your loss because you're getting short near a high as opposed to getting short near a low." (v227, [14:53])

## L. Invalidation — what should NOT happen / what kills the thesis

- For diverging-delta trade: if price comes all the way back to test the swing high/low again after entry, the thesis is weakened. "Once this market starts dropping here you get in, doesn't come all the way back up here, doesn't test the high." (v228, [36:25])
- For multiple-imbalance low: "if you take out that low then this aggressive buying doesn't…the whole premise for getting long off this aggressive buying is no longer there." (v228, [25:33]) — taking out the signal bar's extreme = immediate invalidation.
- For stacked imbalance zone: the first time the market comes back may produce a modest reaction; the level remains valid until definitively broken (not just "breached by a few ticks"). (v228, [19:51])
- No follow-through within 1–2 bars on FlowEdges = signal not triggered (trade entry signal parameter handles this — no triangle printed without follow-through). (v226, [03:04])
