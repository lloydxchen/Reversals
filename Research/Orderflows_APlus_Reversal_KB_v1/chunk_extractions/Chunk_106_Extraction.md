## A. Setup types & names he uses

- **Supportive buying / resistance selling** — passive buyers absorbing aggressive sellers at a low (supportive buying) or passive sellers absorbing aggressive buyers at a high (resistance selling); distinct from classical support/resistance which is price-level based. The signal is in the order flow, not the bar chart. (v218, [06:00]–[07:40])
- **Buying tail / Selling tail ("Double O trade")** — a bar where passive sellers (on a push down) or passive buyers (on a push up) disappear entirely, leaving a zone of zero passive participation at the extreme; price quickly snaps back. Bullish tail = bearish condition; selling tail = opposite. Called "double O trade" in the Order Flow Alchemy course. (v234, [03:03]–[04:30])
- **Prominent Point of Control (POC) anywhere** — enhanced in v5.0 to allow the prominent POC signal to fire anywhere (not just at swing highs/lows). Previously required swing high/low; missed early-trend entries. (v234, [12:32]–[15:22])
- **Order flow sequencing zones** — areas where resting (stacked) liquidity in the order book was taken out; market frequently returns to retest these zones to the tick; both "hold" and "break-through" trades available. (v234, [25:46]–[30:19])
- **Inverse imbalance zones (failed breakout / second-chance)** — when a breakout attempt leaves an inverse imbalance and fails, the market often returns to that zone for a second-chance entry. Volatile markets (NQ, MNQ, gold, crude) show these more; rarely in ES/treasuries. (v234, [30:51]–[36:20])
- **Value area zones (intraday, "until tested")** — intraday bar-by-bar value areas left unvisited ("virgin value areas"); market returns to repair/test them; cascading open value areas in one direction = trend signal. (v234, [37:32]–[48:13])
- **Cascading value areas** — three or more consecutive open value areas in the same direction; sign of a trending market; look to trade with trend until repaired. (v234, [45:41]–[47:45])

## C. Order-flow story & psychology per setup

- **Supportive buying psychology**: market moving up; aggressive buyers lifting offers; BUT simultaneously passive bids absorbing all selling — "someone is putting their hand under the market." When the ratio of passive absorption dominates, price ticks higher. (v218, [06:00]–[07:07])
- **Resistance selling psychology**: market attempting to rally; aggressive buyers still active; BUT passive sellers absorbing all buying, putting a ceiling on the move. (v218, [07:07]–[07:40])
- **Buying/selling tail psychology**: on a push down, passive sellers disappear (no one interested in selling there at any size) → price snaps back because there is nothing stopping it. Trapped longs at the high / trapped shorts at the low. (v234, [03:29]–[03:59])
- **Inverse imbalance / second-chance psychology**: "70 contracts up there did NOT cause the move down — things changed in the market." The trapped traders (e.g., longs at a failed breakout high) had time to exit; what matters on the second approach is that conditions have changed — bullish order flow (slingshot POC, EVA, buying imbalances, sequencing) now supports the long as you approach that zone. (v234, [35:19]–[36:20])
- **Order flow sequencing psychology**: resting stacked liquidity taken out → that area is "scarred"; market participants remember it and react when price returns to it; often holds to the tick. (v234, [26:19]–[27:29])
- **Value area psychology**: markets move in search of value; "virgin" (untested) value areas represent an imbalance that the market will seek to repair; heavily traded intraday value areas act as magnets on retests. (v234, [38:00]–[39:43])

## J. Entry triggers (the exact "go" event)

- **Orderflows Turns — trade entry signal**: price must move at least one tick in the direction of the signal over the next two bars after the signal bar closes; without that follow-through, only the "zone" (colored bar) is shown, NOT the triangle trigger. The triangle = actual "go" signal. (v218, [46:29]–[47:37])
- **Buying/selling tail**: entry is after the tail bar closes and the market begins breaking in the direction of the tail (e.g., selling tail → market starts trading down after bar close). (v234, [07:02]–[07:24])
- **Order flow sequencing**: limit order placed inside the zone or at the zone edge on retest. (v234, [27:29]–[28:27])
- **Value area zones**: limit bid/offer placed near the upper (for longs) or lower end of the untested value area zone; do not wait for midpoint if zone is wide. (v234, [47:45]–[48:13])
- **Prominent POC anywhere**: get long/short on close of the signal bar (or "in this bar" once zone appears). (v234, [13:36]–[14:44])

## R. Contradictions / nuances

- **Trapped trader quantity is explicitly irrelevant**: "70 contracts up there did not cause this move down... it's not like the 70 contracts is causing the market." He rejects the idea that the trapped size drives the subsequent move; it's the zone that matters, not the volume sitting there. This reinforces and extends the digest nuance that "trapped quantity is SMALL." (v234, [35:19]–[35:54]) [REPEATED, now with explicit verbal rejection]

- **Passive buyers vs. aggressive buyers**: positive delta (net buying) at a level does NOT mean the market will go up; what matters is whether the passive bid is absorbing the aggressive selling. Positive delta can coexist with resistance selling if the passive offer is overwhelming the passive bid. (v218, [05:29]–[07:40]) — nuance extending the digest's "positive delta at a low can be bearish" point.

- **"Same settings every market" is explicitly false**: volume threshold, turn strength, tick aggregation, look-back filter, and volatile/normal mode all require per-instrument tuning. He says "you've got to tweak it to your market." (v218, [1:06:23]–[1:07:27]) — mild tension with the digest statement that "same settings every market applies to detector only"; confirms per-market calibration is real.

- **Orderflows Turns works on candlestick charts** (not just footprint): intentionally designed for traders who don't use footprint, as an abstraction layer over the footprint data. (v218, [01:15]–[02:16])

- **Range-based charts explicitly discouraged for order flow** (relative to time-based): "by the time your order hits the market, especially if you're trading Nasdaq on bars that have 20-point ranges, you can find yourself getting in very late." Time-based (30s–2min) preferred. (v218, [1:00:57]–[1:01:27]) — mild contradiction with earlier video recommendations of range charts.

- **Value area "until tested" zones do NOT fire at tick-perfect limit entry in all cases**: "sometimes you'll get filled, sometimes not" — zones are areas, not lines; entry is probabilistic, not guaranteed at midpoint. (v234, [47:45]–[48:13])

- **Exhaustion zone strategy is market-dependent**: in high-volume markets (10yr treasuries), look for retests and reactions; in other markets, look for repair (market trades through and keeps going). (v234, [19:28]–[20:00]) — the action to take at an exhaustion zone depends on the instrument's liquidity profile.
