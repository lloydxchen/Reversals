# Failed Breakouts, Stop-Runs & Liquidity Sweeps

Supports KPI 1/2. **Important nuance: he is skeptical of the popular "stop-run/liquidity-sweep" narrative.**

## Failed breakout / "when the market defies you"
- A signal that **fails becomes a signal the other way** — "go with what is happening." A stacked **sell** imbalance the market ignores (no follow-through) → go **long**. (Ch100/v415 [10:01]) `[REPEATED]`
- **Failed breakout above resistance/HOD** with strong positive delta that gets **absorbed** by resting liquidity = high-probability reversal (= Green Delta Trap mechanics). (Ch016/v478, v488) `[REPEATED]`
- **Poke by one tick then reverse:** "we take out this low by one tick… seven [tiny print]… traders aren't interested." (Ch016/v489 [10:23]) `[REPEATED]`

## Holding-vs-failing asymmetry (key)
The same big fresh bid resolves two ways: **HOLD = reversal up; traded-through-fast = continuation down.** You must watch which happens — don't assume. (Ch013/v428 [01:53]) `[REPEATED]`

## Level inversion (invalidation → flip)
Building volume **above a prior POC** flips it from resistance to support → prior short thesis dead; flip bias. (Ch018/v1 [11:26]) `[REPEATED]`

## His skepticism about stop-runs (do not over-weight this narrative)
- He **distinguishes two look-alikes:** a **stop-sweep** (those traders are already flat — *not* trapped) vs **limit-absorption** (real longs who must puke). They look identical on price but read differently on flow. (Ch007/v280) `[REPEATED]`
- He **rejects "big wick = trapped/stop-run"** as "BS" — there is ample exit liquidity; most wicks are normal facilitation or a triggered stop that snaps back. (Ch013/v425 [09:32]; Ch011/v390) `[REPEATED]`
- Implication: do **not** build a setup primarily on "stops were run." Build it on exhaustion/absorption + no follow-through.

## Operationalization
- Failed-signal-flip: detect a triggered reversal pattern that **fails its own follow-through within the window**, then trade the opposite side on a fresh break. (file 02 §2.x, hypothesis H-fail)
- Sweep-with-no-stacked-imbalance is, per him, an under-watched edge ("practically no one looks for sweeps") — candidate feature, low evidence. (Ch061/v196 [44:10]) `[ONCE]`
- POC/level inversion: monitor volume building on the far side of a prior POC.

## A+ vs mediocre
A+ = failed push at a clean swing with absorption + immediate reverse. Mediocre/avoid = assuming a wick "ran stops" without flow confirmation (his explicit warning).
