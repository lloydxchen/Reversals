# Order Flow Principles

His foundational beliefs about *why* reversals happen — the assumptions every setup rests on. Supports KPI 1/2. Citations `(ChNNN/v#, [mm:ss])`.

## Core principles
1. **Markets seek a fair price; reversals happen at unfair prices.** "Markets move because they're looking for a fair price… once it moves to an *unfair* price, that's where you get rejection." (Ch001/v3 [02:56]) `[REPEATED]`
2. **A reversal = exhaustion of one side's aggression** — "the last buyer has bought / the last seller has sold." Once the side moving price runs out of ammunition and goes *silent*, "what's going to move it further? Nothing" → it reverts. (Ch016/v485 [00:27]; Ch017/v492 [16:38]) `[REPEATED — the master principle]`
3. **Aggressive ≠ winning.** Hitting the offer hard does not mean buyers are right; if they're absorbed and don't continue, they're trapped (Green Delta Trap). "Are these aggressive buyers getting paid?" (Ch016/v478) `[REPEATED]`
4. **Read aggression (delta/imbalance) AND passivity (absorption/resting size) together.** Price moves by aggression; price *stops* because of passive liquidity absorbing it. Reversals live at the hand-off. (Ch089/v310; Ch007/v269) `[REPEATED]`
5. **The footprint shows what price/candles cannot** — "you cannot see exhaustion on a candlestick chart." Bid×ask per price is the irreducible data. (Ch017/v492 [17:10]) `[REPEATED]`
6. **Read intrabar Max/Min delta, not just the headline delta.** A bar that ran +5,278 then closed −578 reveals buyers overwhelmed. (Ch028/v48 [08:30]; Ch094/v352) `[REPEATED]`
7. **React, don't predict.** "I'm not trying to pick the low — I'm trying to pick an *area* where a low can occur," then let the market confirm. "Not a red-light/green-light system." (Ch001/v3 [09:36]; Ch004/v158) `[REPEATED]`
8. **Confluence over single signals.** "Single signals lie. Combinations tell the truth." But confluence is a *screen*, not arithmetic ("6 plots ≠ buy"). (Ch102/v471; Ch085/v284 [26:13]) `[REPEATED]`
9. **Context (location + regime) governs everything.** The same footprint means opposite things mid-trend vs at a swing extreme; trending markets (price leads value) vs rotational (value contains price). (Ch078/v242 [07:33]) `[REPEATED]`
10. **The DOM lies; the footprint is truth.** Resting book size is "a magnet, not resistance"; trade executed volume, not displayed intentions. (Ch100/v411 [08:47]) `[REPEATED]`
11. **Thresholds are market-specific and drift over time** — refuse universal constants; "find your sweet spot." (Ch016/v481; Ch011) `[REPEATED]`
12. **Order flow doesn't catch every turn** — quiet = normal facilitation; pivot markets rather than force trades. (Ch016/v481 [04:08])

## Mental model (one diagram, in words)
Move → push to extreme on aggression → aggression meets passive wall (absorption) and/or runs out of fuel (exhaustion) at a *location* (swing/HOD/LOD/POC/VA) → delta weakens/flips → **the market trades back through the signal bar (confirmation)** → trapped late aggressors exit → reversal. Fast = A+; sideways = failing.
